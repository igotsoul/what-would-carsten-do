# Definition of Ready with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Definition of Ready (DoR) as a working agreement that defines when a backlog item is ready to be pulled into a sprint. He contrasts it with the well-known Definition of Done and shares concrete criteria teams can include in their own DoR to improve sprint commitment quality.

## Methods & Techniques

### Definition of Ready (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A team working agreement that specifies what conditions must be true for a backlog item to be eligible for sprint commitment.
- **When to use:** During backlog refinement and sprint planning, especially in teams that frequently pull in unfinished or unclear items.
- **Group size:** Whole Scrum Team (PO, Dev Team, Scrum Master)
- **Duration:** Ongoing artifact; typically created in a team working session
- **How it works:**
  1. The team aligns on what "ready" means for their context.
  2. Criteria are written down and made visible (e.g., on a wall or shared board).
  3. Before pulling an item into a sprint, the team checks it against the DoR.
  4. Items that don't meet the criteria stay in the backlog for further refinement.
- **Example criteria Carsten suggests:**
  - External dependencies are mapped out with a contingency/handling plan
  - The appropriate level of testing has been agreed upon (unit test, UAT, etc.)
  - Acceptance Criteria are written and agreed upon by PO, business, and dev team
  - PO has signed off, including with a Steering Committee if required

## Key Principles
- The Definition of Ready is a complement to the Definition of Done — DoD defines "finished," DoR defines "ready to start."
- Making the DoR visible (on a wall or shared space) helps teams self-regulate what they pull into a sprint.
- Acceptance Criteria serve as pre-agreed requirements: if they're met, the story is done — no ambiguity after the sprint starts.
- In organizations with external dependencies, the DoR is especially valuable for surfacing blockers before commitment, not during execution.
- The INVEST criteria (Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable) are a complementary tool for backlog quality.

## Facilitation Tips
- Keep the DoR visible at all times — physical or digital, it should be part of every refinement and planning session.
- Treat the DoR as a living agreement: revisit it in retrospectives when items repeatedly fail to finish within a sprint.
- Don't over-engineer it — Carsten's examples are practical and minimal; a short checklist is more likely to be used than a long one.

## Best Used When
- Teams struggle with stories that can't be completed within a sprint due to unclear requirements or unresolved dependencies.
- A new Scrum Team is establishing working agreements and wants to improve sprint predictability.
- Backlog refinement sessions lack a clear quality bar for what's "good enough to plan."

## Related Concepts
- **Definition of Done** — the counterpart artifact defining completion criteria
- **INVEST Criteria** — a framework for evaluating backlog item quality (Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable)
- **Acceptance Criteria** — story-level requirements agreed upon before sprint start
- **Working Agreements** — the broader category of team norms this artifact belongs to
