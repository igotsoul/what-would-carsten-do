# "Suggestions for Success" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a lightweight ground-rule technique called "Suggestions for Success," introduced to him by a co-facilitator named Sophia. Used at the start of a workshop to set the tone and establish shared agreements, it closes with a deliberate callback at the end — making the rules feel lived, not just listed.

## Methods & Techniques

### Suggestions for Success (ab 00:25)
- **What:** A co-created set of workshop ground rules framed as positive "suggestions" rather than rules, introduced at the opening to establish shared agreements and psychological safety.
- **When to use:** At the start of any workshop, especially when tackling difficult or sensitive topics where focus and openness matter.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** 5–10 minutes (opening activity)
- **How it works:**
  1. Present the suggestions at the start of the session — examples used: *Lean in*, *Ask to understand*, *Assume best intentions*, *Don't use devices* (step out if needed rather than multitasking).
  2. Invite participants to add anything missing ("Anything we've forgotten?") — this creates co-ownership.
  3. Allow the group to contribute their own additions (in this case, participants added a *Yes, we can* attitude).
  4. At the close of the workshop, circle back to the suggestions — acknowledge how the group lived by them and name specific examples you observed.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zXQEjUCfkcg&t=25

### Closing Callback to Ground Rules (ab 02:05)
- **What:** At the end of the workshop, explicitly revisiting the opening suggestions and thanking participants for following them — with concrete examples of what was observed.
- **When to use:** As a closing ritual whenever ground rules or suggestions are set at the start.
- **How it works:** Name specific behaviors you saw during the session that matched the suggestions. Express genuine appreciation. This reinforces that the agreements were real, not performative.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zXQEjUCfkcg&t=125

## Key Principles
- Framing ground rules as "suggestions" rather than rules reduces resistance and invites participation.
- Inviting participants to add their own suggestions creates co-ownership and buy-in.
- The technique only lands fully when you close the loop — circling back at the end shows that the agreements were taken seriously.
- Device-free workshops protect group flow; asking people to step out rather than multitask is a concrete, enforceable norm.
- Co-facilitation is a rich source of learning — Carsten credits Sophia entirely for this technique, modeling attribution and openness to peer learning.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use the Vegas Rule alongside Suggestions for Success to address confidentiality (Carsten links to a separate video on this).
- Don't just list the suggestions — watch for them in action during the session so you can name real examples in the closing callback.
- Keep the list short and positively framed (lean in, ask to understand) rather than a list of prohibitions.
- The invitation to add ("Anything we've forgotten?") is as important as the suggestions themselves — it signals that this is a shared space.

## Best Used When
- Opening workshops on difficult, sensitive, or high-stakes topics where psychological safety and focus are critical.
- Working with groups that tend to multitask or disengage.
- Co-facilitating — the technique naturally surfaces in peer learning moments.
