# "People vs Product" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen shares his perspective on why putting people over product is foundational to building high-performing teams. He explores how psychological safety, vulnerability, and sustainable pace create the conditions for genuine teamwork — and offers practical tips for facilitators and coaches to cultivate this in their own teams.

## Methods & Techniques

### Team Check-In Meeting (ab 05:30)
- **What:** A regular team meeting — separate from retrospectives — where members can voluntarily share how they are doing personally and professionally.
- **When to use:** Ongoing, as a recurring ritual to build trust and surface struggles early within the team.
- **Group size:** Small, close-knit team (implied: single Scrum or delivery team)
- **Duration:** Not specified; intended as a lightweight recurring session
- **How it works:**
  1. Create a dedicated space — not a standup — where the agenda is "how are we doing as people?"
  2. Make participation voluntary: no pressure to share personal details, but make it genuinely safe to do so.
  3. Apply the Vegas Rule: what is shared in the meeting stays in the meeting.
  4. Let the team decide collectively how to respond (e.g., redistributing work for a struggling colleague).
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTstdGSvaLM&t=330

### Retrospective as a Safe Space for Inconvenient Truths (ab 02:30)
- **What:** Using the retrospective specifically to surface uncomfortable topics — the things that "hurt a bit" — enabled by pre-existing psychological safety.
- **When to use:** When a team has enough trust to go beyond surface-level retrospective content and address real friction.
- **Group size:** Team-level
- **Duration:** Standard retrospective timebox
- **How it works:**
  1. Build psychological safety before expecting honest retrospective content.
  2. Normalise addressing difficult topics by modelling vulnerability as a facilitator.
  3. Frame it as addressing the "Inconvenient Truth" — Carsten's term for the real issues behind the polite ones.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTstdGSvaLM&t=150

### Low-Stakes Social Activities (ab 06:30)
- **What:** Small, informal social moments within the team to deepen personal connection without requiring large events or forced fun.
- **When to use:** Regularly, as a low-effort investment in team cohesion — especially for new or distributed teams.
- **Group size:** Own team (not cross-team)
- **Duration:** Short; can be embedded in existing meetings
- **How it works:**
  1. Keep it small and silly — a quiz, a round of "Would You Rather", a quick game.
  2. Focus on your own team first; cross-team relationships matter, but intra-team connection is where trust is built.
  3. Consistency matters more than scale.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hTstdGSvaLM&t=390

## Key Principles
- People over product is non-negotiable for Carsten: the team's wellbeing always comes before delivery output.
- Psychological safety does not emerge automatically — it requires deliberate, sustained effort, often more than teams expect.
- Highly motivated, passionate people carry a higher burnout risk; psychological safety must be paired with attention to sustainable pace.
- The facilitator or coach must role-model vulnerability first — sharing their own struggles before expecting others to open up.
- The answers to a team's challenges already exist within the team; the coach's job is to ask better questions, not to provide solutions.
- The "we're like family" sentiment is context-dependent: for some it signals belonging and safety; for others it signals unhealthy expectations. The underlying value — closeness and trust — matters more than the label.

## Facilitation Tips
- Apply the Vegas Rule explicitly in any meeting where personal sharing is invited: name it, don't assume it.
- Invest in formal coaching skills (not necessarily certification) to improve the quality of questions you ask teams.
- Role-model vulnerability as a facilitator — share what is difficult for you before asking others to do the same.
- Do not push or force personal sharing; create the container and let people choose to step in.
- Focus social investment on the immediate team rather than spreading it thinly across the organisation.

## Best Used When
- A team is stuck in surface-level retrospectives and avoiding real issues.
- A facilitator or Scrum Master wants to build psychological safety intentionally rather than hoping it emerges.
- A high-performing but stressed team needs a conversation about sustainable pace.
- A new team needs low-effort rituals to accelerate trust and connection.
