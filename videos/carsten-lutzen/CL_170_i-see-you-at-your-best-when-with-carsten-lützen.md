# "I see you at your best when..." with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces a simple, strengths-based feedback question — "I see you at your best when..." — as an alternative to deficit-focused feedback approaches. The technique helps people discover how others perceive their strengths so they can deliberately build on what's already working. Carsten also explains how to run this asynchronously with a team using a shared whiteboard.

## Methods & Techniques

### "I see you at your best when..." (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A single prompt used to deliver positive, concrete, strengths-based feedback. The giver completes the sentence with a specific observation of when the recipient was doing their best work.
- **When to use:** One-on-one feedback, team retrospectives, asynchronous team exercises, coaching conversations, or any context where you want to surface someone's strengths rather than focus on gaps.
- **Group size:** Any size — works individually or across a whole team
- **Duration:** A few minutes per person for the prompt itself; 1–2 weeks if run as an async whiteboard exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Frame the prompt: "I see you [name] at your best when..."
  2. Complete it with a specific, concrete observation (e.g., "...you dive into a challenge and go over every detail to make sure it all checks out")
  3. The receiver learns what others notice as their standout contributions
  4. They use that insight to intentionally do more of what works
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gGeS3sq6g2k&t=30

### Asynchronous Team Feedback Board (ab 01:45)
- **What:** A shared digital or physical whiteboard where team members write "I see you at your best when..." statements for each colleague, collected over one to two weeks.
- **When to use:** When synchronous feedback sessions aren't feasible, or when you want to give people time to reflect before writing. Also useful to generate a lasting artifact of team strengths.
- **Group size:** Small to medium teams
- **Duration:** Run the board for 1–2 weeks, then hold a follow-up session
- **How it works:**
  1. Set up a whiteboard with one column or section per team member
  2. Ask everyone to write at least one "I see you [name] at your best when..." statement for each colleague
  3. Let the board run for 1–2 weeks
  4. Facilitate a follow-up to help people process what they received and identify next steps
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gGeS3sq6g2k&t=105

## Key Principles
- People grow faster by building on strengths than by fixing weaknesses — deficit feedback is less likely to drive improvement than strengths-based feedback
- Making feedback concrete and situation-specific increases its value; the prompt naturally forces specificity
- Solution-focused feedback shifts attention to "what works" and "how to do more of it"
- Receiving strengths-based feedback helps people understand their own impact and how they are perceived — awareness that enables intentional behavior change

## Facilitation Tips
- Let the async board run long enough (1–2 weeks) for thoughtful contributions rather than reactive ones
- Always follow up after the async exercise — don't leave people with raw feedback and no space to process it
- Help receivers translate feedback into concrete next steps: "How can I do more of this?"
- The prompt works across cultures and seniority levels because it is inherently positive and non-threatening

## Best Used When
- A team needs a low-risk way to practice giving and receiving feedback
- You want to build psychological safety around feedback before introducing more challenging formats
- Team members are unaware of their own strengths or impact on others
- You need an asynchronous option that still generates meaningful personal insight
