# "Best Hopes for Our Family - Part 3"

## Summary
Carsten Lützen shares how he applies agile goal-setting practices at home with his family. Using sticky notes, silent individual reflection, and a shared visual board, his family sets yearly goals together — a practice he has run for three years. The video is a brief, practical illustration of how workplace facilitation techniques can transfer meaningfully into personal and family life.

## Methods & Techniques

### Family Goal-Setting Workshop (ab 00:45)
- **What:** A structured annual goal-setting session where each family member independently writes their hopes for the year, then shares and clusters them on a visible board.
- **When to use:** At the start of a new year, or any time a family wants to align on shared priorities and individual aspirations.
- **Group size:** Family unit (small group, 2–6 people implied)
- **Duration:** Not specified; likely 30–60 minutes based on the process described
- **How it works:**
  1. Each person gets their own color of sticky notes
  2. Individuals silently write down what they want to achieve or experience in the coming year
  3. Going around the table, each person presents four sticky notes at a time
  4. As stickies are shared, the group clusters them in real time (affinity mapping)
  5. All wishes are placed on a visible board (Carsten uses a window next to the dining table)
  6. Completed items are moved to a "done" section as the year progresses
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gYXGGEWIFSs&t=45s

### Persistent Visual Board (ab 01:45)
- **What:** Keeping the goal board in a shared, always-visible household space so it remains top of mind throughout the year.
- **When to use:** Any ongoing goal-tracking context where out-of-sight means out-of-mind.
- **How it works:** Post the clustered sticky notes on a window or wall adjacent to a high-traffic family space (e.g., dining table). Move completed items to a separate "done" area as they are achieved.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gYXGGEWIFSs&t=105s

## Key Principles
- Workplace facilitation tools — OKRs, sticky notes, clustering, visual boards — can translate directly into personal and family contexts with little adaptation
- Silent individual writing before group sharing ensures each person's voice is captured before social influence kicks in
- Visibility drives accountability: placing goals where they are seen daily (and by guests) creates natural opportunities for reflection and conversation
- Completion is not the only measure of success — the practice helps the family maintain focus on what matters, even if not everything gets done

## Facilitation Tips
- Give each participant a distinct sticky note color so contributions remain attributable during clustering
- Present in rounds of four stickies at a time to keep the pace manageable and allow real-time clustering
- Don't worry about completing every goal — Carsten notes his family has never finished all of them, and that's fine; the value is in sustained focus

## Best Used When
- A family or small household group wants a lightweight, recurring ritual for shared intention-setting
- A facilitator or coach wants a concrete, relatable example to illustrate how agile practices apply outside the workplace
- Introducing goal-setting formats to non-corporate audiences who may find OKR/KPI language off-putting
