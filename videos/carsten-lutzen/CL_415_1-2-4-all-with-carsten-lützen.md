# 1-2-4-All with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces 1-2-4-All, one of the simplest Liberating Structures, as a go-to method for inclusive idea generation and reflection. The format ensures every voice is heard by progressively scaling from individual silent reflection to full-group discussion — preventing extroverts from dominating while giving introverts space to contribute.

## Methods & Techniques

### 1-2-4-All (ab 00:40)
- **What:** A structured conversation format that escalates from individual reflection to pairs, then groups of four, then the full group — ensuring broad participation before converging on shared insights.
- **When to use:** Any situation requiring idea generation or reflection where you want all participants to contribute equally — retrospectives, process improvement discussions, departmental prioritization, or any open question to a group.
- **Group size:** Works for any size group; pairs and groups of four are fixed, plenum discussion scales to all.
- **Duration:** ~12 minutes total (1 min individual + 2 min pairs + 4 min groups of four + 5 min full group)
- **How it works:**
  1. Pose a clear question to the group (e.g., "What should we improve in our process?")
  2. **1 min** — Silent individual reflection (optionally note down ideas)
  3. **2 min** — Pair up and discuss what each person reflected on
  4. **4 min** — Form groups of four and share what the pairs came up with
  5. **5 min** — Full group (plenum) shares reflections and key insights
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v3Vdx8QOaOA&t=60

## Key Principles
- The intermediate steps (pairs → fours) are the core value — they give ideas time to form before the high-stakes full-group discussion
- Inclusion by design: the structure prevents extroverts from dominating and gives introverts protected processing time
- Part of Liberating Structures — a "vocabulary" of formats designed to change how groups interact, introduced to Carsten by Barry and Christiaan from The Liberators
- Simplicity is a feature: 1-2-4-All is one of the easiest Liberating Structures to implement and can be applied in almost any context

## Facilitation Tips
- The question you ask is everything — make it open, specific enough to be actionable, and relevant to the group's actual situation
- Silent individual reflection first is non-negotiable; skipping it collapses the format back to a standard group discussion
- Carsten warns against overuse: the format is versatile, but apply it with intention rather than by default

## Best Used When
- Running retrospectives and wanting equal contribution during idea generation
- Working with mixed groups of introverts and extroverts
- Any facilitated session where a few loud voices tend to dominate
- You need a quick, reliable format that requires no materials and minimal setup
