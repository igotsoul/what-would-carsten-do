# "Wicked Questions" by Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Liberating Structure "Wicked Questions" — a facilitation format for surfacing paradoxes and productive contradictions within a group. The core template is: *"How is it that we are [X] and [Y] simultaneously?"* The method helps groups articulate tensions they must navigate together, producing high-quality questions before attempting answers.

## Methods & Techniques

### Wicked Questions (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A Liberating Structure that uses a structured template to help groups surface and articulate paradoxical challenges — tensions that are both true at the same time.
- **When to use:** When a group needs to surface underlying contradictions before problem-solving; useful for introducing complex systemic topics or framing a challenge space.
- **Group size:** Split into groups of 4–6 people, then reconvene in plenary.
- **Duration:** ~20–25 minutes just to generate the questions (answer phase comes later).
- **How it works:**
  1. Introduce the template: *"How is it that we are [A] and [B] simultaneously?"*
  2. Split into groups of 4–6 and generate opposites — what contradictions are in play in the current situation?
  3. Each group shares their questions in plenary.
  4. The full group identifies the most valuable opposites and constructs the final wicked questions.
  5. Questions are refined before any attempt to answer them.
- **Examples given by Carsten:**
  - *"How is it that we value team autonomy and at the same time run towards a common goal?"*
  - *"How is it that I am committed to my job and keeping a good work-life balance?"*
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uzRc-VT4MOc&t=20

## Key Principles
- The official Liberating Structures description ("articulate the paradoxical challenges that we must face together") can feel abstract — Carsten reframes it with a concrete template to make it immediately usable.
- The power lies in spending time *generating good questions* rather than rushing to answers; 20–25 minutes on question creation produces better downstream thinking.
- What contradictions emerge is unpredictable — the surprises are part of the value.
- Wicked Questions work with tensions that are simultaneously true, not problems with a single right answer.

## Facilitation Tips
- Front-load the template clearly before groups start — the format `"How is it that we are X and Y simultaneously?"` is the anchor.
- Let the plenary vote or sense-make on which opposites feel most valuable before constructing the final questions.
- Resist moving to answers too quickly; the quality of the question phase determines the quality of what follows.

## Best Used When
- A group is stuck in either/or thinking and needs to hold two truths at once.
- Preparing for a deeper dialogue or strategic conversation where naming the tensions first helps.
- Teams dealing with complex systemic challenges (autonomy vs. alignment, speed vs. quality, etc.).
