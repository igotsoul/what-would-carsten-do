# "Pancakes" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a simple, playful remote energizer called "Pancakes" — where participants literally go to their kitchen and make a pancake during a session break. Originally introduced to Carsten by a facilitator named Jack, this energizer works exclusively in remote/home settings and delivers a surprisingly effective energy boost combined with a moment of fun and self-accomplishment.

## Methods & Techniques

### Pancakes Energizer (ab 00:20)
- **What:** Participants are tasked to leave the session briefly, go to their kitchen, and make one or two pancakes — then return and share their creation with the group.
- **When to use:** Remote or hybrid sessions where everyone is at home; works well before a brief intro or context-setting segment so the facilitator can still be productive while participants cook.
- **Group size:** Any size, as long as everyone is participating from home.
- **Duration:** 10–15 minutes.
- **How it works:**
  1. Announce the energizer — expect skepticism ("Are we seriously making pancakes?").
  2. Send everyone to their kitchen to make one or two pancakes (basic ingredients: egg, flour, milk; extras optional).
  3. Use the 10–15 minutes to deliver a brief, a context-setting segment, or simply let participants enjoy the break.
  4. When participants return, invite them to show their pancake creation on camera.
  5. Continue the session — participants are now fed, smiling, and re-energized.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8DPQaggreDE

## Key Principles
- Remote workshops lack the natural snack/catering perks of in-person sessions — this energizer compensates by giving participants something tangible and delightful.
- The initial skepticism is part of the fun; leaning into it and committing to the bit generates energy.
- A small act of self-accomplishment (making food) triggers genuine positive emotion and renewed focus.
- Timing the energizer alongside a facilitator brief makes it a dual-purpose activity — no dead time.

## Facilitation Tips
- Commit fully and don't apologize for the unusual format — Carsten emphasizes "yes, go into the kitchen."
- Invite participants to show their creation on camera; the visual sharing moment amplifies the fun.
- If someone doesn't have ingredients or doesn't want to cook, suggest bringing any snack — the social sharing moment is what matters.
- Works especially well when timed with international pancake day for extra thematic resonance, but is effective any day.

## Best Used When
- Running a remote workshop and energy is low before a key segment.
- You want to replace the "comfort of in-person snacks" with something participatory.
- The session has a natural 10–15 minute window (e.g., a brief or context-setting portion) that can run in parallel.
