# "Difference" With Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces a single, deceptively simple coaching question — "What difference would that make?" — as a powerful tool for retrospectives and 1-on-1 coaching. By asking people to articulate the concrete difference their goal would create, coaches help them shift perspective and make their aspirations more tangible and real.

## Methods & Techniques

### The "What Difference Would That Make?" Question (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A solution-focused coaching question that prompts the coachee to articulate the concrete impact of achieving their goal, rather than jumping straight to action steps.
- **When to use:** Retrospectives, 1-on-1 coaching, whenever someone is working toward a goal or trying to resolve a conflict.
- **Group size:** Individual or team
- **Duration:** Can be used as a single question within any coaching conversation; no fixed duration
- **How it works:**
  1. Identify the goal or desired change (e.g., "Suppose you get that promotion" or "Suppose we ship the product" or "Suppose that conflict is resolved")
  2. Ask: **"What difference would that make?"**
  3. Let the person answer from their own perspective first
  4. Then shift perspective: "What difference would that make for the rest of the team? For your family?"
  5. If the answer seems obvious to them, ask them to say it out loud anyway — articulating it makes it more real
  6. Use the emerging picture of "the difference" to then explore what actions could actually create those differences

## Key Principles
- The question targets the *impact* of a goal, not the *path* to it — this is what makes it generative
- Asking people to state the obvious out loud is not redundant; verbalizing makes things concrete and real
- Perspective-shifting (self → team → family) naturally widens the coachee's view without the coach having to push
- The "difference" question is a tool for clarifying *what we're actually after* before deciding what to do

## Facilitation Tips
- Use the "suppose..." framing to enter hypothetical space cleanly: *"Suppose this happened — what difference would that make?"*
- Don't skip the obvious answers; invite the coachee to say them explicitly — it builds ownership
- Layer the perspectives systematically: you → team → others beyond work
- After mapping the differences, transition naturally: *"So what could we do to make these differences actually happen?"*

## Best Used When
- A coachee is stuck on *how* to achieve something and needs to reconnect with *why* it matters
- A team in a retrospective is unclear what success would actually look like
- Someone is working through a conflict and needs help seeing the bigger picture
- You want to generate intrinsic motivation rather than task-list compliance
