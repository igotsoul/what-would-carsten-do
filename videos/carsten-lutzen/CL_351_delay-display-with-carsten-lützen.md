# "Delay Display" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the "Delay Display" retrospective exercise from Retromat — a short, metaphor-based stage-setter that uses a train station display board as a prompt to surface how the team feels about their current direction, blockers, and key messages. Despite its simplicity, it regularly sparks deeper, longer team discussions. It works both in-person and online.

## Methods & Techniques

### Delay Display (ab 00:40)
- **What:** A retrospective opener where the team or product is imagined as a train. Each team member individually writes three sticky notes answering: Where is the train headed? Is there a delay? Any special announcements?
- **When to use:** As a stage-setter at the start of a retrospective, especially to gauge team mood, surface hidden concerns, or check alignment on direction and blockers.
- **Group size:** Any team size (works individually, then shared with the group)
- **Duration:** ~15–30 min (writing phase + discussion)
- **How it works:**
  1. Set up three columns: **Destination**, **Delay**, **Announcement**
  2. Frame the exercise: the team (or product) is a train at a station
  3. Each person writes one sticky per column — silently and individually:
     - *Destination:* Where is this train going? (can be fictional or real, e.g. "Demo Town", "Nowhere")
     - *Delay:* Are we delayed? By how much and why? (e.g. "2 hours because build service is down")
     - *Announcement:* Any special message? (e.g. "Last call before departure", "Train may be redirected")
  4. Everyone shares their stickies without judgment — each reflects their personal version of reality
  5. Facilitate a group discussion: What commonalities emerge? What stands out? What needs to be addressed?

## Key Principles
- Withhold judgment when participants share their stickies — every answer is a valid perception of reality worth exploring
- The metaphor creates just enough distance to allow people to express things they might not say directly (e.g. "going nowhere")
- The exercise is most valuable not for the sticky notes themselves but for the discussion they trigger
- For Scrum Masters and Product Owners: use the output to check whether the product vision has been communicated clearly, identify delays to escalate to stakeholders, and find things to resolve or address

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten emphasizes: do not judge what people have written during the share-out — it is their perception of reality
- If someone writes a bleak answer like "Nowhere", treat it as a valid signal, not a problem to correct in the moment
- The Product Owner should ideally be present — their presence turns the "destination" answers into a real-time vision alignment check
- Works fully online: use a virtual whiteboard or simply have people type their three answers into chat

## Best Used When
- Opening a retrospective and wanting to quickly surface the team's current mood and shared concerns
- The team may be misaligned on direction or has unspoken frustrations
- You want a low-barrier, metaphor-based format that feels playful but leads to substantive conversation
- Remote or hybrid teams (explicitly online-friendly)
