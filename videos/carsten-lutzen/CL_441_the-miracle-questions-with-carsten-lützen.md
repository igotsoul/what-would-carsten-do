# The Miracle Question with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Miracle Question, a core technique from Solution-Focused Coaching. Instead of focusing on how to solve a problem, the technique shifts attention to recognizing what a solved state looks like — enabling the coachee to backtrack from that vision toward concrete actions.

## Methods & Techniques

### The Miracle Question (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A guided imagination exercise that asks the coachee to describe the telltale signs that their problem has been resolved overnight — without them having witnessed how it happened.
- **When to use:** When a coachee is stuck on how to solve a problem; to shift focus from root-cause analysis to solution recognition.
- **Group size:** 1-on-1 coaching, but applicable in small groups
- **Duration:** Varies; allow significant silence and reflection time — not a quick exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Help the coachee identify a clear, concrete problem or question to work with.
  2. Deliver the Miracle Question slowly, allowing pauses: *"Imagine you go to bed tonight and sleep peacefully. Overnight, a miracle happens and the problem is fixed — but you don't know it because you were asleep. When you wake up the next morning, how do you know? What are the telltale signs that the miracle happened?"*
  3. Wait through the silence. The coachee will often say "I don't know" — this is normal. Hold the space.
  4. Help the coachee articulate concrete signs: e.g., "My co-worker says hello to me in the morning," "The business shows up for my demos."
  5. Use those signs as anchors to backtrack toward actionable steps.

## Key Principles
- Solution-Focused Coaching does not obsess over how to find the solution — it focuses on how to *recognize* that the solution has been implemented.
- Concrete, observable signs of success are more useful starting points than abstract problem analysis.
- Backtracking from a desired future state often surfaces actions that forward-thinking problem-solving misses.
- Silence is productive. The Miracle Question requires reflection time; the coachee cannot answer it instantly.

## Facilitation Tips
- Deliver the Miracle Question **slowly** — pace and tone matter. Rushing it undermines the reflective space it needs.
- When the coachee says "I don't know," **don't rescue them**. Wait. Let the silence work for you, not against you.
- Help the coachee make their telltale signs **concrete and observable** — vague answers ("things feel better") need gentle probing.
- Once signs are named, use them as the foundation for backtracking toward actions, not as the end goal themselves.

## Best Used When
- A coachee is stuck in problem-focus and can't see a way forward.
- The team or individual keeps circling root causes without generating movement.
- You want to shift energy from analysis to imagination and ownership.
- A 1-on-1 coaching conversation needs a reframe or a breakthrough moment.
