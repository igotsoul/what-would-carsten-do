# "User Manual to Me" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces "User Manual to Me," a short team-building exercise where each team member writes a personal "user manual" about themselves — covering how they like to work, what they struggle with, and how to communicate with them effectively. The exercise builds mutual understanding within a team and helps people articulate their own working style in their own words.

## Methods & Techniques

### User Manual to Me (ab 00:10)
- **What:** Each team member answers a structured set of questions about themselves and creates a short personal "user manual" they then present to the rest of the team.
- **When to use:** New team formation, onboarding, or as a meaningful introduction exercise at the start of a workshop or team event.
- **Group size:** Works for small to medium teams.
- **Duration:** Short — Carsten suggests roughly 20 minutes for filling out and presenting. Can also be prepared in advance and used as the workshop opener.
- **How it works:**
  1. Each person answers a series of self-reflection questions (see prompts below).
  2. Aim for something concise — Carsten mentions a roughly one-page format.
  3. Each person presents their user manual to the group.
  4. The team uses the shared manuals as a reference for how to collaborate going forward.
- **Example questions mentioned by Carsten:**
  - Conditions I like to work in (e.g., "I prefer quiet, I don't like it when nobody is talking")
  - Things I struggle with — personally and professionally
  - What others may not know about me (common knowledge gaps)
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=per7AdN-a3Y

## Key Principles
- The exercise inverts the usual feedback dynamic: instead of waiting for others to tell you how you come across, you proactively articulate it yourself in your own words.
- Shared user manuals give the team a common reference point — people can refer back to them and act accordingly when collaborating.
- Self-reflection at this level helps people become more self-aware about their own preferences and working style.
- Carsten credits the original concept to Cathy Robinson (link included in the video description).

## Facilitation Tips
- Can be run in two modes: (1) fill out live during a workshop (~20 min total), or (2) ask participants to prepare in advance and use the workshop time purely for presenting.
- Keep the format short and approachable — this shouldn't feel like a personality test. The goal is a quick, honest snapshot.
- Presenting first as the facilitator (sharing your own user manual) lowers the barrier for others to open up.

## Best Used When
- A new team is forming and needs to quickly build mutual understanding.
- Onboarding a new team member who needs to understand existing team members' working styles.
- At the start of a team offsite or longer workshop where psychological safety is important.
- When a team has communication friction and needs a low-stakes way to surface preferences.

## Related Concepts
- Personal Operating Manual (broader term for the same concept)
- Working Agreements / Team Agreements (complementary — defines shared norms, while User Manual to Me defines individual preferences)
- Psychological Safety (this exercise builds the foundation for it)
- Johari Window (similar intent: making the "unknown to others" more visible)
