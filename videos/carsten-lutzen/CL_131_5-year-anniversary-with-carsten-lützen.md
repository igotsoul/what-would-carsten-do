# "5 Year Anniversary" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten celebrates five years of posting weekly YouTube videos, reflecting on the journey, milestones, and community connections that have shaped his development as a Scrum Master and agile coach. This is not a technique-focused video but a personal retrospective on the value of consistent content creation and the doors it has opened. Viewers get a window into Carsten's network, influences, and philosophy around continuous learning.

## Methods & Techniques

### Consistent Weekly Video Publishing as a Learning Practice (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Carsten uses the discipline of publishing one video per week, without exception, as a personal learning mechanism — forcing himself to research and articulate new topics regularly.
- **When to use:** As a professional development strategy for practitioners who want to deepen expertise and build a public presence over time.
- **Duration:** Sustained over 5 years (261 videos at time of recording)
- **How it works:** Commit to a fixed cadence. Each video forces you to learn something new well enough to teach it. The output becomes a portfolio and opens professional doors.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8M1dTkbx9os

### Eco Cycle Planning as an Inspiration Format (ab 05:30)
- **What:** Carsten briefly references using Eco Cycle Planning in a session for Danish IT's project manager conference to help participants get a view on what they are doing.
- **When to use:** When a group needs a systemic overview of their current activities — what to start, stop, continue, or innovate.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8M1dTkbx9os

## Key Principles
- Consistent output over time compounds: 261 videos, 131,000 YouTube views, and significant professional connections all stem from showing up weekly without exception.
- Teaching forces learning: Carsten explicitly uses video creation to push himself to learn new things, not just to share what he already knows.
- Community opens doors: Many of Carsten's most significant professional experiences (conference talks, co-facilitation, coaching invitations) came directly from his visible presence and the connections it created.
- No-estimation thinking evolved over time: Carsten's first video was on Magic Estimation, but through the journey — including conversations with Vasco Duarte on the Scrum Master Toolbox podcast — he came to genuinely value no-estimation approaches.
- Asking better questions applies to yourself too: Carsten references solution-focused coaching (via Solution Surfers) as helping him stop over-engineering and start asking better questions — including of himself.
- Playfulness is a core value: His talk at XP 2022 on "Playful Agility at the LEGO Group" reflects a consistent theme in his work.

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten credits the Liberators (Barry and Christian) as pivotal influences on how he facilitates, specifically through co-designing and co-facilitating Liberating Structures Immersion Workshops in Copenhagen.
- Dandy People's visual posters for agile topics were an inspiration for the format of Carsten's own videos — concise visual overviews of a specific topic or area.
