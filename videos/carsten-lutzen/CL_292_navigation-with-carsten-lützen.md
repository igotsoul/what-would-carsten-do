# "Navigation" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces "navigation" as a core skill in coaching and facilitation — specifically how to steer a session in the moment rather than following a pre-planned agenda. He covers three phases: establishing a north star (coaching agreement), readjusting mid-session by stepping to the "meta level," and closing the conversation in a meaningful way.

## Methods & Techniques

### North Star / Coaching Agreement (ab 00:40)
- **What:** Before diving in, establish a shared goal — a "north star" — that anchors the coaching session. Carsten uses the *Best Hope* exercise for this.
- **When to use:** At the start of any coaching conversation or ad hoc facilitation, before exploring content.
- **Group size:** 1-on-1 primarily, adaptable to small groups.
- **Duration:** A few minutes at session open.
- **How it works:**
  1. Ask the coachee: *"At the end of this session, what would make this the best coaching session for you?"*
  2. Use their answer as the coaching agreement — the shared contract for the session.
  3. Keep the north star visible throughout; revisit if the direction shifts.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lzJse2ESeS8&t=40

### Mid-Session Readjustment — Going to the Meta Level (ab 01:50)
- **What:** Periodically step back from the content of the conversation to evaluate whether the session is still heading toward the north star. Carsten calls this the "meta level" or "balcony view."
- **When to use:** When a coaching conversation has been running for a while or feels stuck; also whenever the coachee seems to be pulling in a different direction.
- **How it works:**
  1. Pause the content-level discussion.
  2. Ask something like: *"With how far we've gone — does this conversation still make sense / add value for you?"* or *"If we stopped right now, what would you take home?"*
  3. Allow the coachee to redirect or reaffirm the north star.
  4. Adjust the coaching agreement if needed — it is not set in stone.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lzJse2ESeS8&t=110

### Closing the Session (ab 03:10)
- **What:** A deliberate way to end a coaching conversation that captures learning and commitment rather than simply running out of time.
- **When to use:** As the session nears its end, or when it becomes clear the north star has been reached.
- **How it works:**
  1. Signal the close gently: *"I can see we're nearing the end — I'm wondering if there's anything from this conversation you'll bring home."*
  2. If the coachee named actions during the session, do NOT recap them yourself — ask the coachee to state them back. The last words of the session should be their own commitment.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lzJse2ESeS8&t=190

## Key Principles
- In coaching and ad hoc facilitation, navigation happens in the moment — it cannot be fully planned upfront.
- A coaching agreement is a contract, not a constraint. It should be revisited and adjusted as understanding deepens.
- The "meta level" / balcony view is a habit worth developing — it feels awkward at first but becomes natural with practice.
- Closing a session well is as important as opening it. A poor close (e.g., "we're out of time") cuts off potential insight and commitment.
- Having the coachee voice their own takeaways and commitments — rather than the coach summarizing — increases ownership.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use the Best Hope question at the start: *"What would make this the best coaching session for you?"* — it primes the coachee to notice progress throughout.
- Don't fear asking meta-level questions mid-session. They feel strange until they become a habit — for both coach and coachee.
- When closing, resist the urge to summarize for the coachee. Prompt them to articulate their own actions: what they said they'd do should be the last thing spoken.
- If the coachee naturally signals they've reached their goal ("I think I've got what I needed"), that is a valid and clean close — use it.

## Best Used When
- Running 1-on-1 coaching conversations without a fixed agenda.
- Facilitating ad hoc team coaching where the direction emerges live.
- You notice a session drifting and want a structured way to recalibrate without disrupting rapport.
- You want to improve session closes beyond "we're out of time."
