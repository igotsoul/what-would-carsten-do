# Retrospective Outcomes with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares practical tips for making retrospective outcomes visible, measurable, and alive between sprints. The core idea is to move from a flat flipchart capturing retro outputs to a dynamic, interactive wall artifact that the team actively updates throughout the sprint — keeping improvement commitments visible and actionable every day.

## Methods & Techniques

### Living Retro Outcomes Board (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A large flipchart or paper placed next to the standup board, showing retro outcomes — updated continuously with post-its to reflect current progress.
- **When to use:** After every retrospective, as a persistent sprint artifact visible to both the team and outside stakeholders.
- **Group size:** Any team size
- **Duration:** Setup takes a few minutes post-retro; maintained daily throughout the sprint
- **How it works:**
  1. After the retro, write outcomes on a large flipchart or piece of paper.
  2. Place it next to the standup board so it enters the daily conversation naturally.
  3. Make each outcome measurable — add a number or a clear done/not-done indicator (SMART format or equivalent).
  4. Add a "Revisit by [date]" marker so the board doesn't feel like stale old information.
  5. Use small post-its to show incremental progress (e.g., "3 tests added so far" → update the post-it as the number grows).
  6. Reference the board at every standup to keep improvement focus active.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qgDnAH2VDRM&t=20

## Key Principles
- Retro outcomes should be **tangible and measurable** — if you can't easily see whether it's done or not, make it more concrete (SMART format helps).
- **Visibility drives accountability** — placing outcomes next to the standup board turns them into a daily conversation, not a once-a-sprint ritual.
- **Retrospectives are not the only place for process improvement.** Carsten explicitly coaches teams that adaptations should happen continuously, not only when the retro comes around.
- A "Revisit by [date]" label prevents the board from becoming background noise — it signals that the information is current and will be refreshed.
- Post-its for updates create **visible momentum**: the board changes as the team makes progress, reinforcing that work is actually happening.

## Facilitation Tips
- Place the outcomes board physically adjacent to the standup board — proximity makes it impossible to ignore.
- Update post-its multiple times a day for fast-moving items; at minimum, discuss the board at every standup.
- Avoid a single flat flip chart that never changes — the lack of movement signals to the team that the outcomes are already forgotten.
- Add the "Revisit then" date at creation time, not as an afterthought, so the board feels like a living commitment from day one.

## Best Used When
- Teams whose retro action items get forgotten within a day or two of the retro.
- Teams where the standup focuses only on sprint tasks and never on process improvements.
- Coaches trying to shift a team from "we only improve during retros" to a culture of continuous adaptation.
