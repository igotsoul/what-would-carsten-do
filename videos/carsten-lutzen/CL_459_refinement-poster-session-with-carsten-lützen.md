# Refinement Poster Session with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen shares a technique called the Refinement Poster Session to inject energy and movement into backlog refinement meetings. Instead of sitting around a table, the team stands and walks from story to story displayed on the walls, with each story having a dedicated Champion who presents it to the group.

## Methods & Techniques

### Refinement Poster Session (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A standing, gallery-walk format for backlog refinement where stories are displayed on the walls and the team moves through them together, guided by a Champion per story.
- **When to use:** When refinement sessions have become mechanical, low-energy, or disengaging — particularly for teams stuck in "sit-and-type" dynamics.
- **Group size:** Whole development team (Scrum team)
- **Duration:** Standard refinement session length
- **How it works:**
  1. Before the session, assign a **Champion** to each backlog item. The Champion ensures the item is ready to refine: clear description, rough acceptance criteria, and external dependencies mapped.
  2. The Scrum Master prints or prepares all stories and posts them on the walls before the session starts.
  3. The team stands up and gathers at the first story on the wall.
  4. The Champion presents their story to the team.
  5. The group discusses, adds notes directly on the paper, removes irrelevant details, or adds new stories by writing on paper and posting them.
  6. Once the group is satisfied, the story can be estimated.
  7. The whole group moves together to the next story and repeats.
  8. After all stories are reviewed, **Magic Estimation** can be used: team members walk around the room and place stories on an estimation wall.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePgQmEtgIpY&t=20

### Magic Estimation (ab 02:10)
- **What:** A quick, physical estimation technique where team members walk around and place stories on an estimation scale on the wall after the poster session.
- **When to use:** As a closing step after all stories have been reviewed in a Refinement Poster Session.
- **How it works:** Once all stories are discussed, team members individually move stories to their preferred position on an estimation wall, creating a relative sizing without lengthy discussion.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ePgQmEtgIpY&t=130

## Key Principles
- Sitting still breeds mechanical behaviour — physical movement changes group dynamics and energy.
- Assigning a Champion per story creates personal ownership and accountability for readiness before the meeting.
- Stories should be ready before refinement: clear goal, rough acceptance criteria, and known dependencies.
- Writing directly on paper and posting new items on the wall keeps the process tangible and collaborative.

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten (as Scrum Master) prepares the wall before the session — teams arrive to a room already set up.
- Keep the group moving as a unit; everyone gathers at each story together rather than self-organizing across the room.
- Allow the group to actively modify stories on the wall — add, remove, or annotate — during the discussion.
- Combine with Magic Estimation at the end for a lightweight, low-friction sizing round.

## Best Used When
- Refinement has become a routine, low-engagement ceremony.
- The team needs a physical and spatial reset to re-engage with backlog work.
- You want to distribute ownership of stories across team members.
