# 15 % Solutions with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Liberating Structure "15% Solutions" — a technique for focusing people on what they can change immediately, without needing additional resources, approval, or others' involvement. The method draws on Solution-Focused Coaching principles and is designed to create quick wins and build momentum toward larger change.

## Methods & Techniques

### 15% Solutions (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A Liberating Structure that focuses participants on the 15% of their work they actually control, rather than the 85% they cannot change. It prompts immediate, autonomous action.
- **When to use:** After identifying key problems or challenges in a group exercise — as a follow-up step to turn insights into concrete, actionable first steps.
- **Group size:** Not specified; works in individual reflection or group settings.
- **Duration:** Short — designed as a quick follow-on exercise, not standalone.
- **How it works:**
  1. After a larger exercise surfaces key issues, ask participants: "What are your 15% Solutions?"
  2. Prompt them to identify actions they can take right away — without needing other people, more budget, or additional resources.
  3. Participants commit to these small, autonomous steps.
  4. Results validate whether the identified problem is real and build a case for deeper change if needed.

## Key Principles
- 85% of our work is outside our direct control — focus energy only on the 15% we can influence.
- Small, autonomous actions create immediate results and generate evidence for larger changes.
- Solution-Focused Coaching tools help narrow down to the essential part of a solution — avoid over-engineering.
- Quick wins build momentum and validate assumptions before investing in bigger interventions.

## Facilitation Tips
- According to Carsten, 15% Solutions should not be used in isolation — combine it with other exercises that surface problems or insights first.
- Use the framing question explicitly: *"What can you go out and do right away that doesn't require other people, more resources, or money?"* — this keeps actions truly autonomous and immediate.

## Best Used When
- A group has identified challenges or action areas and needs to move from insight to action.
- Participants feel stuck waiting for permission, resources, or systemic change.
- You want to close a workshop with concrete individual commitments.
- Building a case for larger organizational change — start small, gather evidence.
