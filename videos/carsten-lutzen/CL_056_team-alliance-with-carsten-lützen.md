# "Team Alliance" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen introduces the Team Alliance exercise, a workshop opening technique from the coaching methodology tradition, shared with him by Daniel R. The exercise invites participants to co-create the relational agreements they want to hold for their time together, rather than having a facilitator impose ground rules. It sets a collaborative, solution-focused tone from the very start of a session.

## Methods & Techniques

### Team Alliance (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A co-created group contract where participants define the relationship and atmosphere they want to cultivate together during a workshop or team engagement.
- **When to use:** At the start of a new workshop, team kickoff, or any session where establishing shared norms and relational quality matters.
- **Group size:** Any size; structured to work in individual, small group, and plenary phases.
- **Duration:** Not specified explicitly; typically 10–20 minutes depending on group size and depth.
- **How it works:**
  1. Prepare a flipchart titled "Team Alliance" and place it visibly in the room.
  2. Present 2–3 prompts to the group. Carsten suggests examples such as:
     - *What is the atmosphere/culture you want to create in this meeting?*
     - *What is our High Dream for how we can work together?*
     - *What will help us when things get tough?*
  3. Use the **1-2-4-All** structure: give individuals 1 minute of silent reflection, then discuss in pairs or small groups, then share in plenary.
  4. Capture responses on the flipchart.
  5. Keep the flipchart visible and refer back to it throughout the day as needed.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7Tp2sR0o_X4&t=30

## Key Principles
- The alliance is co-created by participants, not dictated by the facilitator — ownership stays with the group.
- Prompts are framed positively and solution-focused: asking what will *help* rather than what might *hinder*.
- The physical flipchart stays present and visible throughout the session, reinforcing the shared agreement rather than letting it fade after the opening.
- Carsten notes that the facilitator can subtly shape the conversation through which prompts they choose — a reminder that facilitation is never fully neutral.
- The concept draws on the idea that "everything has and is a relationship," positioning relational quality as a foundational element of any group work.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use the 1-2-4-All structure to ensure everyone has a voice before moving to plenary, including quieter participants.
- Keep the Team Alliance flipchart "loud and proud" — center of the room, not tucked away — so it remains a living reference, not a forgotten slide.
- Customize prompts to the specific context; many alternative prompts are available online and the exercise is highly adaptable.
- The "High Dream" prompt draws on Solution-Focused Approach language and encourages aspirational, generative thinking rather than risk-mitigation thinking.

## Best Used When
- Opening a new team workshop, offsite, or multi-session engagement.
- Starting work with a newly formed team or a team entering a new phase.
- Any facilitated session where psychological safety, collaboration quality, or relational trust is important to the outcome.
- As an alternative to facilitator-imposed ground rules, especially with groups who value autonomy and co-creation.

## Related Concepts
- **1-2-4-All** (Liberating Structures) — the engagement structure Carsten uses to run the exercise.
- **Solution-Focused Approach** — the positive, forward-looking framing of the prompts draws explicitly on this methodology.
- **Team Contracting / Working Agreements** — Team Alliance is a relationship-centered variant of the broader practice of establishing shared norms.
