# Virtues of a Scrum Master with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares two core practices that make a Scrum Master more effective: maintaining a personal Kanban board for transparency, and applying a scientific hypothesis-driven approach to diagnosing team dysfunction. The video is a short, practitioner-level reflection on habits that build credibility and drive continuous improvement.

## Methods & Techniques

### Personal Scrum Master Kanban Board (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A personal task board (To Do / Doing / Done) that the Scrum Master maintains visibly — making their own agenda, priorities, and work transparent to the team and organization.
- **When to use:** Continuously, as a standing practice for any Scrum Master.
- **Group size:** Individual (Scrum Master's own tool)
- **Duration:** Ongoing habit, not a one-time exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Maintain a simple three-column board (backlog, in progress, done)
  2. Populate it with Sprint-related tasks and organizational improvement items
  3. Keep it visible so the team can see what you're working on and why
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LJ0xBE4YKds&t=20

### Scientific Method for Team Diagnosis (ab 01:30)
- **What:** When a problematic behavior is observed (e.g., people zoning out in meetings), Carsten recommends generating multiple hypotheses for the root cause before acting — then systematically falsifying them rather than jumping to conclusions.
- **When to use:** Whenever a team behavior seems off or performance is declining; especially useful to avoid premature or wrong interventions.
- **Group size:** Applicable to any team size
- **Duration:** Varies; hypothesis generation is quick, validation may take one or more Sprints
- **How it works:**
  1. Observe a behavior you want to change (e.g., disengagement in Daily Scrum or Planning)
  2. Generate several competing hypotheses explaining the root cause
  3. Design ways to falsify each hypothesis — what evidence would prove it wrong?
  4. Identify the surviving hypothesis and tackle the actual root cause
  5. Add the fix as a task to your personal Kanban board
  6. Track resolution and continue observing
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LJ0xBE4YKds&t=90

## Key Principles
- Start with yourself — the easiest place to create change is in your own behavior and practices
- Transparency is a core Scrum value that applies to the Scrum Master's own work, not just the team's
- A visible Scrum Master agenda builds trust and shows intent
- Don't jump to conclusions when you see unwanted behavior — what worked before may not work now
- Diagnosis before intervention: use hypothesis-driven thinking to find real root causes
- Fixing the root cause, not the symptom, is what grows a team toward high performance

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten suggests actively walking around and observing meetings — watch body language, phone usage, and engagement levels as signals
- Keep your own board accessible so the team can see it, turning your work into a transparency artifact
- Once a root cause is identified, put the fix task on your board — closing the loop between diagnosis and action

## Best Used When
- A Scrum Master is looking to become more structured and credible in their role
- Teams are showing low engagement in Scrum ceremonies without an obvious reason
- You want to move from reactive firefighting to systematic continuous improvement
