# "Standing and Singing" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen shares a simple energizer for workshops where participants have been sitting for a long time. The activity combines singing a familiar song with a physical trigger — standing up and sitting down whenever a word starting with a specific letter is sung — to get people moving and re-energized.

## Methods & Techniques

### Standing and Singing Energizer (ab 00:20)
- **What:** Participants sing a song (live or via YouTube) and stand up or sit down every time a word starting with a pre-chosen letter is sung. The letter "H" and the song "My Bonnie" are Carsten's go-to example.
- **When to use:** When participants have been sitting for a long time and energy levels are low; ideal as a mid-workshop energizer or icebreaker.
- **Group size:** Any size — works in a room with chairs.
- **Duration:** A few minutes; one or two rounds of a short song is sufficient.
- **How it works:**
  1. Choose a song with frequent occurrences of the trigger letter (e.g., "My Bonnie" for the letter "B").
  2. Have everyone start standing.
  3. Begin singing — either lead the group yourself or play a YouTube version.
  4. Every time a word starting with the chosen letter is sung, participants toggle: stand up if sitting, sit down if standing.
  5. The movement creates energy, laughter, and re-engagement.
- **Facilitation note:** Carsten recommends singing it yourself rather than playing it, as getting participants to sing along amplifies the fun and energy.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dfw6yFAj9KI

## Key Principles
- Physical movement breaks mental fatigue — even a short, playful activity can restore energy during long seated workshops.
- Participation level can be calibrated to the group's "bravery" — facilitators can choose a low-risk version (playing a YouTube video) or a higher-engagement version (group singing).
- Low-prep energizers are valuable tools to keep on hand; Carsten describes them as something to "pull out of the drawer" when needed.

## Facilitation Tips
- Singing yourself rather than using a recording increases energy and group cohesion.
- Adjust the boldness of the activity based on how playful or reserved your group is.
- Use a song with many words starting with the trigger letter to maximize movement (e.g., "My Bonnie" has frequent "B" words).

## Best Used When
- Participants have been sitting through long presentations or working sessions.
- The group needs a quick, low-prep reset before continuing.
- The facilitator wants to introduce light-heartedness and physicality without elaborate setup.
