# "My Journey" by Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen shares his personal career journey from junior developer to agile coach and Scrum Master. He traces the key turning points — from studying computer science to joining the LEGO Group, and from discovering solution-focused coaching to earning an ICF certification — that shaped his identity as a coach today.

## Methods & Techniques

### Solution-Focused Coaching (ab 03:20)
- **What:** A coaching approach that shifts focus away from problem analysis toward building what is actually needed — no more, no less.
- **When to use:** When coaching individuals or teams who tend to over-engineer or generalize solutions beyond what the situation requires.
- **Group size:** Individual or team level
- **Duration:** Ongoing practice, not a single exercise
- **How it works:** Carsten describes it as a mindset shift — moving away from a computer science tendency to generalize everything, toward asking "how can I build the thing I need right now?" It was introduced to him at the Agile & Beyond (AthensX) conference in 2018.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WWdJiRscupE&t=200

### ICF Coaching Certification Journey (ab 04:00)
- **What:** Pursuing an International Coaching Federation (ICF) certification as a structured path to deepen coaching competence — valued for the journey, not the credential itself.
- **When to use:** When you want to move from informally "thinking you're coaching" to having a grounded, validated practice.
- **Group size:** Individual development path
- **Duration:** Multi-year journey
- **How it works:** Carsten completed his ICF certification in 2021. He emphasizes that the value was in the learning and self-validation process — confirming his own ability to coach at both team and individual level.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WWdJiRscupE&t=240

## Key Principles
- Theoretical foundation amplifies practical experience — Carsten credits university study with giving him the "why" behind practices he'd already been applying intuitively.
- The pivot from developer to coach is often gradual — Carsten grew into the Scrum Master role naturally; it wasn't a planned career switch.
- A certification's value lies in the journey, not the paper — the ICF process validated Carsten's self-belief as much as it signaled external credibility.
- Solution-focused thinking is a corrective to over-engineering — especially relevant for coaches with technical backgrounds who default to generalizing problems.
- Combining modalities accelerates growth — Carsten credits the combination of solution-focused coaching, Liberating Structures, and creating videos as defining his coaching identity.
