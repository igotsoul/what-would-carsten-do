# "Powerful Questions" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the concept of Powerful Questions as an essential tool for Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches. Rather than providing a comprehensive list of examples, he focuses on the underlying mindset and behavior required to ask and hold powerful questions effectively — particularly around embracing silence and avoiding the temptation to steer coachees toward predetermined solutions.

## Methods & Techniques

### Powerful Questions as a Coaching Tool (ab 00:30)
- **What:** An umbrella concept for question types that move a coachee's or team's thinking forward — helping them reflect, gain new perspectives, and see past current obstacles.
- **When to use:** During coaching sessions, retrospectives, or any facilitated conversation where the goal is to unlock new thinking rather than deliver answers.
- **Group size:** Applicable in 1-on-1 coaching and team settings
- **Duration:** Technique applies throughout a session, not time-boxed
- **How it works:**
  1. Approach the conversation with genuine curiosity about the coachee or team's situation
  2. Ask a question aimed at shifting perspective or fostering reflection (e.g., "Let's say this is not a problem anymore — how would that make you feel?" or "If this was solved in the blink of an eye, how could you tell?")
  3. After asking, stop talking — hold the silence
  4. If there is no response, do not assume confusion; wait for the coachee to speak
  5. Only if they signal genuine confusion, ask: "Did that make sense, or should I try to rephrase?"
  6. Never answer the question on the coachee's behalf or reformulate toward a solution you already have in mind
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ucFvtpPSNIM&t=30

## Key Principles
- Powerful Questions are context-dependent — what works brilliantly with one person may fail with another; there is no universal list
- Silence after a question is a positive signal: it likely means you have asked something genuinely worth thinking about
- The coach is not the expert on solutions — the coachee or team is; do not bias questions toward a preferred answer
- The metaphor Carsten uses: a powerful question helps the coachee find a path around or over a "big rock" that has been blocking their view
- Curiosity is the prerequisite — be curious about the situation, the person, and the outcome before crafting any question

## Facilitation Tips
- Do not panic during silence; in early practice this is a common impulse — take a breath and trust the question
- If a coachee looks completely blank, wait first; speak only to ask if rephrasing would help
- Never reformulate the question into an answer or steer it toward your own hypothesis
- Search for example Powerful Questions online as a starting library, but internalize the mindset rather than memorizing scripts

## Best Used When
- A coachee or team is stuck and unable to see past a current problem or conflict
- You notice the conversation circling the same ground without progress
- You want to foster ownership of the solution rather than dependency on the coach
- Retrospectives where reflection depth matters more than speed
