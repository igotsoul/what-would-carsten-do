# Customer Letter with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the "Customer Letter" exercise from Marty Cagan's book *Inspired* as a powerful way to formalize and align around a product vision. The exercise asks teams to imagine a future satisfied customer writing to praise the product — and a CEO responding with the company's perspective. Carsten suggests combining it with Solution-Focused questions to deepen its impact.

## Methods & Techniques

### The Customer Letter (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A visioning exercise in which the team writes two fictional letters set in the future: one from a happy customer praising the product, and one from the CEO explaining how the company made it happen.
- **When to use:** Early in product development to align the team and stakeholders on a shared product vision and clarify what success looks like from both the customer and business perspective.
- **Group size:** Team + stakeholders (no specific size mentioned)
- **Duration:** Not specified; likely a workshop-length activity
- **How it works:**
  1. Imagine the product has launched successfully.
  2. Write a letter from a satisfied customer describing why the product is the best — what they love, what they couldn't live without, and what problems it solved.
  3. Write a response from the CEO, explaining what the company had to overcome or align on to build the product (e.g., internal challenges, strategic decisions).
  4. Make both letters publicly available to the team and stakeholders as a shared north star.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3-r8BDmlUQ8&t=30s

## Key Principles
- A compelling product vision must address two perspectives: the **customer** (what problem is solved, what value is delivered) and the **company** (what had to be aligned or overcome internally).
- Making the vision artifact public and visible creates shared clarity and accountability across team and stakeholders.
- The exercise forces specificity: vague aspirations become concrete statements about what the product does and why people love it.
- Carsten hints that combining this exercise with **Solution-Focused questions** can enrich the output — prompting participants to imagine the best possible future state in more detail.

## Facilitation Tips
- Encourage writers to be concrete and personal in the customer letter — what specific feature or outcome would a real customer rave about?
- The CEO letter is a forcing function for surfacing internal blockers and strategic priorities early — treat it seriously, not as an afterthought.
- Consider using Solution-Focused questions (e.g., "What would be different? What would you notice?") to help the group elaborate on the future state before or during writing.
- Sharing the letters with stakeholders creates a lightweight but powerful alignment artifact.

## Best Used When
- A product team needs to establish or re-articulate a shared vision at the start of a product initiative.
- Stakeholders and team members have different or unclear ideas of what success looks like.
- Teams are struggling to connect day-to-day work to a meaningful product direction.
