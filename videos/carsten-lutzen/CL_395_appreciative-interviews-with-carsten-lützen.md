# Appreciative Interviews with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces Appreciative Interviews, a Liberating Structure that surfaces success stories within a group and extracts shared factors behind those successes. The format uses structured pair interviews followed by a 1-2-4-All pattern to progressively synthesize learnings and generate actionable insights. It is especially powerful for shifting focus from problems to what already works.

## Methods & Techniques

### Appreciative Interviews (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A Liberating Structure in which participants interview each other about personal success stories, then cross-share those stories in groups of four to identify common success factors.
- **When to use:** When a group needs to build on existing strengths, shift energy toward what works, or generate positive momentum before planning next steps.
- **Group size:** Works with large groups; pairs are the base unit, then groups of four.
- **Duration:** Roughly 30–45 minutes total (pair interviews + group synthesis + 1-2-4-All debrief).
- **How it works:**
  1. Arrange chairs in pairs facing each other. Participants find a partner.
  2. One person shares a story of a personal or team success — going into detail about what specifically made it successful. The interviewer asks clarifying questions and takes notes.
  3. Swap roles: the interviewer becomes the storyteller.
  4. Two pairs join to form a group of four. Each person retells their partner's success story (not their own).
  5. The group of four identifies common factors and patterns across all four stories — what contributed to success?
  6. Capture insights on a wall, whiteboard, or flipchart using Post-Its or paper (allow ~15 minutes).
  7. Run a **1-2-4-All** debrief: silent individual reflection → pair discussion → group of four synthesis → full group share-out, with one or two representatives per group presenting to the plenum.
  8. Use the collected success factors to define concrete actions to pursue.

### 1-2-4-All (ab 02:45)
- **What:** A classic Liberating Structure used here as the closing synthesis step — moving from individual silence through pairs and foursomes to a full-group share-out.
- **When to use:** Whenever you want to ensure all voices are included before converging on insights or decisions.
- **How it works:** Silent reflection → pair conversation → group of four → full plenum.

## Key Principles
- Focus on successes, not problems — energy follows attention.
- Cross-sharing (telling someone else's story) creates empathy and reinforces the original storyteller's success.
- Common factors across diverse stories are more reliable signals than individual opinions.
- Progressive grouping (1 → 2 → 4 → All) ensures broad participation before converging on shared insights.
- Capturing learnings visibly (wall, whiteboard, flipchart) makes patterns tangible and discussable.

## Facilitation Tips
- Set up chairs in pairs facing each other before participants arrive — it signals the format immediately.
- Prompt interviewers explicitly to ask clarifying questions and take notes; this keeps them active and improves retelling quality.
- When forming groups of four, instruct each person to tell their *partner's* story — this is the key mechanic that builds shared ownership of successes.
- Give the capture phase enough time (~15 min); rushing it loses the richness of the stories.
- In the 1-2-4-All debrief, start with silence so quieter participants form their own views before group dynamics take over.

## Best Used When
- A team or group needs a positive reframe after a difficult period.
- You want to identify what has already been working before planning improvements.
- Running a retrospective or kick-off where building on strengths matters more than diagnosing failures.
- Working with large groups where pair-based formats help surface many diverse stories simultaneously.

## Related Concepts
- **1-2-4-All** — the closing Liberating Structure used to synthesize learnings from the interview phase.
- **Appreciative Inquiry** — the broader philosophy of strengths-based change that Appreciative Interviews is grounded in.
- **Retrospectives** — this format works well as a strengths-focused retro variant.
