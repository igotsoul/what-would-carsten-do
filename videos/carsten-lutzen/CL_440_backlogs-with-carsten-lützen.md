# Backlogs with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten explains the relationship between the Product Backlog and Sprint Backlog in Scrum, framing them as different views on the same list rather than two separate artefacts. The key insight is about responsibility: items in the Sprint Backlog remain part of the Product Backlog, but ownership shifts from the Product Owner to the Development Team once sprint planning concludes.

## Methods & Techniques

### Product Backlog vs. Sprint Backlog — Two Views, One List (ab 00:20)
- **What:** The Sprint Backlog is not a separate list but a subset/view of the Product Backlog, filtered to the items the team committed to for the current sprint.
- **When to use:** Useful mental model to explain to teams why both artefacts exist and how they relate.
- **Group size:** Whole Scrum Team
- **Duration:** Concept to introduce during Sprint Planning or onboarding
- **How it works:**
  1. Product Owner brings a prioritized, ideally refined backlog to Sprint Planning together with a Sprint Goal.
  2. Product Owner and Development Team negotiate which items (typically top priorities) move into the Sprint Backlog.
  3. Once in the sprint, responsibility for those items shifts to the Development Team — they manage and refine the Sprint Backlog as new knowledge emerges.
  4. The Product Owner continues reprioritizing the remaining Product Backlog and prepares the goal for the next sprint.
  5. Items stay in the Product Backlog (and its prioritized view) until they are done.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IaW-g5KLX6M&t=20

## Key Principles
- The Sprint Backlog is a **subset view** of the Product Backlog, not an independent list — same items, different lens.
- **Responsibility shifts at Sprint Planning:** once items enter the Sprint Backlog, the Development Team owns them, not the Product Owner.
- The Product Owner's primary role during the sprint is **acceptance** — verifying that what was delivered matches the intent — not managing Sprint Backlog items.
- The Development Team must be able to **run with Sprint Backlog items autonomously**, implementing and refining without constant Product Owner involvement.
- New knowledge uncovered mid-sprint should be **reflected in the Sprint Backlog** — it is a living artefact.
- The Sprint Goal drives negotiation in Sprint Planning about what enters the sprint.

## Facilitation Tips
- When teams argue about "whose backlog it is," use the two-views framing to dissolve the debate: same list, different responsibility depending on scope.
- Carsten suggests making the priority view visible to the Product Owner at all times so they can reprioritize the remaining backlog while the team executes the sprint independently.

## Best Used When
- Onboarding new Scrum Teams who confuse the two backlog artefacts.
- Sprint Planning facilitation where Product Owner and Development Team negotiate scope.
- Coaching conversations about accountability and autonomy within a sprint.
- Retrospectives where teams surface friction around backlog ownership.
