# Talking with Pixies

## Summary
Carsten introduces "Talking with Pixies," a Liberating Structure development for exploring elusive goals through structured dual-perspective consultation. One "client" shares an uncertain goal with two "pixies" — a Protector Pixie who advocates for staying put, and an Innovator Pixie who advocates for pursuing the goal — deliberately keeping both sides in a positive frame. The format helps individuals and teams clarify whether to pursue a goal by hearing their own situation reflected through two distinct lenses.

## Methods & Techniques

### Talking with Pixies — Full Exercise (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A structured small-group exercise where one client explores an elusive goal with two pixies who take opposing but positive perspectives on whether to pursue it.
- **When to use:** When a person or team faces a goal that feels elusive or uncertain — unsure whether to commit to pursuing it or change direction.
- **Group size:** Groups of 3 (ideal) or 4 (if numbers don't divide by 3). Run in parallel across larger groups.
- **Duration:** ~45–50 minutes for a full group of 3–4 to rotate through all client turns.
- **How it works:**
  1. Introduce the exercise and the two pixie roles.
  2. Client shares their elusive goal for **2 minutes**: what it is, why it matters, what feels uncertain.
  3. Pixies ask **clarifying questions** for 1 minute to build understanding.
  4. Pixies discuss — **Protector Pixie** argues why the current situation is worth protecting (positive framing only); **Innovator Pixie** argues what can be gained by pursuing the goal.
  5. Client turns back and **replays** what they heard from the pixies — integrating the two perspectives.
  6. Rotate: a new client steps in, with new pixies.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lKS_fj3Mhtc&t=20

### Sequential vs. Simultaneous Pixie Variant (ab 02:10)
- **What:** Two structural options for how the pixies deliver their perspectives.
- **Sequential variant:** Both pixies first act as Protector Pixie (2 minutes), then both switch to Innovator Pixie (2 minutes). Easier to track, less chaotic.
- **Simultaneous variant:** One pixie is permanently the Protector, the other permanently the Innovator — they discuss at the same time. More dynamic but harder to follow.
- **When to use:** Carsten recommends the sequential variant for most groups; the simultaneous variant for groups comfortable with structured debate.

## Key Principles
- Both pixie roles stay deliberately **positive**: the Protector explains what is worth protecting about the status quo; the Innovator explains what can be gained by moving forward. Neither uses fear, catastrophizing, or "you'll fail if you don't."
- The **client replaying the pixies' conversation** is the integration step — it forces active listening and helps the client crystallise what resonated.
- Small groups are essential: 3–4 people preserves the intimacy needed for real reflection. Larger groups lose this dynamic.
- The exercise changes perspective structurally rather than asking people to "consider other viewpoints" abstractly — the physical/role separation does the work.
- Inspired by liberating structures logic: distribute control, shift mental models, unlock new pathways through structure rather than instruction.

## Facilitation Tips
- Decide on sequential vs. simultaneous variant before the session and brief participants clearly — mid-exercise switching creates confusion.
- When groups of 4 are needed, Carsten suggests simply keeping two pixies per client and accepting a slightly longer total runtime.
- Brief the pixies explicitly on the **positive framing rule**: it's tempting to slip into "you'll regret staying" or "it's too risky to go" — steer them back to the constructive frame.
- If running in parallel, use a shared timer visible to all groups so rounds stay synchronised without a facilitator circulating constantly.
- Invite participants to reflect: "What did the pixies say that surprised you?" after the replay step — this surfaces what actually shifted.

## Best Used When
- An individual or team is stuck at a crossroads about a goal and rational pros/cons analysis isn't helping
- You want to surface both the value of stability and the value of change without setting up a debate
- Coaching contexts where a coachee needs to hear their own situation from two structured perspectives
- Workshop sequences that deal with innovation or strategic direction decisions
