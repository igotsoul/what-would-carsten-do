# My Coaching Journey with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares his personal journey from computer scientist and over-engineer to ICF-certified Associate Certified Coach (ACC). The video traces key inflection points from 2018 to 2021, highlighting the people, books, and practices that shaped his coaching development. It serves as an honest, personal reflection aimed at inspiring others to invest in their own coaching path.

## Methods & Techniques

### Solution-Focused Coaching (ab 01:40)
- **What:** A coaching approach that focuses on solutions and desired futures rather than problems. Carsten was introduced to it via Roy Marriott and deepened it through work with Jesper (solution focus denmark).
- **When to use:** 1-on-1 coaching, Scrum Master conversations, helping people clarify "done" criteria and remove unnecessary scope.
- **Group size:** Typically 1-on-1
- **Duration:** Varies
- **How it works:** Centers on questions like "How do I know something is done?" and "What are the signs it's done?" — shifting focus from problems to observable outcomes.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=su0FI6zCjcY&t=100

### Peer Coaching / Free Practice Sessions (ab 03:10)
- **What:** Carsten offered free coaching sessions on LinkedIn to practice his skills — connecting with many people while building deliberate coaching practice.
- **When to use:** When developing coaching skills and needing real practice partners outside your organization.
- **Group size:** 1-on-1
- **Duration:** Not specified
- **How it works:** Offer sessions openly (e.g., via LinkedIn), let willing participants sign up, run structured sessions, and treat each one as a learning lab.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=su0FI6zCjcY&t=190

### Mentor Coaching (ab 04:00)
- **What:** Working with an experienced coach who observes your sessions and gives targeted feedback on specific weak points to improve question quality and in-session behavior.
- **When to use:** When progressing toward a coaching certification or wanting to level up beyond self-assessment.
- **Group size:** 1-on-1 (coach + mentor)
- **Duration:** Ongoing over months/years
- **How it works:** Coach is observed or submits recordings; mentor provides pinpointed feedback on questions, presence, and coaching moves.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=su0FI6zCjcY&t=240

### Troika Consulting (ab 05:10)
- **What:** A peer learning format Carsten recommends for coaches to practice and learn together in small groups.
- **When to use:** When a group of coaches or practitioners wants to support each other's development without a formal mentor.
- **Group size:** 3 (classic Troika format)
- **Duration:** Short rounds, typically 15-30 min per round
- **How it works:** One person presents a challenge; the others consult. Roles rotate. Format creates a safe container for real practice and honest reflection.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=su0FI6zCjcY&t=310

## Key Principles

- **Hindsight is 20/20 on over-engineering:** Carsten's background as a "world-class champion in over-engineering" taught him that gold-plating is rarely used — solution-focused thinking helped him break that habit.
- **"How do I know something is done?"** is a powerful question that applies equally in development and coaching — it grounds abstract intent in observable evidence.
- **The road matters more than the certificate:** The ACC certification is described as "a nice piece of paper" — the real value is the journey of practice, feedback, and refinement over years.
- **Language matters:** Clean Language (Judy Rees) reinforced for Carsten how much the exact phrasing of questions shapes coaching conversations.
- **Find your people:** Meeting coaches, journeying together, and finding a guide (like Jesper for Carsten) are accelerators that no book alone can replace.

## Facilitation Tips

- Offer practice coaching sessions openly — the willingness of strangers to give you their time is a gift worth taking seriously.
- Use Troika Consulting as a peer-coaching container when you don't have access to a formal mentor.
- Seek mentor coaching to identify blind spots you can't see yourself.
