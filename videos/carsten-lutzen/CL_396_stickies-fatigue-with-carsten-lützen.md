# Stickies Fatigue with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the concept of "Sticky Notes Fatigue" — the tiredness teams develop after running retrospectives with Post-Its for a long time. He shares three concrete exercises that generate actionable outcomes without relying on writing lots of stickies, applicable both physically and digitally.

## Methods & Techniques

### Walk and Talk (ab 01:20)
- **What:** A retrospective done on foot — pairs walk around the office or outside while discussing pre-prepared reflection questions.
- **When to use:** When the team is stuck in a routine meeting-room retrospective format and needs a change of scenery and energy.
- **Group size:** Works well in pairs; can be broken into multiple pairs simultaneously.
- **Duration:** Flexible; Carsten suggests stopping along the way to share findings between pairs.
- **How it works:**
  1. Prepare a list of reflection questions in advance.
  2. Break the team into pairs.
  3. Pairs walk and talk through the questions.
  4. Facilitator (or each participant) brings a sketchpad to capture key notes.
  5. Take stops along the route to share insights between pairs.
  6. Play around with the format — it's flexible.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ONdebXryd8&t=80

### Chaos Cocktail Party (ab 01:55)
- **What:** A dot-voting format where participants pair up repeatedly, pitch ideas to each other, and distribute points — ensuring everyone is engaged throughout.
- **When to use:** When you need to surface and prioritize ideas without generating walls of sticky notes; great as a retro or ideation exercise.
- **Group size:** Any group that can be broken into pairs.
- **Duration:** Multiple rounds of pairing; Carsten references a dedicated video for full details.
- **How it works:**
  1. Each person writes their idea on the front of a piece of paper.
  2. Pair up; each person presents their idea to their partner.
  3. Each pair distributes exactly 5 points between the two ideas (e.g., 3+2) — no half points.
  4. Reshuffle pairs and repeat for several rounds.
  5. Tally points at the end; the idea with the most points wins the group's focus.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ONdebXryd8&t=115

### Treasure Map / Sea Map (ab 02:45)
- **What:** A visual, metaphor-driven goal-setting exercise where the team draws a ship journey toward a treasure, mapping obstacles and charting a course — usable across multiple retrospectives.
- **When to use:** Long-term planning, goal-setting, or when a team needs a persistent visual to track progress over several sprints or retrospectives.
- **Group size:** Full team; collaborative drawing on a large flipchart.
- **Duration:** Initial session to create the map; then referenced repeatedly in subsequent retros.
- **How it works:**
  1. Place a large flipchart on the table and hand out drawing materials.
  2. Prompt: "Imagine we are a ship — how would it look?" Draw the ship in one corner.
  3. Draw a red X for the treasure somewhere on the map.
  4. Define what's in the treasure — what does success look like?
  5. Map out the space in between: what obstacles exist?
  6. Chart a course from the ship to the treasure.
  7. Return to the map in future retrospectives to check progress on the journey.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9ONdebXryd8&t=165

## Key Principles
- **Stickies Fatigue is real:** Even switching to new exercises can feel stale if they still rely on writing Post-Its — the format itself becomes the problem, not just the specific exercise.
- **Outcome over format:** The goal of a retro is actionable outcomes; the medium (sticky notes) is just a tool, not a requirement.
- **Engagement through movement and dialogue:** Walk and Talk and Chaos Cocktail Party keep everyone active and talking, rather than silently writing.
- **Metaphors create commitment:** The Treasure Map uses narrative and visual metaphor to make goals tangible and emotionally resonant across time.

## Facilitation Tips
- When fatigue sets in, step back and question the *medium*, not just the *exercise* — switching from stickies to drawing or conversation changes the energy entirely.
- For Walk and Talk, prepare your reflection questions in advance and bring something to capture key insights yourself.
- In Chaos Cocktail Party, enforce the no-half-points rule to keep the voting decisive.
- The Treasure Map works best when kept visible and revisited — it's a living artifact, not a one-time exercise.

## Best Used When
- A team has been running retrospectives for a long time and engagement is dropping.
- The team feels like retros are repetitive or low-value.
- You want a retro format that generates discussion and decisions without large amounts of written output.
- You need a long-term planning artifact that spans multiple iterations (Treasure Map).
