# "Instigator" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen shares a quick energizer game called "Instigator," credited to Sendy Kik. The game gets participants out of their seats and moving, using a simple detective-and-mimic mechanic. It is designed to heighten energy in a room without requiring any materials or preparation.

## Methods & Techniques

### Instigator (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A physical group energizer where one person secretly leads movements that the group copies, while a designated detective tries to identify who is leading.
- **When to use:** At the start of a session, after a break, or any time the group needs an energy boost and to get out of their chairs.
- **Group size:** Minimum 8–10 people.
- **Duration:** 5–10 minutes (implied by the game's simplicity).
- **How it works:**
  1. Pick one person to be the **detective** and send them out of the room.
  2. With the remaining group, secretly select one **instigator**.
  3. Have everyone stand in a circle.
  4. The instigator starts a movement (a gesture, dance move, funny motion); the rest of the group copies it.
  5. Bring the detective back into the room.
  6. The instigator regularly changes the movement to make it harder to trace the origin.
  7. The detective has **three guesses** to identify the instigator.
  8. If the detective guesses correctly within three tries, the detective wins; otherwise, the instigator wins.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FOn7pL4ns1c

## Key Principles
- Simple mechanics create high engagement — the game requires no materials, setup, or prior knowledge.
- Physical movement is a powerful tool for shifting energy; getting people away from tables and chairs changes the room's dynamic.
- A light competitive element (detective vs. instigator) adds enough stakes to make it engaging without stress.

## Facilitation Tips
- The instigator must change movements frequently — if they repeat the same move, the detective can easily trace it back to them. Remind the instigator of this before the game starts.
- Keep the circle tight so movements are visible to everyone but the origin is still ambiguous to the detective.
- Encourage silly or exaggerated movements to maximize fun and energy.

## Best Used When
- The group energy is low and people need to physically reset.
- Transitioning into a new session segment and wanting to re-engage participants.
- Working with groups that respond well to playful, low-stakes competition.
- You need a zero-prep, no-materials energizer that works immediately.
