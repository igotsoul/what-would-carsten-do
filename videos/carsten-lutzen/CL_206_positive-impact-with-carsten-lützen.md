# "Positive Impact" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a simple self-reflection tool for coaches, scrum masters, and facilitators to map and evaluate their impact across the people they work with. By creating a structured table and applying the "What? So What? Now What?" reflection framework, practitioners can gain clarity on where they are making a difference — and where they might want to shift their focus or disengage.

## Methods & Techniques

### Impact Mapping Table (ab 01:00)
- **What:** A private, simple table where you list the people you work with, rate your impact on each (big, medium, low), and describe how that impact shows in practice.
- **When to use:** During personal coaching supervision, quarterly reflections, or whenever you feel unclear about your contribution to the organization.
- **Group size:** Individual (1 person — yourself)
- **Duration:** Open-ended; works well as a journaling or whiteboard exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Create a table with columns: person's name, impact level (big / medium / low), and how the impact shows.
  2. Fill in the "as-is" view: how do you currently see your impact on each person?
  3. Fill in a "to-be" view (column 2): who would you like to impact more, who less — and why?
  4. Identify deltas: where are the gaps between as-is and to-be?
  5. Optionally add a third column or row for **negative impact** — people whose experience of you is not positive, or relationships where your presence may not be helpful anymore.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jOD_d7b1JfQ&t=60

### What? So What? Now What? Debrief (ab 03:30)
- **What:** A three-step reflection sequence applied after completing the impact table to turn observations into action.
- **When to use:** After any self-assessment or data-gathering exercise.
- **How it works:**
  1. **What?** — What did you observe? Was it easy or hard to fill in the table? Do you know where your impact is?
  2. **So What?** — Why does this matter to you? Why is it important to influence person A and not person C?
  3. **Now What?** — What concrete actions can you take to move closer to your "to-be" picture? How can you deepen impact in some places and reduce it in others?
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jOD_d7b1JfQ&t=210

## Key Principles
- Your impact as a coach is often intangible — mapping it is an act of making the invisible visible.
- Negative impact is equally worth examining. Carsten notes that some people may not respond well to his style (humor, acting), and recognizing this allows him to adapt.
- Sometimes a coaching relationship has run its course — it may be time to hand off to a different coach or facilitator rather than persist.
- This is a confidential, personal tool — not meant to be shared or reported, but to prompt honest self-assessment.
- The goal is to become more "proficient and efficient" as a coach, not just busier.

## Facilitation Tips
- Keep the table private and confidential — honesty depends on it.
- Don't overthink completeness. The exercise is useful even if you can't name every impact precisely.
- Include negative impact deliberately. Carsten recommends either a third table or a separate column — avoiding it gives an incomplete picture.
- Use a virtual whiteboard or simple document — the medium doesn't matter, getting it out of your head does.

## Best Used When
- You're feeling uncertain about your value or contribution as an agile coach, scrum master, or facilitator.
- You're preparing for a coaching supervision session or a performance conversation.
- You want to deliberately choose where to invest your energy in the coming quarter.
- You're noticing that some relationships feel stuck or that someone might benefit from a different coach.

## Related Concepts
- **Imposter Syndrome** — Carsten links this reflection to earlier videos on imposter syndrome; knowing your impact is an antidote to self-doubt.
- **What? So What? Now What?** — A classic Liberating Structures debrief format, applied here as a personal reflection sequence.
- **Coaching Supervision** — The tool supports the kind of meta-reflection typically done in coaching supervision contexts.
- **Strengths-Based Coaching** — Framing impact in terms of what's working before looking at gaps aligns with appreciative inquiry principles.
