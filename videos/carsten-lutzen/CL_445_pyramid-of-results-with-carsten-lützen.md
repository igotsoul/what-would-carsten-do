# Pyramid of Results with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Pyramid of Results model from the book *Change the Culture, Change the Game*, applying it to team culture development. The core insight is that results are easy to see but hard to replicate — because they emerge from actions, which in turn are shaped by the underlying beliefs and experiences within a team. Carsten shares how intentionally designing experiences can shift beliefs and, over time, transform culture.

## Methods & Techniques

### Pyramid of Results (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A four-layer model showing how Results emerge from Actions, which are driven by Culture, which is rooted in Beliefs and Experiences.
- **When to use:** When reflecting on why a team's results are hard to reproduce, or when trying to intentionally shift team culture.
- **Group size:** Works as a coaching lens for any team size
- **Duration:** Conceptual model — can be introduced in 10–15 min as a facilitated discussion
- **How it works:**
  1. Start at the top: What Results does the team produce?
  2. Ask: What Actions lead to these results?
  3. Ask: What Culture enables or blocks these actions?
  4. Dig deeper: What Beliefs and Experiences underpin that culture?
  5. Identify which beliefs you want to change — then design experiences that foster new beliefs.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4VGRQxnc_DA&t=30

### Designing Experiences to Shift Beliefs (ab 02:00)
- **What:** Rather than documenting or mandating culture, a coach creates deliberate situations that give team members new experiences, which over time change their beliefs and thus the culture.
- **When to use:** When a team has a stuck or counterproductive belief (e.g., "feedback only comes when something goes wrong").
- **How it works:**
  1. Identify the belief you want to change.
  2. Design concrete situations that produce the opposite experience (e.g., introduce regular positive, constructive feedback).
  3. Repeat consistently — experience accumulation leads to belief change.
  4. Changed beliefs shift culture, which unlocks new actions and results.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4VGRQxnc_DA&t=120

## Key Principles
- "Methodologies are someone else's solution to someone else's problem at some other time" — results and actions can't simply be copied from another team.
- If you don't manage the culture, the culture will manage you.
- Writing down culture backfires: political players will comply on paper without living the values.
- Culture change happens at the level of beliefs and experiences — not rules or documentation.
- Transparency as an example: consistently making processes transparent (backlog, demos, Scrum Master work) gives people the *experience* that transparency helps, building the *belief* that it does.

## Facilitation Tips
- Don't try to codify culture in a document — it invites performative compliance rather than genuine adoption.
- Work at the belief level: ask the team what beliefs are currently present, then ask what experiences could shift them.
- Be patient — belief change through experience accumulation is a slow, consistent process.
- Design situations proactively rather than reacting to culture problems after the fact.

## Best Used When
- A team is struggling to replicate another team's success and wondering why copying practices doesn't work.
- You want to start a culture conversation without it becoming a political exercise.
- You're coaching a team through a feedback culture problem or a trust/transparency issue.
- You need a simple mental model to help a team understand *why* their results are what they are.

## Related Concepts
- Book: *Change the Culture, Change the Game* (Roger Connors & Tom Smith) — source of the Pyramid of Results model
- Political players as a culture indicator — people who comply visibly but don't internalize values
- Psychological safety — closely related to the belief/experience dynamic Carsten describes
- Feedback culture — used as a concrete example of belief-shifting through designed experiences
