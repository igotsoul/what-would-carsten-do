# "Gamestorm" Goal Setting with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten presents Gamestorm, a gamified long-term goal-setting workshop format for teams. Inspired by an article by Christiaan from The Liberators, the format combines Liberating Structures (Conversation Cafe, 1-2-4-All, Dot Voting) with a game-like treasure/coins mechanic to help teams define a high-level goal, break it into sub-goals, and generate both enabling actions (Dreamers) and risk-mitigating actions (Killers). The resulting action map is kept alive through an ongoing gamification scheme that tracks team progress over time.

## Methods & Techniques

### Gamestorm Goal-Setting Workshop (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A full-session workshop format that uses gamification (gold coins, themed team corners) and Liberating Structures to help a team set a long-term goal, break it into sub-goals, and identify actions that drive success or prevent failure.
- **When to use:** When a team needs to align on a longer-term direction and create a shared, motivating action plan. An alternative to formats like the Seamap or Treasure Map.
- **Group size:** Whole team, split into two sub-groups (Dreamers and Killers)
- **Duration:** Half-day or longer session (individual phases are 15–20 min each)
- **How it works:**
  1. **Room setup:** Move tables to the sides except one (for the canvas). Arrange a circle of chairs.
  2. **Conversation Cafe:** Facilitate a high-level discussion — "What is the overall goal we want to work towards?"
  3. **Goal agreement:** Use dot voting or discussion to agree on the single high-level goal.
  4. **1-2-4-All — Sub-goals:** Use 1-2-4-All to break the goal into three sub-goal headlines. Dot vote if more than three emerge.
  5. **Canvas setup:** Place the three sub-goals on the canvas. Add a pile of coins (gold plastic, chocolate, or paper) at a "treasure chest" representing the full value of achieving the goal.
  6. **Value allocation:** Team discusses and divides the coin pile across the three sub-goals according to relative importance.
  7. **Dreamers vs. Killers split:** Divide the team into two groups. Dreamers brainstorm actions to ensure each sub-goal is achieved. Killers brainstorm actions to ensure each sub-goal fails (i.e., risks and blockers to prevent).
  8. **Corner work (15–20 min):** Each group works in a themed corner (Killers: dark/edgy theme; Dreamers: hippie/optimistic theme). Can use 1-2-4-All internally for structure.
  9. **Switch and Dot Vote:** Groups swap corners. Killers vote on Dreamers' actions; Dreamers vote on Killers' actions.
  10. **Select top actions:** Aim for three Dreamer actions and three Killer actions per sub-goal.
  11. **Second coin allocation:** Divide coin piles across the selected actions — team discusses relative value of making each action happen (or not happen).
  12. **Ongoing gamification:** As actions are completed, remove them and redistribute their coins. Create a milestone reward scheme (e.g., every 20 coins = cake). Optionally show progress across multiple teams for fun, non-competitive comparison.

## Key Principles
- The coin mechanic makes abstract goal value tangible and forces prioritization conversations that might otherwise stay vague.
- Splitting into Dreamers and Killers surfaces both opportunities and risks in parallel, avoiding groupthink and creating more robust action plans.
- Cross-voting (Killers vote on Dreamers' actions and vice versa) adds perspective and legitimacy to the prioritization.
- Gamification sustains motivation over time — visible progress and small rewards help teams maintain focus on long-term goals between sprints.
- The format deliberately layers multiple Liberating Structures (Conversation Cafe, 1-2-4-All, Dot Voting) rather than inventing new facilitation mechanics from scratch.

## Facilitation Tips
- Theme the team corners deliberately — the physical environment nudges mindset (Killers' corner darker/edgier, Dreamers' corner lighter/optimistic).
- Use physical gold coins (plastic or chocolate) rather than abstract points — the tangibility makes value discussions more concrete and fun.
- Keep 15–20 minutes for the Dreamer/Killer corner work; use 1-2-4-All within each group if you want more structure.
- After the session, your role as Scrum Master or coach is to keep the canvas visible and help the team systematically tackle the identified actions.
- When running across multiple teams, resist framing progress as competition — but do allow the comparison to emerge naturally as a motivating element.

## Best Used When
- A team needs to set or re-align on a longer-term goal (quarter, PI, or beyond).
- You want an alternative to Seamap or Treasure Map that adds more gamification and structured risk-thinking.
- The team benefits from a playful, energizing format to make strategic planning feel less dry.
- You want to surface both enablers and blockers in the same session.
