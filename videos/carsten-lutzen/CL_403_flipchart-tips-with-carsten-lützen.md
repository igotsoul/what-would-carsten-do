# Flipchart Tips with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares his approach to using flipcharts in workshops, inspired by a technique from Ole Rich Henningsen. The core idea: distribute pre-prepared flipcharts around the room rather than stacking them on a single stand, creating movement, progress visibility, and better spatial engagement. Carsten also explains why he prefers flipcharts over PowerPoint for forcing content prioritization.

## Methods & Techniques

### Distribute Flipcharts Around the Room (ab 00:40)
- **What:** Instead of stacking all prepared flipcharts on one stand and revealing them sequentially, hang or place them around the room in the order you plan to use them — clockwise.
- **When to use:** Any workshop where you have pre-prepared flipchart content and a room with wall space.
- **Group size:** Especially effective with groups working at smaller tables distributed around the room.
- **Duration:** Applies to the full session setup, not a specific exercise.
- **How it works:**
  1. Prepare flipcharts upfront.
  2. Arrange them clockwise around the room in the order you expect to use them.
  3. Hang face-to-wall (hidden) or face-out with only the headline visible — depending on whether you want to preview the agenda or keep content hidden.
  4. Move to each flipchart as you reach that point in the session.
  5. Reserve one stand or corner for drawing new flipcharts live during the session.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=85QNvpuoAvM&t=40

### Visible Headlines as Agenda Preview (ab 01:30)
- **What:** When hanging flipcharts around the room, expose only the headline so participants get a sense of what's coming without revealing the full content.
- **When to use:** When you want a lightweight visual agenda embedded in the room setup itself.
- **How it works:** Hang flipcharts face-out but fold or position so only the top headline is visible.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=85QNvpuoAvM&t=90

### Flipchart as a Moving Focal Point (ab 02:10)
- **What:** If you have a flipchart stand on wheels, move it with you as you progress through the room, keeping the active content close to where you're facilitating.
- **When to use:** Larger rooms or when live drawing is needed alongside the distributed setup.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=85QNvpuoAvM&t=130

## Key Principles
- **Spatial distribution creates engagement:** Moving through the room rather than standing at a single point keeps energy up and suits groups working in sub-teams at different tables.
- **Progress is visible:** Participants can physically see the facilitator moving through the prepared flipcharts, giving a tangible sense of advancement through the session.
- **Flipcharts force prioritization:** Unlike PowerPoint (where creating 200 slides is easy), flipcharts impose a natural constraint — you only write what is truly important, resulting in fewer, more precise messages.
- **Illustrations beat bullet points:** Carsten values flipcharts for their ability to support visual storytelling and illustrations that reinforce the message.
- **Credit where due:** This room-distribution technique was introduced to Carsten by Ole Rich Henningsen.

## Facilitation Tips
- Prepare flipcharts before the session and arrange them in the sequence you plan to use them — clockwise helps you navigate intuitively.
- Keep at least one blank flipchart stand available for content that emerges during the session; position it in a corner out of the way.
- If your room doesn't allow wall-hanging, a wheeled stand that travels with you is a viable fallback.
- Hiding the full content but showing headlines gives participants orientation without spoiling the session flow.

## Best Used When
- Running full-day or multi-topic workshops where pre-prepared content drives the agenda.
- Working in rooms with tables arranged in clusters rather than theatre-style seating.
- You want participants to feel movement and progress rather than sitting passively in front of a screen.
- You are replacing or reducing PowerPoint dependency and want a forcing function for content clarity.
