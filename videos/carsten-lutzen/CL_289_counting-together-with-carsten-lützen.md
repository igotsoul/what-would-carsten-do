# "Counting Together" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a simple physical energizer he experienced as a participant in a retrospective, originally facilitated by someone named Kid. Pairs count to three in turns, then progressively replace each number with a physical action — until no words are spoken at all. It's quick, playful, and surprisingly demanding cognitively.

## Methods & Techniques

### Counting Together (ab 00:15)
- **What:** A pairs energizer where partners take turns counting to three, then progressively substitute each number with a physical action (stomping, clapping, double high-five) until the sequence is entirely non-verbal.
- **When to use:** As a warm-up or re-energizer at the start of a session or after a break — especially effective before activities requiring focus or coordination.
- **Group size:** Any even number; everyone works in pairs simultaneously.
- **Duration:** ~5 minutes.
- **How it works:**
  1. Pair up. Take turns saying "one, two, three" — each partner says alternating numbers.
  2. Once the rhythm is established, replace "three" with a physical action (e.g., stomp foot).
  3. Next, replace "two" with another action (e.g., clap).
  4. Finally, replace "one" with a third action (e.g., double high-five).
  5. Now the entire sequence is physical — no words, just figuring out whose turn it is and what to do.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JVIKEPLFznI&t=15

## Key Principles
- Progressive complexity keeps participants engaged — start simple, add challenge in small steps.
- Replacing language with physical action forces full presence and attention; you can't go on autopilot.
- Physical, playful exercises create energy and laughter quickly without requiring materials or setup.

## Facilitation Tips
- Let pairs get comfortable with the basic verbal count before introducing substitutions — rushing the progression kills the fun.
- Introduce substitutions one at a time, clearly demonstrating each before the group tries it.
- Works best in-person; the physical actions are central to the exercise.

## Best Used When
- Opening a workshop or retrospective and the group needs to wake up or connect physically.
- Transitioning after a long sitting/discussion phase and energy is low.
- You want a fast, no-materials energizer that still engages the brain.
