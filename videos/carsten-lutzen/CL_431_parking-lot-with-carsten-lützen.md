# Parking Lot with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Parking Lot, a simple flipchart template for capturing off-topic or premature discussion threads during workshops and meetings. It helps facilitators keep sessions on track without dismissing participant contributions. The card can be reused across sessions with minimal effort.

## Methods & Techniques

### Parking Lot (ab 00:10)
- **What:** A dedicated flipchart area where off-topic or not-yet-relevant discussion items are captured as keywords so they don't derail the session — but aren't lost either.
- **When to use:** In longer workshops or meetings where discussions tend to go off-track or into territory not useful for the current agenda.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** Ongoing throughout session; brief review slots built into agenda
- **How it works:**
  1. Set up a flipchart (or section of one) labeled "Parking Lot" at the start of the session.
  2. When a discussion thread goes off-track or isn't fruitful for all participants, capture the topic as keywords and place it on the Parking Lot.
  3. Build one or more "Parking Lot sessions" into the agenda to revisit parked items.
  4. At the end of the day, review what's on the lot: address anything still relevant, or acknowledge that it can be picked up another time.
  5. Because participants write on sticky notes (not on the flipchart itself), the template is reusable across sessions.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oLNVuJgWJyw&t=10

## Key Principles
- Parking items rather than shutting them down respects contributions while protecting session focus.
- Building Parking Lot review slots into the agenda signals that parked items are genuinely considered, not discarded.
- Reusability (writing on stickies, not the chart) lowers setup overhead and helps teams internalize the habit over time.

## Facilitation Tips
- Write only keywords, not full sentences — fast to capture, easy to scan later.
- Make the Parking Lot visible to everyone in the room so participants trust the process.
- Explicitly close out the Parking Lot at session end: either schedule follow-up or explicitly agree to drop the item.

## Best Used When
- Running full-day or multi-hour workshops where scope creep in discussions is likely.
- Working with groups new to structured facilitation who need a visible, tangible way to see their ideas aren't being ignored.
- Recurring workshops with the same group — reusability pays off quickly.
