# Coaching with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen reflects on what the word "coaching" actually means, contrasting the common misuse of the term in agile contexts with the ICF definition. The core message: coaching is about inspiring clients to find their own solutions — not giving answers. A short but clear conceptual grounding for anyone who calls themselves an Agile Coach.

## Methods & Techniques

### ICF Coaching Definition as a Compass (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Using the International Coach Federation (ICF) definition — "Partnering with clients in a thought-provoking and creative process that inspires their personal and professional potential" — as a guiding principle for coaching interactions.
- **When to use:** When clarifying the coaching role to a new coachee, or when reflecting on whether an interaction is truly coaching vs. consulting/advising.
- **Group size:** 1-on-1
- **Duration:** N/A — this is a mindset, not a timed exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Internalize the ICF definition as a filter for your coaching behavior.
  2. When a coachee expects solutions, gently redirect — your role is to help them explore perspectives and uncover their own answers.
  3. Treat the coachee as the expert on their own situation; the coach is a thinking partner, not a solution provider.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yezwPyFiGN8&t=30s

## Key Principles
- The "Coaching" part of "Agile Coach" is often forgotten — the label doesn't guarantee the practice.
- Coaching is not advice-giving or solution delivery. If the coachee expects you to tell them what to do, Carsten argues you are not the right person in that moment — or that you need to reset expectations.
- The coach's job is to help the coachee find *their own* solution, because no coach can know with certainty what will work for someone else's situation.
- "Leave your ego at the door" — the coachee is the expert on their own problem. The coach holds space and offers perspectives, not answers.

## Facilitation Tips
- Be explicit with new coachees about what coaching is and isn't — unmet expectations (expecting solutions) undermine the relationship from the start.
- Use the ICF definition as a quick self-check: *Am I inspiring potential, or am I just giving advice?*

## Best Used When
- Onboarding a new coachee who is unfamiliar with the coaching relationship
- Reflecting on your own practice as an agile coach or Scrum Master
- Explaining the difference between coaching and consulting to stakeholders or team members
- Study or discussion material for coach training or community of practice sessions
