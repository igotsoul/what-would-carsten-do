# Stealing Ideas from the Shower

## Summary
Carsten makes a case for "shower thoughts" — the ideas that surface when the mind is not actively focused — as an essential tool for creative problem solving in facilitation and knowledge work. He shares his personal practice of running without lyrics to create mental slack, and encourages normalizing unstructured thinking time during working hours.

## Methods & Techniques

### Diffuse Thinking via Physical Movement (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Occupying the body with low-effort physical activity (running, walking, dancing) to free the mind from directed thinking, allowing ideas to surface naturally.
- **When to use:** When stuck on a creative or design challenge — e.g., structuring a workshop, combining exercises, or solving a facilitation problem.
- **Group size:** Individual practice.
- **Duration:** A single run or walk; no fixed time required.
- **How it works:**
  1. Step away from active problem-solving.
  2. Engage in repetitive physical movement — Carsten uses running with instrumental-only music.
  3. Set no specific goal for what the mind should produce.
  4. Allow thoughts to wander freely — solutions may arrive for the current problem, a different problem, or nothing at all.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P_yJ3iagU0c&t=30

### Intentional Silence (ab 01:30)
- **What:** Sitting in silence with no task or goal, purely to allow the mind to wander.
- **When to use:** As an alternative to physical movement when a walk or run isn't practical.
- **How it works:** Remove all inputs and stimulation; make no demand of the mind. Let it go wherever it wants.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P_yJ3iagU0c&t=90

## Key Principles
- Creative problem solving requires slack — unscheduled mental space where no active thinking is demanded.
- The best ideas often arrive precisely when you stop trying to find them.
- This is not a guaranteed technique, but it has a high probability of generating useful insights.
- Physical movement and mental wandering are legitimate parts of knowledge work, not distractions from it.

## Facilitation Tips
- When designing workshops or stringing exercises together, Carsten deliberately uses runs as part of his design process — not just preparation.
- Avoid music with lyrics during movement-based thinking time; lyrics re-engage the language-processing brain and reduce mental wandering.
- Don't assign the mind a specific problem to solve during these sessions — open-ended wandering is more generative than directed incubation.

## Best Used When
- A facilitator or coach feels stuck on a design challenge or session structure.
- Creative energy is low and forced brainstorming isn't producing results.
- Teams or individuals doing knowledge work need permission to normalize thinking time outside of desk work.
