# Eye Contact with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a short icebreaker exercise he learned from coach Kati Hankovszky designed to break habitual seating patterns in recurring team sessions. The exercise uses silent eye contact to prompt people to swap places in a circle, ensuring fresh neighbor combinations before a retrospective or discussion begins.

## Methods & Techniques

### Eye Contact Swap (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Participants stand or sit in a circle. When two people make eye contact, they silently swap places — no words allowed.
- **When to use:** At the start of a retrospective or recurring team meeting where people tend to sit next to the same colleagues every time.
- **Group size:** Any size that fits in a circle
- **Duration:** 1–3 minutes (adjust to what feels right for the group)
- **How it works:**
  1. Arrange chairs in a circle, or have everyone stand in a circle.
  2. Explain the single rule: if you make eye contact with someone, you both switch places — silently.
  3. Run for 1–3 minutes continuously.
  4. Proceed with the session — people are now seated next to different colleagues.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nGc583QJPWs&t=30s

## Key Principles
- Recurring teams fall into habitual seating patterns that can reinforce groupthink or reduce cross-pollination of ideas.
- Nonverbal, playful interaction lowers the social barrier of "forcing" people to move.
- Simplicity is a feature — a single rule is enough to create noticeable group dynamics shift.
- Credit where it's due: Carsten attributes this exercise to coach Kati Hankovszky.

## Facilitation Tips
- Keep the rule strictly nonverbal — no talking during the swap phase reinforces presence and playfulness.
- Adjust the duration to your group's energy; shorter (1 min) for a quick nudge, longer (3 min) for a more thorough shuffle.
- Works with chairs in a circle or standing — standing makes it slightly more energizing.

## Best Used When
- Opening a retrospective with a team that has worked together long enough to have settled seating habits.
- You want to gently disrupt comfort zones without heavy facilitation overhead.
- You need a sub-5-minute energizer that requires zero materials.
