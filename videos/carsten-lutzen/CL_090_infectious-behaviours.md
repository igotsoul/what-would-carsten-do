# Infectious Behaviours

## Summary
Carsten Lützen explores how human behaviour spreads through imitation, drawing on insights from Dr. Leandro Herrero's concept of "viral change." He argues that change agents — coaches, leaders, managers — can deliberately shape organisational culture by being conscious of the stories and behaviours they model, with a particular emphasis on authenticity and narrative.

## Methods & Techniques

### Viral Change Through Storytelling (ab 01:00)
- **What:** Intentionally crafting and spreading positive stories and experiences within an organisation to shift culture and narrative, leveraging humans' natural tendency to copy behaviour.
- **When to use:** When driving cultural or behavioural change in a team or organisation; when negative narratives are dominating.
- **Group size:** Organisational scale — applies to teams, departments, or whole organisations.
- **Duration:** Ongoing practice, not a one-off intervention.
- **How it works:**
  1. Identify the current stories and narratives circulating in the organisation.
  2. Recognise that negative stories travel faster and farther than positive ones.
  3. Deliberately create new positive experiences — good meetings, good retrospectives, good interactions.
  4. Help people articulate and share those experiences as stories.
  5. Focus stories on learnings: what happened, how you got there, and what's in it for the listener.
  6. Model the behaviour yourself as a change agent or leader.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IdDHqEpbRDY&t=60

### Role Modelling as Change Mechanism (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Consciously embodying the behaviours and attitudes you want to see spread, based on the principle that humans — at all ages — copy the behaviour of those around them.
- **When to use:** Any change initiative where a coach, Scrum Master, or leader has visibility and influence.
- **How it works:** Be the change you want to see — not as a slogan, but as a deliberate strategy. If you want a certain behaviour to become infectious, demonstrate it consistently and authentically.

## Key Principles
- Humans never stop copying behaviour — it is how we develop as children and how we continue to operate as adults.
- Negative stories are more infectious than positive ones; they travel faster and farther in organisations.
- To change culture, you must change the stories being told — which means creating new experiences worth telling stories about.
- Authenticity is non-negotiable: presenting a "picture-perfect" narrative that hides struggle actively undermines trust and psychological safety.
- Sharing real learnings — including failures and what you did about them — is more empowering than projecting flawless success.
- Change agents are, by nature, in the "infection business" (Herrero's framing).

## Facilitation Tips
- When running retrospectives or workshops, be intentional about the experience you are creating — it will become a story people tell.
- Avoid projecting false positivity. Carsten is explicit: audiences can tell when dirt is being swept under the carpet, and it erodes credibility.
- Surface and highlight learnings explicitly, not just outcomes — this gives people a story with meaning they can retell.
- Model bravery by sharing what is actually happening, which gives others permission to do the same.

## Best Used When
- You are a Scrum Master, agile coach, or change agent trying to shift team or organisational culture.
- Negative narratives are dominant and spreading unchecked.
- You want to build psychological safety and encourage honest conversation.
- You are preparing for a change initiative and thinking about how to make new behaviours stick.
