# "Scaling Questions" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Scaling Question from the Solution-Focused Coaching Toolbox, a technique he learned and refined through his friend Jesper from Solution Surfers. The video covers the core mechanic of the question, why you don't need to define the low end of the scale, and three concrete variations: individual coaching, silent group exercises, and online retrospectives with a Product Owner.

## Methods & Techniques

### Scaling Question — Core Mechanic (ab 01:00)
- **What:** A coaching question that asks someone to rate their current situation on a scale of 1–10, with 10 defined as the ideal state, then uses the gap to surface what already works and what small changes would signal progress.
- **When to use:** 1-on-1 coaching, retrospectives, or any session where you want to help someone move from problem-focus to solution-focus.
- **Group size:** Individual to small group
- **Duration:** 15–30 min depending on depth
- **How it works:**
  1. Define what "10" looks like — use the coachee's own words to build a vivid picture of the ideal state. Do NOT define "1".
  2. Ask the coachee to place themselves on the scale: "Where do you see yourself?"
  3. Follow up: "What made it a [5] and not a [4]?" — this surfaces what is already working.
  4. Ask: "How would you recognize it became a 5.1?" — focus on signs of change, not actions yet.
  5. Only then: "How can we make sure these changes happen?" — narrow focus to that one small increment.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FiidVI8GIh8&t=60

### Silent Group Scaling (ab 03:30)
- **What:** A physical, silent variation of the Scaling Question for groups. Participants move to a position in the room representing their rating — no verbal sharing required.
- **When to use:** Groups where psychological safety is lower, or when you want to teach the Scaling Question format without personal exposure.
- **Group size:** Small to medium group (works in a room)
- **Duration:** 20–30 min
- **How it works:**
  1. Mark one end of the room as "1", the other as "10".
  2. Pose the topic generically (no personal details required).
  3. Ask participants to stand where they rate themselves — in silence.
  4. Ask reflection questions out loud; participants write answers privately.
  5. By the end, each person has a personal list of 3 actionable things — without having shared their problem with anyone.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FiidVI8GIh8&t=210

### Online Scaling Retrospective with Product Owner (ab 04:50)
- **What:** An async-style online variation using the chat function to collect ratings and reflections privately before revealing them all at once to the Product Owner.
- **When to use:** Remote retrospectives or input sessions feeding into a Product Owner; situations where you want unfiltered, uninfluenced input.
- **Group size:** Team + Product Owner
- **Duration:** 30–45 min
- **How it works:**
  1. Frame the scale: 10 = product on track, extra capacity, interstellar quality.
  2. Ask participants to type their rating in chat — but NOT send it yet.
  3. Ask them to type what is already working — still not sent.
  4. Ask for signs of change and possible actions — still not sent.
  5. All send at once. Product Owner reads everything, reflects first, then team pitches in.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FiidVI8GIh8&t=290

## Key Principles
- **Amplify what works** — Solution-Focused Coaching focuses on what is already going well, not on diagnosing problems.
- **Don't define the "1"** — Carsten used to define both ends of the scale, but learned from Jesper that defining the worst case is unnecessary and potentially harmful. The "10" is enough.
- **The follow-up question is the key** — "What made it a 5 and not a 4?" is the engine of the technique. It forces positive reflection before solution-finding begins.
- **Signs of change before actions** — Ask how someone would *recognize* improvement before asking what they will *do*. This grounds action in observable outcomes.
- **Small increments** — The question focuses on 0.1 steps, not giant leaps. This makes progress feel achievable.
- **Silence protects safety** — The silent group variation allows individuals to engage with a sensitive topic without public exposure.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use the coachee's own words when describing the "10" state — it makes the vision feel owned, not imposed.
- In the silent group variation, resist the urge to prompt verbal sharing. Let the silence do the work.
- In the online variation, have the Product Owner reflect before the team speaks — this avoids the PO anchoring the team's input.
- The technique is versatile: Carsten notes it can be used in retrospectives, 1-on-1s, input sessions, and group workshops with minimal adaptation.
- For further depth, explore "multiscaling" — multiple simultaneous scales on different dimensions.

## Best Used When
- A team or individual is stuck in problem-focus and needs to shift to solution-focus.
- You want structured retrospective input without vulnerability-heavy personal sharing.
- A Product Owner needs unfiltered, parallel team input before discussion.
- Psychological safety is moderate — the silent variation lowers the barrier to honest self-assessment.
