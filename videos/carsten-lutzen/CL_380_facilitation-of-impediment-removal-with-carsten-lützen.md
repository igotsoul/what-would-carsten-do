# "Facilitation of Impediment Removal" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten explores the distinction between removing impediments yourself as a Scrum Master versus facilitating the team to remove impediments on their own. He contrasts the Inside-Out perspective of a Scrum Master with the Outside-In view of an Agile Coach, and argues that a Scrum Master's deep team knowledge makes them powerful change agents — but only if they resist becoming a bottleneck by doing everything themselves.

## Methods & Techniques

### Facilitation of Impediment Removal (ab 01:30)
- **What:** Instead of the Scrum Master personally resolving every impediment, they facilitate the team and Product Owner to solve problems themselves — stepping in only when necessary.
- **When to use:** Whenever a Scrum Master risks becoming a single point of failure for problem-solving; especially in self-organizing teams where empowerment matters.
- **Group size:** Scrum Team (Development Team + Product Owner)
- **Duration:** Ongoing practice, not a single exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Identify whether you as Scrum Master are becoming the bottleneck for impediment resolution.
  2. Shift from solving to enabling — ask who else in the team could own this impediment.
  3. Extend your network to support the team without taking over.
  4. Allow the team to interact directly with stakeholders, users, and the Product Owner rather than routing everything through you.
  5. Reserve direct intervention for cases where facilitation genuinely isn't enough.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mzRYACuBYXc&t=90s

### Inside-Out vs. Outside-In Perspective (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A conceptual lens distinguishing the embedded Scrum Master (Inside-Out) from the external Agile Coach (Outside-In), each with different blind spots and strengths.
- **When to use:** When deciding who should lead an intervention or when an Agile Coach and Scrum Master are collaborating on the same team.
- **How it works:**
  - The Scrum Master is inside the team — they know individual dynamics and how to move people, but may be too close to spot systemic issues.
  - The Agile Coach is external — they can spot patterns the Scrum Master misses, but have less leverage to create change.
  - Used together, they complement each other: the Coach spots, the Scrum Master acts.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mzRYACuBYXc&t=30s

## Key Principles
- A Scrum Master who personally resolves all impediments becomes a bottleneck — the opposite of the intended effect.
- Empowering the team to solve their own impediments moves them closer to stakeholders, users, and the Product Owner — valuable beyond just solving the immediate problem.
- Agile Coaches should not underestimate the Scrum Master's advantage: intimate knowledge of the team gives the Scrum Master unique leverage to drive real change.
- The question isn't "should I remove this impediment?" but "should I facilitate its removal?"

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten recommends asking yourself: "Am I the bottleneck here?" as a regular self-check.
- Prefer facilitating impediment removal over doing it yourself — intervene directly only as an exception.
- Use your network to support team members who are solving impediments, rather than taking the problem off their plate entirely.

## Best Used When
- Coaching Scrum Masters who tend to over-function and carry too many problems themselves.
- Discussing the Scrum Master role in team retrospectives or coaching sessions.
- Clarifying the complementary relationship between a Scrum Master and an Agile Coach on the same team.
