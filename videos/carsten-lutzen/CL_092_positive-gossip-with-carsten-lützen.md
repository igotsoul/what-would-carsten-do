# "Positive Gossip" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen presents a Liberating Structures-inspired activity called Positive Gossip — a structured sequence of paired conversations designed to surface and share appreciative feedback within a group. The activity blends Impromptu Networking, What/So What/Now What, and Triads to help teams practice positive feedback and break negative conversational cycles. It is short, energising, and well-suited for groups that already have some psychological safety.

## Methods & Techniques

### Positive Gossip (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A multi-round paired conversation activity where participants share appreciative "gossip" — positive observations about colleagues' behaviors and contributions — before reflecting together in small groups.
- **When to use:** When a team needs to practice giving positive feedback, build appreciation habits, or interrupt a negative feedback culture. Best in groups where people feel somewhat safe already.
- **Group size:** Any group that can form pairs and then groups of four.
- **Duration:** Not explicitly stated; implied 20–35 minutes based on the number of rounds.
- **How it works:**
  1. **Round 1 — Pairs (ab 00:45):** Invite participants to form pairs, standing face to face. Prompt: "Share some positive gossip — positive words about a recent behavior or contribution from one of the *other* people in this group." Both partners share. Keep the next prompts hidden until each round begins.
  2. **Round 2 — New pairs (ab 01:30):** Form new pairs. Prompt: "Share some positive gossip about a person who recently guided or supported you, either professionally or privately." Ensure both partners share.
  3. **Round 3 — New pairs, direct appreciation (ab 02:00):** Form new pairs again. Prompt: "Share positive gossip about a behavior or contribution you have seen from *your current partner*." This shifts from talking about absent others to direct peer appreciation — Carsten notes this can feel nerve-wracking but is valuable practice.
  4. **Groups of four — What/So What/Now What (ab 02:30):** Merge pairs into groups of four. Facilitate three questions in sequence:
     - *What?* — What did you just experience? What stood out?
     - *So What?* — Why is this important? What did you pay attention to? What was valuable?
     - *Now What?* — Given what you heard, what is a good next step for you? How would that look?
  5. **Whole-group debrief (ab 03:15):** Brief open debrief — invite one or two sentiments from the room.

## Key Principles
- Withhold all prompts until each round starts — revealing questions up front would reduce spontaneity and the gradual shift from "gossip about others" to direct appreciation would lose its impact.
- The progression from anonymous (talking about someone not present) to direct (talking about the person in front of you) is intentional and creates a safe on-ramp to giving face-to-face positive feedback.
- Positive Gossip is framed as a practice — something to do repeatedly to build a habit of noticing and verbalising positive contributions.
- The activity is designed to counter negative conversational cycles, not just as a one-off exercise.

## Facilitation Tips
- Do not reveal Round 3's prompt early — Carsten emphasises this is "a bit of a kicker" that only lands if participants don't anticipate it.
- Only run Round 3 (direct appreciation to partner) in groups where psychological safety already exists to some degree.
- Keep the debrief at the end light — one or two sentiments from the crowd is enough; the paired conversations carry the weight.
- Model the tone by using the word "gossip" playfully to lower the stakes of giving feedback.

## Best Used When
- A team rarely gives each other explicit positive feedback and needs a structured nudge.
- You want a short, energising opener or closer that generates genuine connection.
- Running a retrospective or team session where building appreciation is a goal.
- The group has sufficient psychological safety for direct peer-to-peer appreciation.

## Related Concepts
- **Impromptu Networking** (Liberating Structures) — same paired, rotating structure used in Rounds 1–3.
- **What/So What/Now What** (Liberating Structures) — used verbatim in the group-of-four reflection phase.
- **Triads** (Liberating Structures) — cited by Carsten as an inspiration for the third round's direct appreciation prompt.
- **Positive feedback practice** — framed as a muscle to train, not a one-time event.
