# My 4 Step Retrospective

## Summary

Carsten Lützen shares his go-to retrospective format consisting of four sequential steps that balance positive reflection with actionable improvement. The format emphasizes starting positively, following up on previous commitments, exploring the iteration broadly, and then focusing on concrete next steps — without jumping into solution mode.

## The 4 Steps

### Step 1: Achievement Posters (ab 00:18)

- **What:** Team members individually and silently reflect on what they achieved and are proud of from the last sprint/iteration. Achievements are captured on a poster in categories.
- **Categories Carsten uses:** Product (features delivered), Knowledge (what was learned), Social (team bonding activities), Other (catch-all).
- **Why it works:** Starts the retro on a positive note. Over time, the collection of posters becomes a motivating visual history — you can look back a year and see what the team was proud of.
- **Key insight:** If the "Social" category is consistently empty, that's a signal to check on team wellbeing.
- **Format:** Individual + silent reflection, then sharing.
- **Duration:** ~5 minutes
- **Source:** [00:18](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=18)

### Step 2: Follow-Up (ab 01:30)

- **What:** Review the focus points from the previous retrospective. The "driver" (person responsible for tracking, not doing all the work) gives an update on progress.
- **Purpose:** Creates accountability and shows the team that retro outcomes actually lead to action.
- **Decision:** Team decides whether to close the item, carry on, or deprioritize it.
- **Key insight:** The driver is NOT the person who does all the work — they just ensure something happens and report back.
- **Duration:** ~5 minutes
- **Source:** [01:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=90)

### Step 3: Retro Wheel / Modified Starfish (ab 02:05)

- **What:** A modified starfish exercise with six categories instead of five: Start, Stop, Continue, More, Less, and Appreciative Feedback.
- **Process:** 1-2-4-All format — first individual silent reflection (~1 min), then plenary discussion. Then dot-voting to identify where the energy is.
- **The six categories:**
  - **Start:** Things the team should begin doing
  - **Stop:** Things that don't make sense anymore
  - **Continue:** Things that are working well (positive reinforcement)
  - **More:** Things to increase (e.g., knowledge sharing, team huddles)
  - **Less:** Things to decrease (not stop, just reduce)
  - **Appreciative Feedback:** Non-sarcastic, non-ironic appreciation for a specific person in the meeting (not the facilitator)
- **Facilitator tip:** Track trends across retros — items often move from "Start" → "Continue" → eventually "Stop". This is valuable pattern data.
- **Key insight on Appreciative Feedback:** Carsten introduces it as "non-sarcastic, non-ironic appreciative feedback to a person in this meeting and it cannot be me" — this framing closes most avoidance behaviors, especially with engineers.
- **Duration:** ~30 minutes (bulk of the retro)
- **Source:** [02:05](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=125)

### Step 4: Focus Points / Improvement Points (ab 03:34)

- **What:** Top-voted items from Step 3 become focus points. For each, the team assigns a driver and identifies "signs of positive improvement."
- **The "Signs" technique:** Instead of jumping into solutions, the team imagines the problem is already solved and brainstorms: "How would you know? What would be different?" This generates signs like "when we come to the meeting and there's an agenda and everyone is on point."
- **Why this works:** Avoids premature solution mode. The driver can later review the signs and develop better solutions than what would emerge from a time-pressured retro.
- **Process:** 3-4 minutes individual silent brainstorming on signs, then sharing.
- **Duration:** ~15 minutes
- **Source:** [03:34](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=214)

## Total Duration

~55-60 minutes (5 + 5 + 30 + 15)

## Key Principles

- **Start positive:** Achievement posters set an appreciative tone
- **Follow up:** Retro outcomes must lead to visible action
- **Silent reflection first:** Every step starts with individual, silent thinking before group discussion
- **No premature solutions:** Focus on signs of improvement, not fixes
- **Driver ≠ Doer:** The driver ensures progress, not single-handedly delivers it
- **Appreciative feedback as standard practice:** Builds psychological safety through structured recognition

## Best Used When

- Regular sprint/iteration retrospectives
- Teams that need more structure in their retros
- Teams where retros feel unproductive or too negative
- Teams that struggle with follow-through on retro action items
