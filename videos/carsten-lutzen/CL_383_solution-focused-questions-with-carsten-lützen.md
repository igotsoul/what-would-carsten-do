# Solution Focused Questions with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces Solution-Focused Questions as a mindset shift away from problem analysis toward outcome recognition. Instead of asking "how do we solve this?", you first ask "how will we know we've solved it?" — and then backtrack to identify the actions that lead there. Carsten describes this as an eye-opener that changed how he works as both a Scrum Master and Agile Coach.

## Methods & Techniques

### Solution-Focused Questioning (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A questioning approach that starts with defining what success looks like — the signs of a good solution — before identifying actions. You backtrack from the desired outcome rather than forward-planning from the problem.
- **When to use:** Coaching conversations, retrospectives, product backlog refinement, or any situation where a team risks solving the wrong problem.
- **Group size:** Works 1-on-1 and in group settings
- **Duration:** Can be applied as a brief framing exercise or as the backbone of a full coaching conversation
- **How it works:**
  1. Identify a goal or situation you want to improve
  2. Ask: *What are the signs that something has changed?*
  3. Ask: *How will I recognize that we've hit the target?* (not: how do we hit it)
  4. Once success is visible and concrete, backtrack: *What actions led up to that?*
  5. Define and prioritize only those actions — not everything that seemed relevant upfront
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IJc7TpxopBQ&t=40

## Key Principles
- Focus on **outcomes, not solutions** — clarity about what "done" looks like prevents solving the wrong problem
- **Recognition before action** — you can only plan effective actions once you know what success looks and feels like
- Backtracking from a desired future state surfaces the *right* actions, not just the obvious ones
- Applicable across contexts: coaching, retros, backlog work — the same question structure transfers broadly

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten explicitly distinguishes "how do we hit the target?" (premature) from "how will we recognize we hit the target?" (productive) — hold this distinction firmly when facilitating
- Use the "What's better?" question as a companion technique (Carsten references a separate video on this)
- Encourage people to sit with the recognition question before jumping to solutions — the pause is the point

## Best Used When
- A team or individual is jumping straight to solutions without clarity on what success means
- Retrospectives where the team keeps taking actions that don't stick
- Coaching conversations where the coachee is stuck in problem analysis
- Product backlog discussions where scope creep or misaligned priorities are recurring

## Related Concepts
- Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (SFBT) — the source discipline behind this questioning approach
