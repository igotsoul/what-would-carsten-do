# 3O's with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten makes the case for Scrum Masters running regular one-on-ones with individual team members. He shares his approach to these conversations — covering both personal wellbeing and professional development — and explains how the insights gained help him facilitate and coach the team more effectively.

## Methods & Techniques

### One-on-Ones for Scrum Masters (ab 00:20)
- **What:** Regular individual conversations between the Scrum Master and each team member, covering personal wellbeing and professional growth.
- **When to use:** Ongoing practice, especially useful when team mood is hard to read, individuals seem disengaged, or the Scrum Master wants to proactively support team health.
- **Group size:** 1-on-1
- **Duration:** Not specified; implied to be a recurring short conversation
- **How it works:**
  1. Create a safe, confidential space — explicitly commit that nothing shared leaves the room without permission.
  2. Check in on personal life: Is anything at home causing stress? Do they need to vent?
  3. Explore professional development: Are they working on assignments they enjoy? Is there training they want?
  4. Even without budget or formal mandate, offer hints on who to approach to make things happen.
  5. Use what you learn to tailor facilitation, retrospective design, and coaching to individual aspirations.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v0JygY1k7kA&t=20

### Open Door Policy (ab 02:40)
- **What:** Always being available when a team member comes to you with a problem — treating that conversation as the highest priority in the moment.
- **When to use:** Any time a team member initiates; should be a standing commitment, not a scheduled slot.
- **How it works:** When someone comes to you, drop everything else. That problem is the most important thing right now. Never postpone.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v0JygY1k7kA&t=160

## Key Principles
- The Scrum Master's lack of formal power is an asset: people share things with you precisely *because* you have no mandate over them.
- Confidentiality is non-negotiable — explicitly promise that nothing leaves the room unless agreed, and keep that promise.
- Team mood at the aggregate level can mask individual struggles; one-on-ones surface what group dynamics hide.
- Knowing individual aspirations lets you shape facilitation and retrospectives to help people grow in the direction they want.
- There is no "I" in team, but individual conversations are how you keep the team healthy.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use insights from one-on-ones to inform retrospective design — tailor exercises to what individuals are working through.
- Carsten frames one-on-ones as energizing for himself as well, not just a service to the team — treat them as a learning opportunity.
- Never let a team member's request for a conversation wait; immediacy signals that you genuinely prioritize them.

## Best Used When
- A Scrum Master wants to move beyond surface-level team dynamics and understand what is really going on.
- Team members seem hesitant to raise issues in group settings.
- You want to proactively support individual development without formal HR authority.
- The team mood appears fine but something feels off.

## Related Concepts
- Psychological safety (creating conditions where individuals feel safe to speak honestly)
- Servant leadership (Scrum Master as someone who removes obstacles for individuals, not just the team)
- Retrospectives (informed and improved by individual insights gathered in 1-on-1s)
