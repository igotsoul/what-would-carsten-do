# "Appreciative Postcards" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares "Appreciative Postcards," a retrospective closing activity where team members write peer appreciation notes that are then distributed during the sprint. The exercise extends the positive energy of a retro into everyday sprint life while also giving Scrum Masters useful insight into team dynamics.

## Methods & Techniques

### Appreciative Postcards (ab 00:20)
- **What:** Team members write appreciative notes to each other at the end of a retrospective, which the Scrum Master then hands out during the sprint — keeping the retro alive and spreading positive feedback over time.
- **When to use:** As a closing activity for a retrospective, especially when the team needs a morale boost or when psychological safety around positive feedback is still developing.
- **Group size:** Any team size
- **Duration:** 5–10 min during the retro; distribution happens throughout the sprint
- **How it works:**
  1. At the end of the retrospective, hand out small pieces of paper or postcards (nothing fancy required).
  2. Each team member writes one or more appreciative postcards to other team members — something that made them happy in the sprint, something the person brings to the team, or any positive recognition they want to share.
  3. Depending on team maturity, encourage people to sign the postcards.
  4. Collect all postcards at the end of the retro.
  5. During the sprint, hand them out at natural moments — at standup, after lunch, etc.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jcAJuI-nIdw&t=20s

## Key Principles
- Extending a retrospective's positive energy into the sprint itself keeps the team mindset alive beyond the ceremony.
- Peer-to-peer recognition is powerful — Carsten notes how visibly people "light up" when receiving a postcard.
- The format works even with minimal materials; simplicity lowers the barrier to participation.
- Signing postcards is a maturity signal — encourage it when the team is ready for transparent appreciation.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use postcard distribution as a diagnostic tool: if one team member receives no postcards, investigate why. If one person receives all of them, that's also worth reflecting on — both patterns reveal team dynamics.
- Choose casual, organic moments for distribution (standup, after lunch) rather than formal ones to maximize the feel-good effect.
- The exercise is sourced from Retromat — a useful reference for discovering similar activities.

## Best Used When
- Closing a retrospective and wanting to leave the team on a positive note.
- The team needs reinforcement of positive behaviors and peer recognition.
- You want to carry retrospective energy into the sprint without adding more ceremonies.
- You're looking for a lightweight diagnostic on team relationships and social dynamics.
