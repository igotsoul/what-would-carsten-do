# If you were me... with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a single, deceptively simple coaching question he learned from Daniel Meier and Jesper: "If you were me, what question would you like me to ask you?" This technique helps coaches navigate moments of uncertainty in a session by giving the coachee agency to surface what's really on their mind.

## Methods & Techniques

### "If You Were Me" Question (ab 0:40)
- **What:** A meta-coaching question that invites the coachee to name the question they wish the coach would ask — then the coach simply asks it.
- **When to use:** When the coach is unsure which direction to take the conversation, or suspects the coachee is holding back something important.
- **Group size:** 1-on-1 coaching
- **Duration:** A single question; integrates into any coaching session
- **How it works:**
  1. Ask: *"If you were me, what question would you like me to ask you?"*
  2. Wait and let the coachee respond.
  3. Parrot the question back: *"OK, so what is [topic they named]?"*
  4. Let the coachee speak to what was weighing on them.
  5. Follow up with classic coaching questions focused on learning and outcomes.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CX_DRsA61O8&t=40s

## Key Principles
- Coachees often know exactly what question they need — they're just waiting for it to be asked.
- Giving the coachee agency over the direction of the conversation is itself a coaching intervention.
- Parroting back the coachee's own framing keeps the session in their language and their frame, not the coach's.
- The question may feel awkward or "stupid" the first time — ask it anyway and let silence do the work.

## Facilitation Tips
- Don't rush after asking — wait and let the coachee sit with the question.
- After they name the question they want, parrot it back verbatim rather than rephrasing it.
- Use follow-up questions to anchor the conversation on learning and concrete outcomes.

## Best Used When
- A coaching conversation has lost momentum or direction.
- You sense the coachee is circling around something but hasn't named it.
- You want to shift agency to the coachee without losing the thread of the session.
