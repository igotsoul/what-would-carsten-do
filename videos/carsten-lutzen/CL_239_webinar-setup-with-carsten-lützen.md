# Webinar Setup with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares his personal technical setup for running live-drawing webinars and online presentations. The video explains how he combines an iPad, a note-taking app, and a screen mirroring tool to draw slides live during sessions — giving presentations a dynamic, hand-crafted feel.

## Methods & Techniques

### Live-Drawing Webinar Setup (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A hardware and software combination that allows the facilitator to draw slides live on an iPad and share them in real time during an online meeting or webinar.
- **When to use:** When you want to create engaging, in-the-moment visual presentations rather than static slide decks — particularly useful for agile coaching, workshop facilitation, and teaching.
- **Group size:** Any (works for webinars and online meetings of all sizes)
- **Duration:** Setup is permanent/reusable; not time-bound
- **How it works:**
  1. Use a tablet (Carsten uses an iPad with Apple Pencil) running a note-taking app with presentation mode (Carsten uses GoodNotes).
  2. Pre-draw some slides in advance; leave space to draw others live during the session.
  3. Connect the iPad to a laptop (Mac or PC) using a screen mirroring app — Carsten uses **Reflector**, which supports AirPlay and Chromecast.
  4. In the online meeting (e.g., Zoom, Teams), share the mirrored iPad window as your screen.
  5. Draw and annotate live during the presentation — participants see the drawing in real time.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HGIhe38TN24&t=30

## Key Principles
- Pre-cooking some content is fine, but leaving room to draw live keeps presentations dynamic and responsive.
- The setup is intentionally flexible: any tablet, any note-taking app, Mac or PC — the specific tools matter less than the workflow.
- Simplicity and reliability matter: Carsten uses Reflector simply because "it works" and he has used it for years.

## Facilitation Tips
- GoodNotes' **presentation mode** hides the editor UI so participants only see the "paper" — cleaner and less distracting.
- Zoom has built-in iPad mirroring; for other platforms, a dedicated mirroring app like Reflector bridges the gap.
- Android tablets with Chromecast can substitute for iPad/AirPlay if needed.

## Best Used When
- You want to facilitate online sessions with a visual, hand-drawn aesthetic instead of polished slide decks.
- You are an agile coach, Scrum Master, or facilitator who draws models and frameworks during explanations.
- You want to adapt your visuals in real time based on participant responses.
