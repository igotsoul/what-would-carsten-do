# "Optimums" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the concept of local vs. global optimums as a mental model for understanding team performance plateaus. Drawing on heuristics from computer science — specifically taboo search — he argues that teams stuck in a local optimum may need to intentionally try things that make things worse short-term in order to discover a better state. The video is a conceptual framing with implications for how Scrum Masters and coaches think about team improvement over time.

## Methods & Techniques

### Taboo Search as Team Improvement Strategy (ab 01:30)
- **What:** A heuristic borrowed from computer science where you deliberately try actions you know will produce a temporarily worse result, in order to escape a local optimum and discover a better one.
- **When to use:** When a team is performing well but seems to have plateaued — no obvious next improvement steps are visible, and things are starting to feel stale.
- **Group size:** Team level
- **Duration:** Ongoing experiment cycle; no fixed time
- **How it works:**
  1. Recognize the team is in a local optimum (performing but not improving)
  2. Identify something to deliberately break down or stop doing — even if it currently "works"
  3. Observe what happens when you rebuild or replace it
  4. Use the experience to surface what actually matters to the team and its environment
  5. Climb back up to a new — potentially better — local optimum
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=R6-CvBUr-Fc&t=90

### Local Optimum Mapping (ab 00:45)
- **What:** Using the metaphor of hilltops to diagnose where a team currently stands relative to its potential — are they stuck on a small hill when a bigger one is reachable?
- **When to use:** During retrospectives, coaching conversations, or team health checks when discussing growth trajectory.
- **Group size:** Team level or 1-on-1 coaching
- **Duration:** Discussion/reflection; 15–30 min depending on depth
- **How it works:**
  1. Introduce the local vs. global optimum concept (hilltop metaphor)
  2. Ask the team to reflect: "Are we stuck? Do we feel like we've run out of improvements?"
  3. Explore what a "descent" (intentional disruption) might look like — what could you stop, change, or break?
  4. Frame the journey as learning, not failure

## Key Principles
- A team in a local optimum has no obvious small-step path to improvement — that's the defining feature of being on a hilltop.
- The global optimum is a unicorn: useful as a direction, not a destination. The real goal is finding a *better* local optimum.
- Staying still is not neutral — teams that don't actively seek new optimums risk slowly degrading without noticing.
- Intentional descent (trying something worse on purpose) is a legitimate and creative improvement strategy, not a failure.
- Psychological safety is a prerequisite for this approach: teams need to feel safe experimenting with things that might not work.
- The Tuckman model's "performing" stage is the entry point to this challenge — performing teams still need somewhere to go.

## Facilitation Tips
- Explain *why* you're doing something counterintuitive before asking the team to try it — "we're doing this because it can help us find an even better place."
- Frame taboo search experiments as playful and creative, not risky or worrying.
- Small experiments first: stopping stand-ups, changing a ceremony format, dropping a practice — see what the absence reveals.
- Acknowledge that descent is uncomfortable; normalize it as part of the journey.
- Use the hilltop metaphor visually if possible — it makes the abstract concept concrete quickly.

## Best Used When
- A team is performing well but improvement ideas have dried up
- Retrospectives feel repetitive or produce no new action items
- A Scrum Master or coach suspects the team is slowly stagnating without realizing it
- You want to introduce the idea of deliberate experimentation in a team's process
- Psychological safety is already reasonably established (this concept requires it)

## Related Concepts
- **Tuckman Model** — Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing; the "performing" stage is where local optimum thinking becomes relevant
- **Taboo Search** — Heuristic algorithm from computer science; explores worse-performing neighbors to escape local optima
- **Psychological Safety** — Prerequisite for the team to feel safe trying things that might not work
- **Kaizen / Continuous Improvement** — Related philosophy, though Carsten's framing adds the nuance that improvement sometimes requires intentional regression
- **Experimentation Mindset** — Treating process changes as hypotheses to test, not commitments to keep
