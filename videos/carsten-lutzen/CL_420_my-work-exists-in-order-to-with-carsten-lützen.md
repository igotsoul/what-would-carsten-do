# My work exists in order to with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares his personal answer to the Liberating Structures prompt "My work exists in order to..." — a reflective exercise he encountered at a workshop with The Liberators. He explains his own purpose as a Scrum Master and Agile Coach, and invites viewers to formulate their own answer. The video is primarily a personal reflection rather than a technique walkthrough.

## Methods & Techniques

### "My work exists in order to..." (ab 00:15)
- **What:** A Liberating Structures prompt that invites participants to articulate the deeper purpose behind their role — not just what they do, but why the job exists at all.
- **When to use:** Team workshops, coaching sessions, or personal reflection — especially useful when a team or individual has lost sight of purpose or is going through a transition.
- **Group size:** Works individually or in groups of any size.
- **Duration:** Can be a brief standalone reflection (5–10 min) or the seed for a longer conversation.
- **How it works:** Each person completes the sentence "My work exists in order to..." and shares their answer. The prompt forces specificity — not just a job description, but the outcome or impact the role is meant to create.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=44EWhGX8X4s&t=15

## Key Principles
- Carsten's own answer: *"My work exists in order to make teams happy and inspire them to work in new ways to deliver value."*
- Happy, energized teams are more likely to deliver world-class products and experiences.
- Technical excellence and process excellence are interdependent — you cannot have one without the other.
- Inspiring teams to try something new — whether technical or process-related — moves them one step closer to learning and delivering more value.

## Facilitation Tips
- The prompt works because it forces people to move beyond job titles and task lists into purpose and impact.
- Carsten encountered this in a Liberating Structures workshop run by The Liberators (Barry and Christiaan) — worth attributing the origin if using it with a group.

## Best Used When
- Kick-off sessions where a team needs to align on purpose.
- Coaching conversations where someone is unclear about the value of their role.
- Retrospectives or team health checks focused on motivation and meaning.
- Onboarding new Scrum Masters or agile coaches who are defining their identity in the role.
