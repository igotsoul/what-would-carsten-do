# Mad Tea Party with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces "Mad Tea Party," an unofficial Liberating Structure that functions as a scalable icebreaker. Participants form inner and outer circles facing each other, answer prepared questions in pairs, then rotate — making it suitable for large groups where full engagement is essential.

## Methods & Techniques

### Mad Tea Party (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A paired conversation icebreaker where participants form two concentric circles facing each other, answer a timed question together, then the outer circle rotates to a new partner.
- **When to use:** Event or workshop openings; especially effective with large crowds where typical icebreakers fall flat.
- **Group size:** Scales to very large groups (any even number)
- **Duration:** Depends on number of questions and time per question; each round is 30 seconds to 1 minute
- **How it works:**
  1. Split participants into two equal halves — one forms an inner circle facing outward, the other an outer circle facing inward, so every person is looking at a partner.
  2. Prepare a series of questions in advance.
  3. Show the first question; pairs discuss and answer in turns (~1 minute per question, or 30 seconds each depending on question depth).
  4. When time is up, the outer circle moves two places, giving High-Fives to the person they pass but do not stop at.
  5. Show the next question and repeat.
- **Variation:** Use the "36 Questions to Fall in Love" as the question set for a playful and surprisingly connecting experience with large groups.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Bqcifo0GwtQ

## Key Principles
- Scales exceptionally well — suitable for crowds where most icebreakers don't work
- Keeps everyone engaged simultaneously; no waiting, no passive observers
- Question depth determines timing — shallow questions warrant 30 seconds, deeper questions up to a minute or more
- The High-Five during rotation adds a playful, energetic transition between rounds

## Facilitation Tips
- Prepare questions before the event — the quality of questions drives the quality of connection
- Adjust round time to match question depth; don't default to one duration for all questions
- The "36 Questions to Fall in Love" set is a bold and memorable variation worth considering for the right group and context

## Best Used When
- Opening a large event or conference where participants don't know each other
- You need a quick, high-energy warm-up that still creates genuine connection
- The group is too large for traditional icebreaker formats
- You want full simultaneous engagement with no dead time
