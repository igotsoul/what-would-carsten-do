# "Spot the Elephant" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces "Spot the Elephant," a retrospective exercise sourced from Retromat that anonymously gauges the team's emotional temperature before a retro begins. Participants secretly choose one of four symbolic cards that represent different team states, giving the facilitator early signal about what the retrospective should focus on — and whether the original plan needs to change.

## Methods & Techniques

### Spot the Elephant (ab 00:45)
- **What:** An anonymous card-based check-in that reveals hidden tensions, hurt feelings, disengagement, or unspoken conflicts before the retrospective gets underway.
- **When to use:** With newly formed teams still finding their shared values, or with teams where something is quietly brewing under the surface but hasn't been named yet. Also useful as a general team temperature read.
- **Group size:** Any team size (one card per participant)
- **Duration:** ~5–10 min (check-in phase); may reshape the full retro
- **How it works:**
  1. Prepare four physical cards for each participant — a boot crushing a flower, a sun, a moon, and an elephant.
  2. Explain the exercise and the meaning of each card (see Key Principles).
  3. Pass around an opaque envelope; each person anonymously places one card inside.
  4. Collect the remaining three cards per person in a separate pile.
  5. **Shuffle the played cards** before revealing them to prevent counting who played what.
  6. Lay all played cards face-up on the table so the entire team can see — observe team reactions as you do this.
  7. Use the result to decide whether to continue with your planned retro or pivot to a different facilitation approach.

## Key Principles

- **Four card meanings:**
  - **Elephant** — There is an unaddressed issue in the room. The "dangerous" card; signals that something needs to be surfaced, either in this retro or a dedicated follow-up session booked shortly after.
  - **Boot crushing a flower** — Someone had their feelings hurt last iteration. Points to psychological safety concerns or discomfort within the team.
  - **Moon (indifferent)** — "Everything is meh." Signals disengagement or low motivation; the retro should pivot toward re-energizing the team.
  - **Sun** — Everything is going well; continue as-is.
- **Anonymity is non-negotiable.** Shuffle the played cards before revealing them. Do not peek in advance. The envelope must be opaque. Privacy protects honesty.
- **No witch hunt.** If someone plays an elephant, do not try to identify who or assume you know why. The card is information, not an accusation.
- **The reveal is team input, not just facilitator input.** The moment cards go on the table, the team is also reading the room together. That reaction is valuable data.

## Facilitation Tips

- Carsten stresses preparing **alternative facilitation strategies in advance** — before the retro starts, not after you see the result. Know what you'll do if an elephant appears, if feelings were hurt, or if the team is disengaged.
- If an elephant card is played, either address it within the current retrospective or explicitly commit to a dedicated follow-up session soon after. Don't ignore it.
- Observe the team's nonverbal reactions as you lay the cards on the table — their body language gives additional signal beyond the cards themselves.
- The exercise works best when introduced clearly and calmly, framing it as a safe, judgment-free temperature check rather than a diagnostic or evaluation.

## Best Used When

- A team is newly formed and still establishing norms and psychological safety.
- You sense something unspoken is affecting team dynamics but it hasn't surfaced yet.
- You want a low-risk way to invite people to signal discomfort before diving into a standard retro format.
- You need a quick, anonymous pulse check that can also inform your facilitation strategy on the fly.

## Related Concepts

- **Retromat** — The source of this exercise; a tool for discovering and combining retrospective activities.
- **Psychological Safety** — The boot/flower card directly signals safety concerns; the exercise as a whole depends on creating enough safety for honest participation.
- **Facilitation Flexibility** — The exercise is explicitly designed to trigger a plan change, connecting to the broader facilitator skill of reading the room and adapting in the moment.
