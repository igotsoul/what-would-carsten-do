# "Coloring" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares his practical experience coloring flip chart banners and headers using three different tools: jumbo markers, crayons, and chalk-based pastels. Over 4–5 years of producing flip charts, he has developed clear preferences based on speed, messiness, and visual quality. The video gives facilitators concrete guidance on which tool to choose depending on their situation.

## Methods & Techniques

### Jumbo Markers / Pins for Coloring (ab 00:45)
- **What:** Using large jumbo-sized office markers to fill banner areas on flip charts with solid, vibrant color.
- **When to use:** When you want the most visually vibrant result and have enough time; good for smaller areas.
- **Group size:** N/A (solo prep activity)
- **Duration:** Slow — takes considerable time for larger surfaces
- **How it works:** Apply the marker evenly across the banner area. Requires precision to get an even fill.
- **Pros:** Vibrant, visually striking color; widely available in most offices.
- **Cons:** Time-consuming for large areas; risk of ink bleed-through despite "bleed resistant" labeling on flip chart paper.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=80cvxcvgA-s&t=45

### Crayons for Coloring (ab 01:30)
- **What:** Using standard children's wax crayons (the same ones kids use) to quickly fill banner areas on flip charts.
- **When to use:** Default go-to method — when speed matters and you want a clean, non-messy process.
- **Group size:** N/A (solo prep activity)
- **Duration:** Fast — large areas can be covered quickly
- **How it works:** Apply the crayon sideways or flat across the banner area. Leaves a slight texture on the paper that is usually invisible from a distance.
- **Pros:** Fast, not messy, widely available (even children's crayon sets work), produces decent color quality for banners.
- **Cons:** Colors less vibrant than markers; texture visible up close.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=80cvxcvgA-s&t=90

### Chalk-Based Pastels for Coloring (ab 03:00)
- **What:** Using chalk-based pastel sticks combined with a sponge or paper tissue to blend and fill large areas.
- **When to use:** When you have time and want artistic effects — gradients, sky/grass backgrounds, or more advanced visual compositions.
- **Group size:** N/A (solo prep activity)
- **Duration:** Fast application, but messy cleanup required
- **How it works:** Apply the pastel chalk directly to the paper, then blend and even out using a sponge or paper tissue. Work within defined borders carefully, as the tissue tends to spread chalk outside the intended area.
- **Pros:** Enables gradients and advanced effects; fast coverage of large areas; good for artistic backgrounds.
- **Cons:** Messy — chalk dust on hands, tissue, floor, and surrounding surfaces.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=80cvxcvgA-s&t=180

## Key Principles
- Carsten's personal rule of thumb: crayons 90% of the time (fast, not messy), chalk pastels for more artistic work, and jumbo markers rarely for large surfaces due to time cost and bleed risk.
- "Bleed resistant" labeling on flip chart paper is not always reliable — some markers apply so much ink that bleed-through happens anyway.
- The texture left by crayons is often imperceptible from normal viewing distance, making it a non-issue in practice.
- Tool choice should reflect the situation: available time, desired aesthetic, and tolerance for mess.

## Facilitation Tips
- Keep a set of basic children's crayons in your facilitation kit — they are cheap, lightweight, and outperform expensive alternatives for everyday flip chart work.
- When using chalk pastels near a defined boundary (e.g., a banner box), work inward from the border rather than outward to reduce spillover.
- For quick nature-themed backgrounds (green grass, blue sky), chalk pastels with a paper tissue wipe are significantly faster than any other method.

## Best Used When
- Preparing visual flip charts, banners, or posters for workshops, retrospectives, or training sessions.
- A facilitator wants to improve the visual quality of handmade materials without investing in expensive tools or spending excessive prep time.
