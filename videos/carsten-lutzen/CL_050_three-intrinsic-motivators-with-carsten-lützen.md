# Three Intrinsic Motivators

## Summary
Carsten Lützen introduces Dan Pink's intrinsic motivation model from the book *Drive*, focusing on three core drivers: purpose, mastery, and autonomy. The video explains how these three elements interact, what happens when only some are present, and how coaches and facilitators can actively help team members experience all three to reach a state of genuine drive and engagement.

## Methods & Techniques

### Dan Pink's Drive Model — Purpose, Mastery, Autonomy (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A three-part model of intrinsic motivation where people experience genuine drive when they perceive purpose in their work, feel they are mastering relevant skills, and have autonomy over how they work.
- **When to use:** When diagnosing low engagement, designing workshops, coaching individuals or teams, or reflecting on team health.
- **Group size:** Any — works for individuals, pairs, or full teams.
- **Duration:** Flexible — can be a short reflection exercise or a deeper team conversation.
- **How it works:**
  1. **Purpose** — Does this person perceive their work as meaningful to *them* (not just the company)? Does it create a sense of fulfillment?
  2. **Mastery** — Does this person *perceive themselves* as skilled and growing? The key is self-perception, not external assessment — the drive must come from inside.
  3. **Autonomy** — Does this person feel they have real choice in how they work and solve problems, or is it dictated by others?
  4. Explore the overlapping zones: Purpose + Mastery without Autonomy = **Discipline** work. Autonomy + Mastery without Purpose = **Dedication** work. Purpose + Autonomy without Mastery = **Desired** work (you do it because you can't stop, even though you don't feel expert yet). All three together = **Drive**.

### Name It and Tame It — Team Reflection Exercise (ab 03:30)
- **What:** A facilitated conversation where team members put words to *when* they feel each of the three motivators, making implicit feelings explicit and actionable.
- **When to use:** Team retrospectives, coaching conversations, or team health checks where engagement or motivation is a theme.
- **Group size:** Small teams; can be done in pairs as interviews.
- **Duration:** Short — can be run as a pair interview exercise in 15–30 minutes.
- **How it works:**
  1. Introduce the three motivators (purpose, mastery, autonomy).
  2. Have team members interview each other in pairs: "When do you feel this?" and "When do you feel drive?"
  3. Surface patterns and discuss as a team.

## Key Principles
- Intrinsic motivation is about **self-perception**, not external judgment — what matters is whether the individual *feels* they have mastery and autonomy, regardless of what others think.
- The drive must come from inside; external declarations ("you are empowered") are insufficient without the person actually feeling it.
- The three motivators are not binary — they overlap and combine to create different engagement states (discipline, dedication, desired work, drive).
- Carsten connects this model to the Management 3.0 concept of happiness and to other Western motivation frameworks, noting significant overlap.
- As a coach or facilitator, your role is not to fix motivation by decree, but to create conditions where people genuinely experience all three.

## Facilitation Tips
- Don't just tell people they are empowered — help them *feel* it. Saying "you have autonomy" is not the same as someone experiencing autonomy.
- Use pair interviews as a low-barrier entry point: have team members ask each other when they feel purpose, mastery, and autonomy in their daily work.
- Name the zones (discipline, dedication, desired, drive) to give teams a shared vocabulary for discussing their experience.
- This model works well alongside other motivation or happiness frameworks — Carsten suggests combining it with the Management 3.0 Moving Motivators or similar tools.

## Best Used When
- A team seems disengaged, low-energy, or going through the motions.
- You are designing a retrospective or team health workshop and want to surface motivation themes.
- Coaching an individual or team on ownership, empowerment, or growth.
- Introducing managers or Scrum Masters to the concept of intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation.
