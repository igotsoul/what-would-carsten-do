# "What? So What? Now What?" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Liberating Structure "What? So What? Now What?" — a three-question debrief format for making sense of a shared experience. Using progressive rounds of individual reflection, pair discussion, and group conversation, it surfaces insights and translates them into concrete next steps. It is short, reusable, and inclusive.

## Methods & Techniques

### What? So What? Now What? (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A structured debrief format built on three sequential questions: What did we experience? So what does it mean? Now what should we do? Each question is processed through the 1-2-4-All Liberating Structure.
- **When to use:** Immediately after a workshop, session, or any shared experience where the group needs to make sense of what happened and decide on next steps.
- **Group size:** Small groups of 5–7 people; not larger.
- **Duration:** Not specified exactly, but implied to be fast — Carsten calls it "a rather fast way to get all this information from everybody in the room."
- **How it works:**
  1. Split participants into small groups of 5–7.
  2. **Question 1 — "What?"**: Ask "What did we just experience / see?" Start individually in silence, then share in pairs, then groups of four, then the full group.
  3. **Question 2 — "So what?"**: Ask "What does this mean?" Use the same 1-2-4-All progression to give everyone time to form their own opinion before sharing.
  4. **Question 3 — "Now what?"**: Ask "What should we do with what emerged?" Focus on common observations, patterns, and a-ha moments. Capture outputs. Same 1-2-4-All progression.
  5. At the full-group stage, share learnings, outcomes, and key factors of success.

### 1-2-4-All (embedded structure) (ab 00:45)
- **What:** A Liberating Structure used inside "What? So What? Now What?" to progressively scale individual thinking into group discussion — solo → pairs → groups of four → full room.
- **When to use:** Any time you want to ensure every voice is heard before converging on shared meaning.
- **Group size:** Works for any room size.
- **Duration:** Varies; used here as the scaffolding for each of the three debrief questions.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iBQqUlWQ24c&t=45

## Key Principles
- Always move from individual reflection to small group to large group — this ensures personal thought before group influence.
- Each question builds on the previous: shared facts (What) → shared meaning (So What) → shared action (Now What).
- Capturing patterns and a-ha moments at the "Now What?" stage turns reflection into actionable outcomes.
- The format is reusable across different types of shared experiences — not just workshops.

## Facilitation Tips
- Start every question individually and in silence before opening to pairs or groups — this protects divergent thinking.
- Focus the "Now What?" round specifically on common observations and patterns across the room, not individual takeaways.
- Capture outputs explicitly during the "Now What?" phase so insights are not lost after the session.

## Best Used When
- Closing a workshop, training, or retrospective and needing a structured way to surface collective meaning.
- The group has had a significant shared experience and needs to process it together before leaving.
- You want a fast, inclusive debrief that works even with larger rooms.
