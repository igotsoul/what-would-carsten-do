# "Quiet Time" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a small but impactful facilitation insight inspired by a conference presentation: reframing silent reflection time so that thinking about nothing is the primary option, and focusing on the topic is secondary. This subtle shift makes silence feel legitimate for everyone — especially introverts — rather than pressure-laden reflection time.

## Methods & Techniques

### Quiet Time Reframe (ab 01:10)
- **What:** Instead of instructing participants to "reflect in silence on X," you invite them to "just sit in silence — you can either think about nothing, or think about X." This makes doing nothing the default, and working on the topic optional.
- **When to use:** At the start of any individual reflection phase, particularly in structures like 1-2-4-All or any workshop opening where you want people to arrive mentally before diving into content.
- **Group size:** Any size
- **Duration:** Typically 1–2 minutes, consistent with standard silent reflection phases
- **How it works:**
  1. Begin the individual/silent phase as usual.
  2. Instead of saying "reflect on [topic]," say: "Just sit in silence. You can either think about nothing, or you can think about [topic]."
  3. The primary invitation is silence and mental rest — the topic becomes a secondary option once participants feel ready.

## Key Principles
- Silence has intrinsic value in meetings and workshops — it is not just a vehicle for task-focused reflection.
- Bastian Ovako (referenced by Carsten) argues that 10% of meeting time should be spent in silence, actively and intentionally.
- Making "thinking about nothing" a legitimate choice reduces cognitive pressure and helps participants — especially introverts — arrive mentally before engaging with content.
- Small changes in how you phrase an instruction can significantly shift the psychological experience for participants.

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten suggests applying this reframe to 1-2-4-All and similar structures whenever an individual silent phase is included.
- Use the phrasing "you can either think about nothing, or [topic]" — the order matters. Nothing comes first.
- This is especially valuable for more introverted participants or when a group has been in back-to-back meetings and needs to decompress before engaging.

## Best Used When
- Opening a workshop or meeting where participants may be mentally scattered or fatigued.
- Facilitating 1-2-4-All or other Liberating Structures with a silent individual phase.
- Working with groups that include introverted participants or those who struggle with "on-demand" reflection.
- Any context where psychological safety and mental arrival matter before diving into content.

## Related Concepts
