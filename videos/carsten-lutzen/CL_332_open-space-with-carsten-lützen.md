# "Open Space" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces Open Space Technology as a participatory format for workshops and meetings where participants themselves set the agenda around topics they genuinely want to explore. The format enables self-organization at scale and is highlighted as a powerful Liberating Structure. The video covers how participants propose and cluster topics, then self-select into sessions based on their interests.

## Methods & Techniques

### Open Space Technology (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A large-group facilitation format where participants co-create the agenda by proposing topics they care about, then self-organize into working sessions around those topics.
- **When to use:** When you want high participation and ownership over the agenda — useful for conferences, team offsites, community gatherings, or any situation where the group's collective intelligence should drive the conversation.
- **Group size:** Scales widely — from small groups to very large gatherings (potentially hundreds of participants).
- **Duration:** Can range from a few hours to multiple days depending on scope.
- **How it works:**
  1. Participants are invited to propose any topic they want to discuss or explore.
  2. Proposed topics are clustered and arranged on a visible agenda/marketplace board.
  3. Participants self-select which sessions to attend — guided by the "Law of Two Feet": if you're not learning or contributing, move to another session.
  4. Sessions run in parallel; outputs are captured and shared with the whole group.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ONVYV_S_FQY

### Topic Clustering (ab 02:30)
- **What:** After participants propose topics, similar or related topics are clustered together to reduce duplication and surface thematic threads.
- **When to use:** During the agenda-building phase of an Open Space session.
- **How it works:** Topics written by participants are posted visibly; the facilitator or participants group related items, creating a consolidated agenda with meaningful clusters.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ONVYV_S_FQY&t=150

## Key Principles
- Open Space works because it puts agenda authority in the hands of the people who will actually do the work — not the organizer.
- Self-selection ensures people are in the room where they can contribute most; no one is stuck in an irrelevant session.
- The format is connected to Liberating Structures, emphasizing participatory design over top-down facilitation.
- The format can run both in-person and online.

## Facilitation Tips
- Create a safe, visible space (physical or digital) where topics can be posted and seen by everyone.
- Encourage all participants to propose topics — not just the loudest voices.
- Make the clustering step collaborative where possible so participants feel ownership over the agenda.
- Keep the opening brief: explain the format, the law of two feet, and then get out of the way.

## Best Used When
- You have a group with diverse interests or expertise and want to surface what matters most to them.
- A pre-set agenda would feel too constraining or top-down.
- You want high energy, engagement, and shared ownership of outputs.
- Running an unconference, community event, team kick-off, or large retrospective.
