# "Impediment Bashing" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces "Impediment Bashing," a visual technique for Scrum Masters and agile coaches to make their impediment-removal work visible to their teams. Using a simple three-column board, the approach creates transparency around the coach's work and builds team trust by showing that raised issues are actively being addressed.

## Methods & Techniques

### Impediment Bashing Board (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A personal kanban-style board with three columns — "To Bash", "Bashing", and "Bashed" — used by a Scrum Master or agile coach to track impediments they are actively working on removing.
- **When to use:** Ongoing practice for any Scrum Master or coach supporting one or more teams, especially useful when the coach's work feels invisible to the team.
- **Group size:** Personal tool for the coach; visible to the whole team.
- **Duration:** Continuous; pruned periodically.
- **How it works:**
  1. Anyone (team members or the coach) adds impediments to the **To Bash** column — anything on the radar that needs addressing.
  2. The coach picks items and moves them to **Bashing** as active work begins. This may involve direct action, pushing back to the team, or influencing stakeholders outside the team.
  3. Once the team or the person who raised the issue agrees the impediment is resolved, it moves to **Bashed**.
  4. The Bashed column grows over time, giving the coach a sense of completion and demonstrating progress to the team. Prune it periodically to keep it manageable.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kcRGitFXSyg&t=30

## Key Principles
- Not every impediment is the coach's to solve — some belong to the team; the coach's role is to identify what sits within their circle of influence.
- Visibility of the coach's work builds trust: the team can see that items placed on the board will be acted upon.
- A growing "Bashed" column provides a tangible sense of accomplishment for the coach and demonstrates value to the team.
- Inspired by personal kanban thinking (Carsten credits Gahn as a key influence).

## Facilitation Tips
- Place the board somewhere visible to the team — physically in the office if co-located, or on a virtual whiteboard for remote/hybrid teams.
- Invite the team to add to the "To Bash" column themselves; this increases buy-in and surfaces issues the coach might not yet be aware of.
- Before archiving a bashed item, confirm with the reporter that the impediment is genuinely resolved.
- Prune the Bashed column periodically to prevent clutter while preserving the motivational effect of visible progress.

## Best Used When
- A Scrum Master or agile coach wants to make their behind-the-scenes work transparent to the team.
- Teams are skeptical about what the coach actually does or whether raised issues are followed up on.
- The coach supports multiple teams and needs a personal system to track outstanding impediments across contexts.

## Related Concepts
- Personal Kanban (the direct inspiration for this technique)
- Impediment backlog (common Scrum practice this builds upon)
- Circle of influence (Covey) — distinguishing what the coach can act on vs. influence vs. neither
