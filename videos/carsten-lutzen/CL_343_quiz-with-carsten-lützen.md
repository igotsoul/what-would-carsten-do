# "Quiz" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares how he uses short quizzes (around 10 questions) as energizers or icebreakers to accelerate team forming. Whether joining a new team or kicking off a new product, a well-crafted quiz creates moments of laughter and personal connection that go beyond professional interactions.

## Methods & Techniques

### Team Quiz as Energizer / Icebreaker (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A short quiz of ~10 questions used at the start of a team session to help people get to know each other or a product — fast, playful, and low-prep.
- **When to use:** New team forming, onboarding, team kick-offs, or any session that benefits from a warm-up with connection.
- **Group size:** Any team size (works in-person and remote via chat)
- **Duration:** 5–10 minutes
- **How it works:**
  1. Prepare ~10 questions on a theme (see variants below)
  2. Present questions one by one via slides or a quiz tool
  3. Participants write answers individually or in the chat — **don't send until the facilitator says "go"** to keep answers unbiased
  4. Reveal answers, discuss, laugh together
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qFRoY2ixLes&t=20

### "Get to Know Me" Quiz — Facilitator-About-Themselves Variant (ab 00:35)
- **What:** The facilitator builds a quiz about themselves, including personal and fun facts, to model vulnerability and give the team a fuller picture of who they're working with.
- **When to use:** When joining a new team as Scrum Master or coach — helps break the professional distance from day one.
- **How it works:** Prepare ~10 questions about yourself, mixing fun facts with things that aren't visible in day-to-day work. The winner of the quiz then prepares an energizer for the *next* session — introducing themselves in the same format. This creates a chain of connection moments across sessions.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qFRoY2ixLes&t=35

### Product / Company History Quiz (ab 01:30)
- **What:** A quiz about the product or company history, used when a new team starts on an existing product.
- **When to use:** New team taking over or joining an existing product; useful for building shared context alongside team connection.
- **How it works:** Don't just pull from the company history page — find quirky, surprising, or funny facts. Look for unusual angles on what has happened. The goal is to make people think *and* laugh, not just recite facts.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qFRoY2ixLes&t=90

## Key Principles
- ~10 questions is the sweet spot — enough to have substance, short enough to stay energizing
- Personal content (fun facts, non-work sides of people) accelerates team forming more than purely professional content
- The "winner prepares next energizer" mechanic distributes ownership and keeps the practice going without the facilitator always driving it
- Quirkiness and humor are features, not accidents — deliberately look for surprising angles

## Facilitation Tips
- **Chat answer timing:** When running remote, ask participants to type their answer but *not send it* until you say "go" — this keeps everyone honest and prevents anchoring on others' answers
- **Low-tech is fine:** A simple PowerPoint or Keynote slideshow works just as well as a dedicated quiz tool (Kahoot etc.)
- **Make it personal:** Include things about yourself that aren't visible in professional contexts — this signals that it's safe for others to do the same

## Best Used When
- Kicking off work with a new team
- Starting a session that needs energy and connection, not just information transfer
- Onboarding a new team member to an existing group
- Building a "getting to know each other" habit across multiple sessions (chain mechanic)
