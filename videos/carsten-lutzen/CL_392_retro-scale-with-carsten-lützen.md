# Retro Scale with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces a scaling exercise for retrospectives inspired by the OSKAR coaching model. Teams rate themselves on a 0–10 scale, discuss their ratings, then identify small incremental steps (baby steps) to improve by just 0.1. The format combines individual reflection with structured dialogue and draws on the Liberating Structures concept of 15% Solutions.

## Methods & Techniques

### Retro Scale Exercise (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A solution-focused retrospective format where team members individually rate the team on a 0–10 scale, share and discuss their ratings, then collaboratively identify the smallest possible improvement step.
- **When to use:** When a team wants to assess overall team health or dive into a specific area of collaboration (e.g., backlog refinement, meeting quality). Easily adaptable to different focus areas.
- **Group size:** Team-sized (standard Scrum team)
- **Duration:** Not specified; likely 30–60 min depending on depth of dialogue
- **How it works:**
  1. Define the scale: 0 = "We are not a team, we only work together because we have to" — 10 = "We are the best team ever, High-Performance Team doesn't even cover it."
  2. Team members reflect individually and silently, writing their rating on a Post-It.
  3. Each person presents their rating and explains their reasoning (e.g., "I give us a 4 because our meeting preparation needs work").
  4. Shift focus: How do we get to 4.1, 5.1, or 7.1? — not a big leap, just a baby step.
  5. Individuals reflect again and prepare concrete small actions ("To get to 5.1, we need to start doing X and Y").
  6. Open constructive dialogue around the identified actions; allow additional rounds if new ideas emerge.
  7. Define recognition criteria: "A sign that we are at 4.1 would be that we no longer spend planning time on X."
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B4nDrD2HOcs&t=30

### 15% Solutions (ab 02:10)
- **What:** A prompt asking what actions the team can take within their own control — without needing extra money or resources — to make the next small improvement step.
- **When to use:** After ratings are shared, to focus the team on agency and realism rather than wishful thinking.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** Built into the exercise above
- **How it works:** After the rating round, explicitly frame the improvement question with "What can you do without extra resources or budget?" to keep actions concrete and immediately actionable.

## Key Principles
- The goal is incremental improvement — not a leap from 4 to 8, but from 4 to 4.1. Small, safe-to-try steps.
- Individual silent reflection before group discussion protects diverse perspectives and prevents anchoring.
- Defining what "better" looks like (recognition criteria) makes improvement tangible and verifiable.
- The format is adaptable: use it for overall team health or zoom into specific collaboration areas.
- Rooted in the OSKAR coaching model (Origin, Scale, Know-how, Affirm & Action, Review) — this retro format operationalizes the "Scale" step in a team setting.

## Facilitation Tips
- Define the scale anchors clearly and vividly before asking for ratings — the contrast between 0 and 10 helps people calibrate.
- After the first sharing round, redirect energy immediately toward improvement: "How do we get to X.1?" — this keeps the retro forward-looking.
- Take multiple rounds if needed; allow space for "I just had a brilliant idea" moments — Carsten explicitly welcomes these.
- Use the recognition-criteria question as a closing anchor: "What would we notice if we had actually improved?"

## Best Used When
- The team feels stuck or wants a structured way to talk about team effectiveness without blame.
- You want a coaching-style retro that builds on strengths rather than dwelling on problems.
- The team needs a concrete, low-risk action commitment at the end of the session.
- You are already familiar with OSKAR or solution-focused approaches and want to introduce them in a team format.

## Related Concepts
- **OSKAR Model** — Solution-focused coaching framework by Roy Marriott; this retro format adapts the Scale step for team use. Carsten has a separate video on OSKAR linked in the description.
- **15% Solutions** — Liberating Structures practice focused on actions within one's own sphere of control.
- **Solution-Focused Retrospectives** — Broader family of retro formats that emphasize strengths and future possibilities over root-cause analysis of problems.
