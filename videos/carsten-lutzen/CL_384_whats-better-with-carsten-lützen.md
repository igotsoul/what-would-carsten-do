# "What's Better" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces a single, deceptively simple question from Solution-Focused coaching: "What's better since last time?" Used at the start of a retrospective or coaching session, it shifts the group's attention toward what is already working, creating positive energy before diving into improvement work. The technique requires no preparation and pairs naturally with 1-2-4-All for group settings.

## Methods & Techniques

### "What's Better?" Opening Question (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A single Solution-Focused question — "What's better since last time / last retrospective?" — used to open a meeting, retro, or coaching conversation with a strengths-based lens.
- **When to use:** Start of a retrospective, beginning of a coaching session, or as a warm-up for any regular team meeting.
- **Group size:** Works for 1-on-1 coaching and larger groups alike.
- **Duration:** 5–10 minutes depending on group size and follow-up depth.
- **How it works:**
  1. Gather the group and pose the question: "Since our last retrospective, what's better?"
  2. Give people quiet time to reflect individually.
  3. In group settings, use 1-2-4-All so every voice gets space to form an opinion before sharing.
  4. Follow up by amplifying: "How can we do more of that? How can we make it even greater?"

## Key Principles
- The question is deliberately simple — its power comes from the positive framing, not complexity.
- Solution-Focused coaching consistently redirects attention from problems to progress; this question is one of its most reusable tools.
- Starting with "what's better" primes the group toward a constructive, energized mood before tackling what's not working.
- The natural follow-up is amplification: identify what's improving and then ask how to accelerate it — not just document it.

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten recommends pairing with **1-2-4-All** in group settings to ensure quieter participants form their own view before group influence sets in.
- Resist the urge to complicate the question. The simplicity is the feature, not a limitation.
- The question works across contexts: retros, coaching conversations, or even informal check-ins at the start of a meeting.
- After collecting "what's better" answers, explicitly bridge to amplification questions rather than letting the moment pass unused.

## Best Used When
- Opening a retrospective and wanting to avoid starting in a deficit/complaint mindset.
- A team needs an energy reset or has been stuck in a problem-focused pattern.
- Running a coaching session and wanting to surface client-side progress before exploring challenges.
- Time is short and a structured warm-up is not feasible — this question needs zero materials.

## Related Concepts
- **Solution-Focused Coaching** — the broader methodology this question comes from; emphasizes resources, progress, and desired futures over problem analysis.
- **1-2-4-All** (Liberating Structures) — recommended by Carsten as the group format to pair with this question.
- **Appreciative Inquiry** — related strengths-based tradition; shares the principle of starting from what works.
- **Amplifying Questions** — the natural follow-up family: "How can we do more of this? What would make it even better?"
