# "Stress" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen, agile coach and Scrum Master, shares a personal and practical approach to recognizing and responding to stress. The core message is that each person has unique stress symptoms, and the key skill is learning to recognize your own signals early enough to act on them. Carsten also points to root-cause tools and psychological safety as enablers for getting help.

## Methods & Techniques

### Know Your Own Stress Symptoms (ab 00:40)
- **What:** Identify the personal behavioral signals that indicate you are under stress, before the stress becomes overwhelming.
- **When to use:** As an ongoing self-awareness practice and as a coaching conversation starter with individuals experiencing burnout risk.
- **Group size:** Individual or 1-on-1 coaching
- **Duration:** Reflective exercise; can be done in a few minutes or explored deeper over multiple sessions
- **How it works:**
  1. Think back to a previous stressful period in your work life.
  2. Compare your behaviors then versus when you were not stressed.
  3. Identify the specific signals — behavioral, emotional, or physical — that showed up.
  4. Write them down and train yourself to notice them in real time.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0KzkllGuKpQ&t=40s

### Nine Whys for Root Cause (ab 02:50)
- **What:** Use the Liberating Structures "Nine Whys" exercise to dig into the underlying causes of stress-driven behavior.
- **When to use:** Once a stress symptom has been identified and the person wants to understand what they are avoiding or running from.
- **Group size:** Can be done with a friend, coach, or trusted colleague
- **Duration:** Not specified; typically 20–30 minutes in the Liberating Structures format
- **How it works:** Ask "why" repeatedly (up to nine times) to move from surface behavior to the root cause driving the stress response.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0KzkllGuKpQ&t=170s

## Key Principles
- Stress symptoms are personal — what signals stress for one person may not for another; self-knowledge is the foundation.
- Catching a symptom early is half the battle; the other half is asking "what am I avoiding or running away from?"
- Behaviors like procrastination, comfort eating, or needing silence can all be stress signals worth examining non-judgmentally.
- Carsten emphasizes acting on the signal once recognized rather than waiting — the longer the delay, the harder it becomes.
- Psychological safety is a prerequisite for asking for help from a manager; without it, people must rely on coaches or trusted peers instead.

## Facilitation Tips
- When coaching someone around stress, help them first identify their personal symptom list before jumping to solutions.
- Carsten suggests using a reflective question like "what behaviors changed during your last stressful period?" as an entry point.
- Nine Whys (Liberating Structures) is specifically recommended for surfacing root causes once a symptom is caught.
- Normalize the conversation: frame stress symptoms as information, not weakness.

## Best Used When
- Coaching an individual who is showing early signs of burnout or overload.
- Facilitating a team conversation about wellbeing and psychological safety.
- Preparing a workshop segment on self-awareness or resilience.
- Manager or Scrum Master conversations about sustainable pace.
