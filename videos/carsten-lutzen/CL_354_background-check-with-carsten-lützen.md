# "Background Check" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a quick online icebreaker called "Background Check" designed for remote teams. Participants change something in their background or appearance during a break, then the group tries to spot what changed — a playful way to spark curiosity and connection in virtual settings.

## Methods & Techniques

### Background Check (ab 00:20)
- **What:** An online icebreaker where participants secretly change one thing about their background or appearance during a break, then the group discusses what changed when they return.
- **When to use:** At the start of a remote workshop or meeting, or after a break, to re-energize participants and build curiosity about each other's home environments.
- **Group size:** Any size (works per-participant round-robin)
- **Duration:** Length of a regular break + a few minutes per participant for reveal
- **How it works:**
  1. Before the break, ask everyone to turn off their camera.
  2. During the break, each participant changes one thing — swap a shirt, move a plant, add or remove an item from the background.
  3. When the meeting resumes, go through each participant and have the group spot and discuss what changed.
  4. Continue with the planned workshop agenda.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Iu6NYS9t3MA&t=20

## Key Principles
- Remote work gives us an unusual window into colleagues' private spaces — this exercise turns that into a connection opportunity rather than an awkward backdrop.
- Curiosity about each other's environment builds rapport and psychological safety without requiring deep personal disclosure.
- Simple constraints (change exactly one thing) create a focused, low-stakes game that everyone can participate in.

## Facilitation Tips
- Keep the instruction simple: "change exactly one thing" — participants can decide how subtle or obvious to make it.
- Encourage variety: changes can be to clothing, objects, lighting, or background arrangement.
- The discussion after is where the real value lies — invite brief stories about the items or space, not just "I see you moved the plant."

## Best Used When
- Opening a remote workshop or after a mid-session break when energy is low.
- Teams that are newly distributed or haven't met in person.
- Any virtual meeting where you want a light, non-threatening way to humanize the remote experience.
