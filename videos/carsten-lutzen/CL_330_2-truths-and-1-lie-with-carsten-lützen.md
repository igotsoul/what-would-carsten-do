# "2 Truths and 1 Lie" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the classic icebreaker "2 Truths and 1 Lie" as a quick energizer for groups. Participants prepare two true statements and one lie about themselves, share them with the group, and let others guess the lie. The exercise helps people get to know each other better in a fun, low-stakes way — and can be stretched across an entire day as a running thread through breaks.

## Methods & Techniques

### 2 Truths and 1 Lie (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Each participant prepares two true statements and one lie about themselves. The group discusses and votes on which statement is the lie before the person reveals the answer.
- **When to use:** At the start of a workshop or team event to warm up the group and create connection; also works as a day-long engagement thread during breaks.
- **Group size:** Works for any group size; adapt by going around the table or room.
- **Duration:** A few minutes per person; short enough to run for a full group in one go, or distribute across the day.
- **How it works:**
  1. Ask participants to prepare in advance: two truths and one lie about themselves (professional or personal).
  2. Each person shares their three statements — verbally, on a flip chart, or in a slide.
  3. Give the group 1–2 minutes to discuss: which statement is the lie?
  4. Group votes on their guess.
  5. The person reveals the lie and optionally shares a brief story or detail about the truths.
  6. Repeat for each participant, or spread across the day (see variation below).
- **Variation — Day-long detective format:** Post everyone's three statements on a flip chart in the room. Participants investigate during breaks, sparking organic conversations. Reveal answers at the end of the day.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TSGHtmdMZTg

## Key Principles
- Prepare in advance: asking participants to think ahead lets them choose what they're comfortable sharing — keeping it psychologically safe.
- The choice between professional and personal content is left to the individual, reducing pressure.
- The "detective" variation extends the energizer's value beyond a single moment by fueling break conversations throughout the day.
- Simple formats that prompt genuine curiosity create openings for real connection.

## Facilitation Tips
- Give clear preparation instructions before the session so participants arrive with their three statements ready.
- Decide upfront whether participants should also share a brief story — this significantly affects timing.
- The day-long flip chart variation works especially well for full-day workshops where you want to build energy across multiple breaks, not just the opener.
- Encourage storytelling after the reveal — the lie and the truths are conversation starters, not just a game.

## Best Used When
- Kicking off a workshop, training, or team event where participants don't know each other well (or want to deepen existing connections).
- You need a short, no-materials energizer that still generates genuine engagement.
- You want a recurring thread throughout a full-day session rather than a one-shot opener.
