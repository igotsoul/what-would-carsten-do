# "Tall Plank Game" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen introduces a physical energizer called the Tall Plank Game, designed to get participants moving and energized during in-person sessions. It requires no materials, takes only one to two minutes, and creates a fun, competitive atmosphere through a simple plank-position hand-tapping challenge between pairs.

## Methods & Techniques

### Tall Plank Game (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A paired physical energizer where participants get into a tall plank position and compete to tap each other's hands, scoring a point for each successful tap.
- **When to use:** At the start of a session or as a mid-session energy boost when participants need to get active and re-engaged.
- **Group size:** Any even number; works in pairs.
- **Duration:** 1–2 minutes maximum — Carsten emphasizes keeping it short to avoid tiring people out.
- **How it works:**
  1. Ask everyone to pair up.
  2. Both partners get into a tall plank (push-up position, arms extended).
  3. Facing each other, each person tries to tap the other's hand to score a point.
  4. Participants naturally move around and adapt their strategy — the effort level is self-directed.
  5. Stop after 1–2 minutes.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l-0H6D3YY1E&t=30s

## Key Principles
- Role modeling is essential — Carsten stresses that the facilitator must demonstrate the exercise themselves, or it will fall flat.
- Keep energizers brief; the goal is to raise energy and pulse, not exhaust participants.
- Self-directed effort respects different fitness levels — people choose how hard they push.
- A light competitive frame ("friendly competition") makes physical activities more engaging without adding pressure.

## Facilitation Tips
- Always demonstrate the plank position yourself before asking participants to do it — do not just describe it verbally.
- Know your audience: this activity requires physical comfort with floor-based exercise and works only in in-person settings.
- Frame it as fun and optional in intensity so participants at different fitness levels feel included.
- Cut it off while energy is still high — stopping at 1–2 minutes leaves people energized rather than worn out.

## Best Used When
- Participants have been sitting for a while and need a physical reset.
- You want to inject energy and laughter into an in-person workshop or training.
- You need a quick, no-materials energizer that works for any group size.
