# The Bungie Product Owner with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the "Bungie P.O." anti-pattern — a Product Owner who appears only at Sprint Planning and Sprint Review, then disappears for the rest of the sprint. He explains why this happens, what damage it causes to the team and product, and what a genuinely available Product Owner looks like.

## Methods & Techniques

### Recognizing the Bungie P.O. Anti-Pattern (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A Product Owner who is only present at ceremony bookends (Planning, Review, occasionally Retro) but absent during the sprint itself, leaving questions unanswered and the vision unguarded.
- **When to use:** Use this pattern as a diagnostic lens during retrospectives or coaching conversations when teams express confusion about priorities or direction.
- **Group size:** Relevant for any Scrum team
- **Duration:** N/A — this is a diagnostic concept, not an exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Observe whether the P.O. is consistently absent mid-sprint
  2. Check retrospective signals: are team members asking "Where is the P.O.?" or "What are the priorities?"
  3. Identify the root cause (stakeholder chasing, too many teams, wrong role fit)
  4. Address by reducing the P.O.'s team/task load or coaching on availability expectations
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fx6CGBNHfnM&t=30

## Key Principles
- The Product Owner must be available during the sprint to answer questions — not just at ceremonies. Availability enables genuine innovation and ensures the team builds the *right* thing.
- The Development Team is one of the most important assets a P.O. has. Neglecting that relationship undermines product value.
- When the Scrum Master fills the P.O. void, it comes at a cost: the SM can no longer focus on team health and impediment removal.
- The more the P.O. evangelizes the Product Vision directly to the team, the more energy and buy-in the team develops.
- If a P.O. is stretched across too many teams or tasks, the solution is to narrow scope — not to accept chronic unavailability.
- Carsten references Roman Pichler's *Agile Product Management with Scrum* as the source of the "Bungie P.O." label.

## Facilitation Tips
- **Retrospective signal:** If team members are raising "Where is the P.O.?" or "What's the vision?" as topics, name the Bungie P.O. pattern explicitly — it gives the dysfunction a shared vocabulary.
- **Coaching the P.O.:** Help them see the Development Team as a primary stakeholder, not a secondary one behind external stakeholders.
- **Coaching the organization:** If the P.O. genuinely cannot be available, the structural fix is fewer teams or reduced responsibilities — not compensating workarounds.

## Best Used When
- Coaching a Product Owner who is frequently pulled away from their team
- Running a retrospective where team frustration points to unclear priorities or missing direction
- Diagnosing why a Scrum team keeps building the wrong thing despite good intentions
- Onboarding new Product Owners to set clear availability expectations upfront
