# 6 Year YouTube Anniversary

## Summary
Carsten Lützen marks six years of posting weekly YouTube videos on facilitation, agile coaching, and graphical facilitation. He shares channel statistics transparently, reflects on his motivation for continuous learning, and briefly outlines recent professional milestones including an ICF certification renewal and pursuit of PCC status.

## Methods & Techniques

### Weekly Video as a Learning Discipline (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Carsten uses weekly video creation as a deliberate practice tool — committing to publish one video every week without exception, each covering a tool or technique in facilitation, coaching, or graphical facilitation.
- **When to use:** When you want to build consistent professional habits, deepen your own understanding, or develop presentation and communication skills.
- **Group size:** Individual practice
- **Duration:** Sustained over years (6+ years in this case)
- **How it works:** Commit to a fixed cadence. Each episode forces you to research, digest, and explain a concept in your own words — reinforcing learning and building a public body of work over time.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jhvw-WoJLR0

## Key Principles
- Carsten's primary motivation for creating content is **self-development**, not audience growth — using the channel to pace his own learning and sharpen presentation skills.
- **Explaining in your own words** is a core learning method: if you can teach it, you understand it.
- Transparency about metrics (views, subscribers, followers) without vanity — sharing numbers to be honest, not to impress.
- Steady, consistent output over time compounds into a meaningful body of work even without viral growth.
