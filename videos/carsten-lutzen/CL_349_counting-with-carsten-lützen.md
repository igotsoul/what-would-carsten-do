# "Counting" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a simple physical energizer called "Counting" — ideal for post-lunch energy dips in workshops. Participants count to five in multiple languages while moving different body parts, escalating to full jumping. The exercise is deliberately silly, gets hearts beating, and works especially well with diverse, multilingual groups.

## Methods & Techniques

### Counting Energizer (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A physical movement exercise where participants count to five using different body parts, cycling through multiple languages chosen by the group.
- **When to use:** After lunch or whenever energy in the room drops and people need to re-engage physically.
- **Group size:** Any size; works well with diverse/multilingual groups.
- **Duration:** ~5 minutes (adjustable based on number of languages used).
- **How it works:**
  1. Start with English. Count to five while moving one body part at a time in sequence: right arm, left arm, right leg, left leg, shoulders, hips.
  2. Ask the crowd which other languages to use next (e.g., Danish, Spanish, Finnish). Repeat the full body-part sequence in each language.
  3. After completing all languages with individual body parts, do one final round *without pauses* — everyone jumps and moves freely through the count.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0e9ZWu4nX-o&t=30

## Key Principles
- Embracing looking silly lowers social barriers and creates shared fun — Carsten frames this explicitly as part of the exercise's value.
- Inviting the crowd to suggest languages gives agency and leverages the diversity of the group as a resource.
- Physical movement with increasing intensity (structured → free) progressively raises energy rather than spiking it all at once.

## Facilitation Tips
- Be brave and model the exercise yourself — Carsten demonstrates the full sequence on camera despite "looking funny."
- Let go of perfect pronunciation when using languages you don't speak; it adds to the humor and psychological safety.
- Collect language suggestions from participants before starting to build buy-in and spot multilingual speakers who can help lead their round.

## Best Used When
- Post-lunch energy dip in a full-day workshop.
- Working with diverse or international groups where multiple languages are present.
- You need a quick re-energizer with zero materials and minimal setup.

## Related Concepts
- General energizer/icebreaker design: short, physical, inclusive, low-barrier
- Psychological safety through shared vulnerability (looking silly together)
