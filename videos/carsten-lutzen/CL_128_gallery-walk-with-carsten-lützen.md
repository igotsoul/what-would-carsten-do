# "Gallery Walk" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten walks through how he facilitates a Gallery Walk — a technique for group sensemaking where participants silently inspect materials placed around the room before debriefing together. While the concept is common, Carsten highlights what separates a good Gallery Walk from a great one, focusing on the value of silence, unhurried pacing, and strong debrief questions.

## Methods & Techniques

### Gallery Walk (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Participants silently walk around a room inspecting materials (user stories, data points, charts, etc.) placed at different stations, taking personal notes before a structured debrief.
- **When to use:** When a group needs to collectively make sense of multiple items — e.g., backlog refinement, reviewing interview data, inspecting test results, or any situation where you want diverse perspectives on distributed content.
- **Group size:** Larger groups implied; works well when individual reading would be inefficient or when collective sensemaking is the goal.
- **Duration:** 5–10 minutes for the silent walk phase (depending on the number of stations); additional time for debrief.
- **How it works:**
  1. Place materials around the room — on walls or on the floor (Carsten notes the floor is a valid option).
  2. Invite (don't instruct) participants to walk around as if in a museum — in silence.
  3. Encourage note-taking: interesting observations, contradictions, or connections between items.
  4. Give people plenty of unhurried time. If people start talking, gently ask them to stay quiet and wait a few more minutes.
  5. Debrief using 1-2-4-All: 1 minute individual reflection in silence → 2 minutes in pairs → 4 minutes in small groups → full group sharing.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8ODEZn6TtV4&t=30

### 1-2-4-All Debrief (ab 02:20)
- **What:** A structured conversation format that progressively bubbles up insights from individual to pair to small group to full room.
- **When to use:** After any silent reflection or gallery walk phase, to surface individual observations without groupthink dominating early.
- **How it works:** 1 minute alone → 2 minutes in pairs → 4 minutes in groups of four → open plenary sharing.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8ODEZn6TtV4&t=140

## Key Principles
- The magic of a Gallery Walk happens in the silent inspection phase — don't rush it.
- Silence is not just a preference; it's essential. Talking too early collapses individual sensemaking into group consensus before insights have formed.
- The floor is as valid a display surface as the walls — don't over-constrain the setup.
- "Invite" participants rather than "instruct" them — framing matters for engagement.
- Strong debrief questions are open and forward-looking, not evaluative.

## Facilitation Tips
- If participants start talking during the walk, gently ask them to remain quiet and wait a few more minutes — don't let the silence collapse prematurely.
- Budget generously for the silent walk: 5–10 minutes depending on the number of stations.
- Carsten's favourite debrief questions:
  - "What was the biggest surprise to you?"
  - "What are you going to take away from this and act on?"
  - "What would you like to be able to act on, but can't right now?"
- Avoid yes/no or evaluative questions in the debrief — open, curious questions generate richer outputs.

## Best Used When
- A group needs to inspect multiple pieces of content simultaneously (e.g., sprint data, user stories, interview findings).
- You want to avoid one person or one item dominating the conversation before everyone has formed their own view.
- You need to create shared understanding across a team without a presenter walking everyone through each item.
- Refinement sessions, retrospectives, research readouts, or any workshop where distributed materials need collective sensemaking.
