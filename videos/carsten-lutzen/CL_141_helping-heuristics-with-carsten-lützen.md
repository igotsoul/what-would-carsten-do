# "Helping Heuristics" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces Helping Heuristics, a Liberating Structure designed to help groups explore different ways of asking for and giving help. Participants rotate through four distinct coaching styles within a triad setup, experiencing how each approach affects the relationship between coach and client. It is equally useful for introducing Liberating Structures concepts and for running workshops on feedback, help-seeking, and coaching dynamics.

## Methods & Techniques

### Helping Heuristics — Full Structure (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A Liberating Structure in which trios cycle through four two-minute rounds of different helping styles, with an observer tracking how each style affects the coach-client relationship.
- **When to use:** When you want to teach different coaching/helping approaches experientially, or when running a workshop focused on how to ask for help and how to give it effectively.
- **Group size:** Multiples of 3 (triads: one coach, one client, one observer). Larger groups simply form multiple triads running in parallel.
- **Duration:** ~15 minutes per full round (4 × 2-minute rounds + triad debrief). Rotate roles for up to three rounds if time allows.
- **How it works:**
  1. **Setup:** Divide into groups of three. Assign roles — coach, client, observer. If time permits, everyone will play every role.
  2. **Client shares a challenge** — something real and current they want to think through.
  3. **Round 1 — Quiet Presence (2 min):** The coach listens silently, giving full attention. No responses, no phone, no notes. The observer watches how this impacts the relationship. (Same principle as "seen, heard, respected.")
  4. **Round 2 — Guided Discovery (2 min):** The coach asks open, non-judgmental questions (e.g., "What happened then?"). The observer notes whether the interaction shifts. (Same principle as appreciative interviews.)
  5. **Round 3 — Loving Provocation (2 min):** The coach offers slightly provocative ideas or pushes a little. The observer notes any change in relationship dynamic. (Same principle as Troika Consulting.)
  6. **Round 4 — Process Mindfulness (2 min):** The coach co-creates with the client — following wherever the client leads and exploring where that takes them.
  7. **Debrief in triads:** What did each role observe? How did the client experience each round? How did the coach experience it?
  8. **Optional plenary debrief:** Share general observations only — not the specific challenge discussed.

## Key Principles
- Each of the four rounds maps to a principle used in other Liberating Structures (quiet presence → "seen, heard, respected"; guided discovery → appreciative interviews; loving provocation → Troika Consulting), making this a useful meta-exercise for introducing the broader LS toolkit.
- The observer role is central: tracking how different helping styles change the quality of the relationship, not just the content of the conversation.
- Not all helping styles work equally well in all situations — the structure is designed to surface that learning experientially rather than through instruction.
- If time is limited, you can run with only one coach and one client (no rotation), though this reduces the depth of the learning about how a single challenge evolves across styles.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use a timer or raised hand signal to manage the two-minute transitions cleanly — strict timing is what creates the distinct contrast between rounds.
- In the plenary debrief, explicitly instruct participants to share only general observations, not the personal challenge — this protects psychological safety.
- Carsten recommends reading the official article by The Liberators and the description on liberatingstructures.com for deeper background before facilitating.
- If the group is squeezed for time, skip role rotation rather than shortening the rounds — the rhythm of four full rounds matters more than everyone experiencing every role.

## Best Used When
- Running workshops or team sessions on coaching, peer feedback, or help-seeking culture.
- Introducing Liberating Structures to a group and wanting them to experience multiple LS principles in a single exercise.
- Working with Scrum Masters, agile coaches, or team leads who want to expand their repertoire of helping styles.
- Teams that struggle with how to ask for or receive help effectively.

## Related Concepts
- **Seen, Heard, Respected (SHR)** — the quiet presence round draws directly on this LS.
- **Appreciative Interviews** — the guided discovery round uses the same open, non-judgmental questioning approach.
- **Troika Consulting** — the loving provocation round mirrors the provocative consulting element of Troika.
- **Liberating Structures** — the full toolkit of which Helping Heuristics is one structure; see liberatingstructures.com.
