# Crowdsourcing with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Liberating Structure "25/10 Crowdsourcing," a fast, anonymous ideation and prioritization exercise. Participants write bold ideas on index cards, circulate them through the crowd for anonymous rating, then tally scores to surface the group's top priorities. It's a quick, energizing way to democratically identify the most compelling ideas in any group.

## Methods & Techniques

### 25/10 Crowdsourcing (ab 00:30)
- **What:** An anonymous ideation and dot-voting exercise where participants write bold ideas, circulate cards for peer rating over multiple rounds, then rank by total score to reveal group priorities.
- **When to use:** When you want to surface and prioritize improvement ideas or bold proposals from the whole group — without hierarchy or social pressure influencing votes.
- **Group size:** Any size that allows people to move around and exchange cards freely.
- **Duration:** ~15–25 minutes (3 min circulation per round × 5 rounds + writing and sorting time).
- **How it works:**
  1. Pose the prompt: *"If you were 10 times bolder, what big idea would you recommend?"*
  2. Each participant writes one idea on an index card (give thinking time if needed).
  3. Everyone walks around. When two people meet, they exchange cards, read the idea, then move on and exchange again. Do this for 3 minutes.
  4. When a bell rings, each person rates the card they're holding: 1 (low) to 5 (high). Write the score on the back.
  5. Repeat card exchange and rating for a total of 5 rounds (more rounds are fine).
  6. After the final round, sum all scores on each card.
  7. Sort cards from highest to lowest score — the result is an anonymously crowd-sourced priority list.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_6cCfzPmQuw&t=30

## Key Principles
- Anonymity removes social pressure — people rate ideas on merit, not on who proposed them.
- The physical movement of exchanging cards adds energy and makes the process feel playful rather than procedural.
- The prompt "10 times bolder" deliberately invites stretch thinking, surfacing ideas people might otherwise self-censor.
- The method produces a prioritized list without facilitated debate — the crowd wisdom does the work.
- Can be naturally combined with follow-on structures like **15% Solutions** to immediately act on the top-rated idea.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use a bell or audible signal to mark the end of each circulation round — keeps the energy crisp.
- Give participants a moment of quiet thinking time before writing, especially with groups unfamiliar to bold ideation.
- During debrief, ask: *"What did you notice on the cards you read? Were there common themes? What can we do to capture the learnings?"*
- The exercise pairs well with Chaos Cocktail Party (another Liberating Structure) — similar movement-based format but different purpose.
- Post the official Liberating Structures description link in the chat/follow-up for participants who want the full spec.

## Best Used When
- A group needs to prioritize improvement ideas without letting loudest voices dominate.
- You want high engagement and physical energy in an ideation session.
- Time is limited but you still need democratic input from everyone in the room.
- You want to follow up immediately with action planning (e.g., 15% Solutions on the winning idea).
