# "The Janitor" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen shares his go-to metaphor for explaining the Scrum Master / agile coach role: the janitor. The video offers a concise, memorable framing of what it means to serve teams and organizations by optimizing their environment rather than seeking the spotlight. It is a short reflection on role identity and how to communicate it to others.

## Methods & Techniques

### The Janitor Metaphor (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A self-description framing the agile coach or Scrum Master as a janitor — someone who sweeps the corners, keeps the environment clean, and fixes things so others can do their best work.
- **When to use:** When explaining your role to stakeholders, new teams, or anyone who asks "what do you actually do?" — especially when you want to convey humility and a service orientation without sounding passive.
- **Group size:** N/A — this is a communication and framing tool.
- **Duration:** N/A
- **How it works:**
  1. Position yourself as someone who operates in the background, not in the limelight.
  2. Emphasize that your job is to make the surrounding environment — physical, relational, systemic — as clean and optimal as possible for teams to thrive.
  3. Acknowledge that "fixing things" can involve active facilitation (e.g., Liberating Structures, solution-focused coaching).
  4. Note that it also occasionally requires assertiveness: stepping forward, setting constraints, or redirecting — "dancing on the tables" — when the situation calls for it.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qzH83TSQUBQ&t=30

## Key Principles
- The agile coach role is fundamentally about enabling others, not being visible or central.
- A good metaphor for your role should be simple, humble, and carry a touch of humor — it makes it stick.
- The role has a dual nature: mostly background support, but occasionally assertive intervention when the system needs a nudge or constraint.
- "Cleaning the environment" applies to both physical space and the organizational/team system.

## Facilitation Tips
- When introducing yourself or your role to a new team or organization, lead with a metaphor rather than a job description — it creates an immediate shared picture.
- Carsten mentions Liberating Structures and solution-focused coaching as specific tools that fit within the "fixing things" dimension of the janitor role.

## Best Used When
- Onboarding into a new team or organization and needing to set expectations about how you will show up.
- Coaching other Scrum Masters or agile coaches on how to articulate their value without overstating their authority.
- Reflecting on your own role identity and where you sit on the spectrum from servant to assertive leader.
