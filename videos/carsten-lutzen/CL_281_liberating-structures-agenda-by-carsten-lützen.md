# "Liberating Structures Agenda" by Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces a personal web tool he built to generate printable agenda cards for physical workshops using Liberating Structures. Rather than hand-crafting kanban-style agenda cards for each session, facilitators can select structures and support elements on his website and download a ready-to-print PDF. The tool is a practical shortcut for creating visual agendas that participants can follow on the wall.

## Methods & Techniques

### Visual Kanban Agenda Wall (ab 00:10)
- **What:** A physical kanban board on the wall showing the workshop agenda as individual cards — one per exercise or activity — so participants can always see the flow of the day at a glance.
- **When to use:** Physical workshops where you want participants to feel oriented and see the structure of the day. Particularly useful for full-day or multi-exercise sessions.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** Ongoing throughout the workshop (setup before session starts)
- **How it works:** Print or handwrite individual cards for each exercise (e.g., Impromptu Networking, Troika Consulting), breaks, and structural elements (intro, closing, lunch). Arrange them on a wall in sequence. Participants can see where they are in the day at any moment.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VPMlyu4DhJk&t=10

### Liberating Structures Agenda PDF Generator (ab 00:45)
- **What:** A free web tool on Carsten's personal website that lets facilitators select Liberating Structures (and support elements) and download a print-ready PDF of agenda cards.
- **When to use:** When preparing a physical workshop using Liberating Structures and you want visual agenda cards without creating them manually.
- **Group size:** N/A (planning tool)
- **Duration:** N/A (preparation tool)
- **How it works:**
  1. Visit Carsten's website and navigate to the Liberating Structures page.
  2. Select the structures you want to run (e.g., Impromptu Networking, 1-2-4-All, Troika Consulting, Conversation Cafe).
  3. Add support structures as needed: introduction slide, To Do / Doing / Done headers, best hopes opener, closing/recap card, lunch, and up to three breaks.
  4. Click "Download PDF" — the tool generates agenda cards and sends the PDF to your computer.
  5. Print and arrange on the wall before the session.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VPMlyu4DhJk&t=45

### Best Hopes Opener (ab 02:10)
- **What:** A structured opening question Carsten routinely uses to surface what participants want from the day before diving into content.
- **When to use:** At the start of any workshop or training day to set shared intentions and create buy-in.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** Short (included as a standard support card in the agenda tool)
- **How it works:** Ask participants: "What are your best hopes for today?" Collect responses visually or verbally before moving into the first exercise.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VPMlyu4DhJk&t=130

## Key Principles
- Visual agendas on the wall help participants stay oriented throughout the day — they always know where they are and what's coming.
- Reusable, printable cards reduce the manual preparation burden for recurring workshop formats.
- Support structures (intro, headers, closing, breaks) are as important to plan explicitly as the exercises themselves — they belong on the agenda wall too.
- Closing and a session rating/feedback moment are default elements Carsten builds into every session agenda.

## Facilitation Tips
- Include a "Best Hopes" card at the start and a closing/recap card at the end as standard practice — they frame the session intentionally.
- Use To Do / Doing / Done headers on the agenda wall to make progress visible as the day unfolds.
- Plan for up to three breaks explicitly and represent them on the wall — participants appreciate seeing rest built into the structure.
- Each structure card on Carsten's tool links to both the official Liberating Structures website (for steps and riffs) and his own YouTube facilitation walkthroughs.

## Best Used When
- Preparing a physical workshop that uses multiple Liberating Structures in sequence.
- You want a professional, consistent visual agenda without investing hours in manual card design.
- Working with groups who benefit from seeing the full day's structure at a glance.
- Building a repeatable facilitation practice with standardized visual materials.
