# "Double Diamond" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the Double Diamond model as a universal workshop planning framework that structures any meeting around two diverge-converge cycles: first to define the right problem, then to find the right solution. He demonstrates how to map concrete facilitation techniques onto the model through two worked examples — a retrospective and a team goal-setting session.

## Methods & Techniques

### Double Diamond Model (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A two-phase diverge-converge structure for workshops. The first diamond expands and then narrows the problem domain; the second expands and then narrows the solution domain.
- **When to use:** Planning any workshop, retrospective, or structured meeting where you need to ensure both problem clarity and solution quality.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** Scales to the meeting length — the model is the container, not the content
- **How it works:**
  1. Start with a **problem statement**
  2. **Expand** the problem domain — surface different perspectives, understand scope
  3. **Contract** the problem domain — agree on which part of the problem to address
  4. **Expand** the solution domain — generate many possible solutions
  5. **Contract** the solution domain — converge on agreed solution(s)
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cMAjCr9jS8&t=30

### Retrospective Mapping to Double Diamond (ab 02:30)
- **What:** A concrete exercise sequence that maps standard retro formats onto the Double Diamond structure.
- **When to use:** Designing a retrospective with a clear diverge-converge arc.
- **How it works:**
  1. **Set the Stage / Icebreaker** — entry point
  2. **Starfish with appreciative column** (problem expand) — brainstorm issues; problem statement: "How can we make this team even more awesome?"
  3. **Dot Voting** (problem contract) — select the highest-priority problem(s) to address
  4. **15% Solutions** from Liberating Structures (solution expand) — identify changes the team can make without external help
  5. **Dot Voting** again (solution contract) — pick the solutions the team has most faith in
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cMAjCr9jS8&t=150

### Team Goal-Setting Session Mapping (ab 03:50)
- **What:** An exercise sequence for co-creating a team goal using the Double Diamond structure.
- **When to use:** When a team needs to define or refresh their shared purpose or aspiration.
- **How it works:**
  1. **Problem statement:** "What do we aspire to become as a team?"
  2. **Conversation Café** (problem expand) — structured discussion ensuring everyone is heard
  3. **1-2-4-All** debrief (problem contract) — individual reflection surfacing into shared candidate goals
  4. **Crowdsourcing** (Liberating Structure, covers solution expand + contract) — brainstorm and vote simultaneously to identify the winning team goal
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9cMAjCr9jS8&t=230

## Key Principles
- The Double Diamond is a **meta-framework** — it tells you what kind of activity you need at each stage, not which specific exercise to use.
- **Diverge before you converge:** Skipping the expansion phase leads to solving the wrong problem or picking weak solutions.
- **One exercise can cover multiple phases** — Crowdsourcing, for example, both expands and contracts the solution domain in a single activity. You don't always need four separate exercises.
- The model connects directly to the **Derby-Larsen retrospective model** — Carsten notes they map cleanly onto each other.
- Reusing familiar techniques (e.g., Dot Voting in both diamonds) reduces cognitive load and keeps sessions fluid.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use the Double Diamond as a **design checklist** when planning workshops: have you covered problem expansion, problem selection, solution generation, and solution selection?
- Don't force four distinct exercises — let the model guide the **type** of activity needed, then choose exercises that fit your group and context.
- Carsten recommends keeping the problem statement visible throughout, so the whole group stays anchored to the same starting point.
- Dot Voting is a low-friction convergence tool that works well in both diamonds because participants already understand it by the second use.

## Best Used When
- Designing a workshop or retrospective from scratch and needing a structural scaffold
- Diagnosing whether a previous session failed due to skipping divergence or convergence
- Explaining workshop logic to stakeholders or team members unfamiliar with facilitation design
- Mapping Liberating Structures or other exercises into a coherent arc
