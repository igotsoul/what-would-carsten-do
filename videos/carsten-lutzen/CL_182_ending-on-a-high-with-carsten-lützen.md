# "Ending on a High" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a collection of short, playful closing activities designed to send workshop participants out the door in a positive, energized state. The video covers five quick techniques ranging from physical activities to a simple reflective debrief. Carsten emphasizes reading the room before choosing which activity fits the context.

## Methods & Techniques

### Massage Circle (ab 00:48)
- **What:** Participants stand in a circle and give the person in front of them a two-minute shoulder massage.
- **When to use:** To close a workshop with a warm, physical, community-building moment. Requires psychological safety in the group.
- **Group size:** Any size that can form a circle
- **Duration:** ~2 minutes
- **How it works:** Ask everyone to stand in a circle facing the back of the person in front of them, then massage their shoulders for about two minutes.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4-Ywg0UJEss&t=48s

### High Five Circle (ab 01:18)
- **What:** Participants stand in a circle and attempt a synchronized group high five.
- **When to use:** Quick, fun closer — works especially well because it's surprisingly tricky to coordinate, adding a moment of shared laughter.
- **Group size:** Any circle-sized group
- **Duration:** 1–3 minutes
- **How it works:** Everyone stands in a circle and high-fives simultaneously. It often takes a few tries to get right, which is part of the fun.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4-Ywg0UJEss&t=78s

### Rocket (ab 01:35)
- **What:** A full-body patting energizer inspired by a childhood birthday tradition.
- **When to use:** To re-energize the body after a long sitting session and close with a physical, playful activity.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** 1–2 minutes
- **How it works:** Participants bend down, then pat their legs all the way up their body — legs, torso, arms, and head — as they rise. Simulates a "launching rocket" movement.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4-Ywg0UJEss&t=95s

### Stretching (ab 02:05)
- **What:** Simple group stretches as a closing movement break.
- **When to use:** Classic option when the group has been seated for a long time and needs a gentle physical reset before leaving.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** 2–5 minutes
- **How it works:** Lead or invite participants through a few simple stretches together.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4-Ywg0UJEss&t=125s

### One Word Debrief (ab 02:20)
- **What:** Each participant shares exactly one word summarizing their experience of the day or workshop.
- **When to use:** Lightweight closing reflection that also gives the facilitator a quick read on what landed. Works well when time is short or a full debrief isn't needed.
- **Group size:** Any — pass a ball or token to keep turns clear
- **Duration:** 2–5 minutes depending on group size
- **How it works:** Pass a ball around the circle. Each person says one word — about the day, the session, or whatever prompt you set — then passes. No elaboration allowed.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4-Ywg0UJEss&t=140s

## Key Principles
- Context matters before choosing a closer: consider what topics were covered in the workshop and whether the energy is right for physical contact or playfulness.
- Not every closing activity needs to be pre-planned — Carsten often picks one in the moment based on the energy in the room.
- The goal is lighthearted: send people out the door feeling good, not loaded with more reflection.
- Physical activities help participants reconnect with their bodies after sitting for long periods.

## Facilitation Tips
- Check the room's psychological safety before using touch-based activities like the massage circle.
- The One Word Debrief is deceptively informative — it gives the facilitator genuine insight into what participants took away, even though it feels playful.
- The High Five Circle's difficulty is a feature, not a bug — the attempts create shared laughter and a sense of togetherness.
- Having two or three of these in your back pocket means you can pick the right one in the moment rather than forcing a pre-planned choice.

## Best Used When
- Closing any workshop or meeting where you want to leave participants energized rather than drained.
- The group has been sitting or in deep discussion for an extended period and needs a physical or emotional reset.
- You want a closing ritual that builds group cohesion rather than just summarizing content.
- Time is short and you need something quick but meaningful.
