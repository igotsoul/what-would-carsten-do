# "Positive Energizer" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares a simple energizer he learned from a colleague named Mid, used to open meetings or workshops on a positive note. Participants individually reflect on two strengths-based questions before sharing with the group, creating bonds and building momentum through appreciation and pride.

## Methods & Techniques

### Positive Energizer (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A two-question reflection exercise that primes participants with positivity and builds connection by surfacing pride and peer appreciation.
- **When to use:** At the start of a workshop, team meeting, or network event — any time you want to set a positive, connected tone before diving into content.
- **Group size:** Any size; works for teams, workshops, and network meetings alike.
- **Duration:** ~5–10 minutes depending on group size and sharing format.
- **How it works:**
  1. In silence and individually, participants write answers to two questions:
     - *"What are you most proud of right now?"*
     - *"What impresses you most about someone on this team?"*
  2. Participants share their answers with the group — use volunteers, round robin, or your preferred approach.
  3. Allow people to choose whether to share something personal or professional; do not force private disclosure.
  4. When phrasing the second question, keep it scoped to people *in this meeting* to intentionally build bonds within the current group.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZPsMxA27mOw&t=20

## Key Principles
- Priming people with positive reflection before a session shifts the energy and mindset of the whole group.
- Scoping appreciation to *"someone on this team / in this meeting"* intentionally strengthens bonds within the present group rather than diffusing attention elsewhere.
- Giving people choice over what to share (personal vs. professional) lowers the barrier to participation and respects psychological safety.
- Silence during individual reflection is important — it gives people time to genuinely think before sharing.

## Facilitation Tips
- Let participants self-select what type of content they share; some will go personal, some professional — both are valid.
- Choose your sharing format (volunteer, round robin, etc.) based on how well you know the group.
- Works equally well in-person and virtually — no adaptation needed.
- Credit the source of the exercise to the person you learned it from; it models a culture of attribution and appreciation.

## Best Used When
- Opening a team meeting, retrospective, or workshop and wanting to shift energy toward the positive before the main work begins.
- Working with a group that needs stronger interpersonal bonds or tends to start meetings cold.
- Running a virtual session where connection is harder to establish naturally.
