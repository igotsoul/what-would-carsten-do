# "My Board" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen, an agile coach and Scrum Master, shares his personal kanban-style board system for managing daily work. The board segments tasks across multiple planning horizons — from "never" to "done" — to reduce cognitive load, maintain focus, and create a visible sense of progress.

## Methods & Techniques

### Personal Kanban with Time-Horizon Columns (ab 00:45)
- **What:** A virtual kanban board segmented not just by status, but by planning horizon — mirroring the multi-horizon planning approach used with agile teams.
- **When to use:** Daily personal task and work tracking, especially for coaches or practitioners juggling many parallel commitments across different timelines.
- **Group size:** Individual (personal tool)
- **Duration:** Ongoing habit; Carsten estimates it takes 1–2 months to become second nature.
- **How it works:**
  1. **Later** — Items you intend to do but have no defined timeline for yet.
  2. **Now** — Items planned for the current week; functions as a weekly plan.
  3. **To-do** — Items actively being worked on right now.
  4. **Done** — Completed work; provides a visible record of progress.
  5. **Never** — Items that will not happen, but are kept rather than deleted. This preserves the history of ideas that didn't make the cut and leaves the door open for revisiting them later.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_dEowbFFryw&t=45

## Key Principles
- Always keep slack in your schedule for unforeseen, urgent requests — especially important for coaches whose job is to unblock teams.
- Writing things down frees cognitive capacity; you no longer need to hold open loops in your head.
- The "Never" column is intentional: deleting items loses track of ideas that didn't make the cut. Keeping them visible acknowledges reality without marking them as done.
- Moving items across columns creates a physical sense of progress that supports mental wellbeing.
- Tooling should be frictionless and accessible anywhere — Carsten uses a simple virtual whiteboard, not specialized software.
- Habits take time: even experienced practitioners should expect 1–2 months before a new tracking habit becomes automatic.

## Facilitation Tips
- This board pattern can be introduced to individuals or teams as a lightweight personal planning tool — it requires no special software and can be set up on any virtual whiteboard.
- The time-horizon framing (Later / Now / To-do / Done / Never) is more nuanced than a standard kanban and can prompt useful conversations about prioritization and what "active" really means.
- The "Never" column is a conversation starter: it normalizes the idea that not everything gets done, and that's okay to name explicitly rather than silently drop.

## Best Used When
- Coaching practitioners who struggle with task overload or context-switching.
- Introducing personal agile practices to individuals new to visual work management.
- Facilitating reflection on focus, priority, and sustainable pace at the individual level.
- As a self-management example to share in agile training or onboarding contexts.
