# "Santa's Nice List" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces "Santa's Nice List," a Christmas-themed team exercise designed as either an opening activity or a year-end closing ritual. Using the 1-2-4-All structure, it guides teams through three positively-framed prompts: celebrating achievements, identifying what to carry into the new year, and appreciating people who made a difference. The exercise is grounded in solution-focused coaching — the idea that solutions already exist in past successes.

## Methods & Techniques

### Santa's Nice List (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A structured team exercise using 1-2-4-All to reflect on achievements, forward-looking intentions, and appreciations — framed around making "Santa's nice list."
- **When to use:** Year-end team gatherings, pre-holiday retrospectives, or as an opening/energiser in a seasonal context. Works as a standalone 40–45 minute session.
- **Group size:** Any team size; structured into pairs and groups of four before full group debrief.
- **Duration:** ~40–45 minutes total (15 min per first two prompts, 5–6 min for appreciations).
- **How it works:**
  1. **Prompt 1 — Achievements (ab 01:10):** "What did we do as a team to make it to the nice list?" Covers both internal team support and contribution to the wider organisation. Run as 1-2-4-All (1 min individual → 2 min pairs → 4 min groups of four → plenary). ~15 minutes total.
  2. **Prompt 2 — Carry Forward (ab 02:00):** "Which of these things can we move into the new year?" Builds on Prompt 1 using a solution-focused lens — good things from the past become the foundation for next year. Run as 1-2-4-All. ~15 minutes total.
  3. **Prompt 3 — Appreciations (ab 02:40):** "Who are the people who made a positive difference?" Name them and say thank you. Can surface allies and positive influencers across the wider organisation. ~5–6 minutes (names tend to surface quickly from prior rounds).

## Key Principles
- **Solution-focused framing:** Carsten explicitly roots this exercise in solution-focused coaching — solutions are already present in past successes. Prompt 2 deliberately harvests that.
- **End on an appreciative note:** The sequence is intentional — achievements first, then forward momentum, then human appreciation. Energy rises toward the close.
- **Internal and external lens:** Each prompt can be framed both inward (how did we support each other?) and outward (how did we serve other teams or the organisation?). Facilitators should make this explicit.
- **Tailor the framing:** Carsten advises adapting the "nice list" metaphor to fit the team's actual context and environment — the Christmas wrapper is optional, the structure is not.

## Facilitation Tips
- Reframe and tailor Prompt 1 to match the team's language and context — don't rely on the Christmas metaphor carrying the weight.
- In Prompt 3, the names often emerge naturally from the previous two rounds, which is why it needs less time.
- Use the outputs to close the session on a celebratory note — Carsten suggests treating the results as material for celebration, not just reflection.
- Pair the session with a small celebration ritual to honour the achievements and people named.

## Best Used When
- Closing out a year, quarter, or major project phase
- Teams need a positively-framed retrospective rather than a problem-focused one
- You want to build team energy and morale heading into a break or new cycle
- The team would benefit from explicitly naming and thanking contributors across the wider organisation
