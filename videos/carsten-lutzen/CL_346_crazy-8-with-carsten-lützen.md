# "Crazy 8" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces Crazy Eights, a rapid ideation exercise from Jake Knapp's Google Design Sprint. In just 8 minutes, participants fill 8 squares on a folded piece of paper with ideas — one per minute — to generate a high volume of ideas quickly and shift people into a creative mindset.

## Methods & Techniques

### Crazy Eights (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A timed ideation format where participants sketch or write one idea per square across 8 squares in 8 minutes, prioritizing quantity over quality.
- **When to use:** When a workshop needs to open up divergent thinking — especially in the "diamond opening" phase of a double diamond process — and generate many ideas quickly.
- **Group size:** Any; each participant works individually on their own sheet.
- **Duration:** 8 minutes for the exercise, ~10 minutes total including a brief debrief.
- **How it works:**
  1. Each participant takes an A4 (or similar) sheet of paper.
  2. Fold in half three times to create 8 equal squares, then unfold.
  3. Pose a strong prompt — Carsten recommends a "How Might We..." question (e.g., "How might we alleviate this problem?").
  4. Start a timer. Participants have 1 minute per square to write, draw, or sketch an idea.
  5. Call time after each minute ("Pling!") until all 8 squares are filled.
  6. Optional debrief: participants share anything they're proud of or found interesting.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I0tx_qnrYEI&t=20s

## Key Principles
- The goal is quantity, not quality — lower the bar to open up creative thinking.
- A well-framed "How Might We..." question is critical to giving participants a productive direction.
- Time pressure is intentional: it prevents overthinking and encourages instinctive ideation.
- The exercise shifts participants from passive workshop attendees into active, creative contributors.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use a clear, energizing signal when each minute ends (e.g., "Pling! Move to the next square.") to maintain momentum.
- For remote workshops, participants can do the paper folding offline at their desk, or use an online whiteboard with 8 pre-drawn boxes.
- Keep the debrief light and quick — ask only for highlights people are excited about, not a full review of every idea.

## Best Used When
- Early in a workshop when you need to diverge and generate many ideas before converging on solutions.
- The group is stuck or anchoring on too few ideas.
- You want to quickly energize participants and get them into a creative, generative mode.

## Related Concepts
- **Google Design Sprint** (Jake Knapp) — the source framework for Crazy Eights.
- **Double Diamond model** — Crazy Eights fits the first diamond's divergent opening phase.
- **"How Might We" framing** — a complementary prompt technique often paired with ideation exercises.
