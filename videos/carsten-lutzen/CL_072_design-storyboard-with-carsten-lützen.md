# "Design Storyboard" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten Lützen introduces the Liberating Structure "Design Storyboard," a structured approach for designing workshops and meetings. The method guides a design team through clarifying purpose, reviewing standard approaches, and brainstorming agenda items before filling out a storyboard table. The result is a clear, purposeful workshop agenda with defined goals, chosen methods, and identified alternatives.

## Methods & Techniques

### Design Storyboard (ab 00:30)
- **What:** A Liberating Structure used to collaboratively design the agenda for a workshop or meeting. It produces a structured table with agenda items, goals, methods, steps, and owners — along with alternative strings.
- **When to use:** When preparing a workshop or meeting, especially when a design team needs to align on purpose and structure before the event.
- **Group size:** A small design team or design committee.
- **Duration:** 25–70 minutes depending on complexity.
- **How it works:**
  1. **Clarify purpose** — Why does this meeting or workshop exist? Ensure everyone on the design team agrees on the "why."
  2. **Review standard approach** — What normally works? What doesn't? What falls between the cracks?
  3. **Identify participants** — Who needs to be in the room, and who should be part of the design team?
  4. **Brainstorm agenda items** — Given the purpose and standard approach, what agenda items are needed? What goal must be true after each item?
  5. **Select methods** — For each agenda item, choose a structure or exercise (Liberating Structures or otherwise). Document the steps and who facilitates.
  6. **Fill in the storyboard table** — Columns include: agenda item, goal, method/microstructure, steps, and facilitator.
  7. **Define alternative strings** — Identify fallback or alternative sequences (e.g., if "What? So What? Now What?" doesn't land, a Conversation Café might serve as an alternative).

## Key Principles
- Always start from purpose and work toward method — never reverse-engineer a goal to justify a method you want to use.
- Having a clear purpose agreed upon by the design team is the foundation before any agenda can be built.
- Building alternative strings into the storyboard keeps the facilitation nimble and choices transparent.
- Design Storyboard works with any method or exercise — not just Liberating Structures.

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten warns against starting with a preferred technique and then working backward to justify it — this undermines workshop integrity.
- Documenting alternative agenda strings in the storyboard allows facilitators to pivot gracefully without losing the plot.
- The storyboard table should capture not just what you'll do, but why you're doing it — making the logic visible to the whole design team.

## Best Used When
- Designing a workshop or meeting with a small team before the event.
- You want to ensure the design team has shared clarity on purpose before committing to an agenda.
- You need a structured way to document and communicate the workshop flow, including contingency options.
