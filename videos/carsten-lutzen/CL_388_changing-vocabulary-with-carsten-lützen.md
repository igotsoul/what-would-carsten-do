# Changing Vocabulary with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten challenges two common terms in the agile world — "Agile" and "Sprint" — arguing that more precise language leads to clearer thinking and healthier team culture. He proposes replacing "Agile" with "Incremental and Iterative" and "Sprint" with "Iteration" to better reflect what teams are actually doing and to reduce stress connotations.

## Methods & Techniques

### Replace "Agile" with "Incremental and Iterative" (ab 00:30)
- **What:** Instead of using the term "Agile" — which has become vague and diluted — use "Incremental and Iterative" to communicate precisely what you mean.
- **When to use:** When discussing ways of working with teams, stakeholders, or leadership where "Agile" triggers misunderstandings or means different things to different people.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** N/A — this is a language shift, not a timed exercise
- **How it works:**
  1. Notice when you're about to say "Agile" in a coaching or facilitation context.
  2. Ask whether "Incremental" (building on previous deliveries) or "Iterative" (learning through smaller cycles) more precisely captures what you mean.
  3. Use the more specific term instead.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X-Iq_0wGhcE&t=30

### Replace "Sprint" with "Iteration" (ab 01:40)
- **What:** Carsten suggests dropping the Scrum term "Sprint" in favour of "Iteration" to reduce stress associations and reinforce sustainable pace.
- **When to use:** When working with teams experiencing stress or burnout, or when onboarding people to Scrum for the first time.
- **Group size:** Any
- **Duration:** N/A — language shift
- **How it works:**
  1. Introduce the reframe: a sprint implies running fast and hard for a short burst — not sustainable long-term.
  2. An iteration implies a repeating, learning-oriented cycle — inherently sustainable.
  3. Adopt "Iteration" in daily language and team artefacts where the team is open to it.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X-Iq_0wGhcE&t=100

## Key Principles
- Language shapes culture: the words we choose influence how teams feel and behave, not just what they think.
- "Agile" has been diluted to the point of near-meaninglessness in many organisations — more precise alternatives restore clarity.
- **Incremental** = building on top of previously delivered work.
- **Iterative** = working in smaller cycles and learning from each one.
- Sprinting is by definition unsustainable over time; iterating is not — and sustainable pace is a core agile value.
- Small vocabulary shifts can be a low-friction coaching intervention with meaningful cultural impact.

## Facilitation Tips
- Use this reframe as a gentle provocation in team retrospectives or agile introduction sessions — ask the team what "sprint" feels like versus what "iteration" feels like.
- When stakeholders ask "what does Agile mean here?", pivot to "let me explain what we mean by incremental and iterative" — it tends to land more concretely.
- Carsten recommends introducing these alternatives gradually ("in the context where it makes sense") rather than mandating a terminology change.

## Best Used When
- Coaching teams or organisations where "Agile" has become a buzzword without shared meaning.
- Working with teams showing signs of unsustainable pace or sprint-related stress.
- Facilitating agile introduction workshops where fresh language avoids pre-existing baggage.
- As a reflective prompt in a retrospective or team coaching session about how language shapes team experience.
