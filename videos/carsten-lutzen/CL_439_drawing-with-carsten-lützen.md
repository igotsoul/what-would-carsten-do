# Drawing with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten shares practical drawing tips for facilitators who don't consider themselves naturally creative. The video covers simple techniques for headlines, stick figures, arrows, and coloring on flipcharts — with the goal of making workshop visuals more engaging than PowerPoint slides.

## Methods & Techniques

### Text-First Layout (ab 00:45)
- **What:** Always write the text before drawing the surrounding box or border, so the container fits the content rather than the other way around.
- **When to use:** Any time you're creating a flipchart headline or labeled section.
- **How it works:** Write the text first → draw the box or banner shape around it → add decorative ends to turn it into a banner shape.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJbhtRSXmNo&t=45

### Banner Headline (ab 01:00)
- **What:** A simple go-to headline style that looks polished with minimal effort.
- **How it works:** Draw a simple rectangle around the text → add a small folded triangle shape on both ends → optionally color the banner and add shadow.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJbhtRSXmNo&t=60

### Flipchart Border (ab 01:30)
- **What:** A finishing touch that frames the entire flipchart content.
- **When to use:** At the end of completing a flipchart to make it look tidy and intentional — even if the lines aren't perfectly straight.
- **How it works:** Draw a border around the full page content after filling in all other elements.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJbhtRSXmNo&t=90

### Simple Stick Figures (ab 02:00)
- **What:** Two or three easy person styles that communicate emotion and action without artistic skill.
- **How it works:**
  1. Draw a circle head
  2. Add eyes (dots for neutral, curved for looking) and a mouth (straight, curved up/down for mood)
  3. Simple body: one curved torso shape, three lines for legs/stance, two feet
  4. Optional arm with mitten-style hand
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJbhtRSXmNo&t=120

### Sketchy Arrow (ab 03:15)
- **What:** A hand-drawn arrow that looks intentional even without precision.
- **How it works:** Draw the arrow shaft first → add two angled lines for the arrowhead → close the tip into a point.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJbhtRSXmNo&t=195

### Shadow Coloring with Gray Marker (ab 04:00)
- **What:** Using a single gray marker to add depth and dimension to any element.
- **When to use:** To make headlines and arrows stand out without needing a full color set.
- **How it works:** Add a gray shadow on one side of a banner or arrow — no need for precision, a rough shadow already makes a significant visual difference.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJbhtRSXmNo&t=240

### Color Accents with Crayons (ab 04:45)
- **What:** Subtle color use to convey meaning and add warmth to flipchart visuals.
- **How it works:** Use light green or similar colors to signal tone (e.g., a "happy slide") → shade the darker part of a banner to suggest depth → use yellow/orange as a skin tone hint on figures.
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qJbhtRSXmNo&t=285

## Key Principles
- Text always comes before the container — never draw a box and try to fit text inside it.
- Imperfection is acceptable and even expected; flipcharts feel more alive than PowerPoint precisely because they're hand-drawn.
- You don't need fancy markers — a single black marker is enough to start.
- A gray shadow marker alone provides disproportionate visual impact for minimal effort.
- Sign your flipcharts with your initials so participants know who to ask follow-up questions.

## Facilitation Tips
- Carsten recommends using Google Image Search and visual dictionaries for inspiration when stuck on how to draw something.
- Prepare flipchart outlines in advance (borders, headlines) but fill in bullet points live during the session — this keeps the feel interactive without sacrificing preparation.
- After the session, take flipcharts off the easel and hang them on the wall so participants can review them during breaks and spark informal discussion.
- Flipcharts as wall displays are a low-tech, high-engagement alternative to leaving slides on a screen.

## Best Used When
- Preparing for workshops or training sessions where you want visual materials that feel personal and interactive.
- Facilitators who feel they "can't draw" and want a minimal, rules-based approach to visual facilitation.
- Any context where you want to move away from PowerPoint dependency toward more co-created, tactile workshop environments.
