# "Future Campfire" with Carsten Lützen

## Summary
Carsten introduces the "Future Campfire" (also called Futurality Campfire or Time Travel), a creative debriefing exercise for use at the end of a workshop or change initiative. Participants split into Elders and Younglings, then imagine themselves 50 years in the future reflecting on what made their change effort successful. The exercise creates a solution-focused, storytelling-driven dialogue that helps teams surface pride, insight, and concrete actions from their work.

## Methods & Techniques

### Future Campfire / Time Travel (ab 00:20)
- **What:** A retrospective debriefing exercise where participants role-play as future elders and curious younglings gathered around a campfire 50 years later, reflecting on the change that began today.
- **When to use:** At the end of a full-day workshop or at a significant milestone in a change initiative, to debrief in an engaging, future-oriented, and solution-focused way.
- **Group size:** Teams of 4–6 people, with 2–3 designated as Elders and the rest as Younglings.
- **Duration:** Approximately 10 minutes per small group, plus a plenary debrief.
- **How it works:**
  1. Split participants into groups of 4–6.
  2. Assign 2–3 people per group as **Elders** (they were present and part of the change) and the rest as **Younglings** (curious newcomers eager to hear what happened).
  3. Set the scene: everyone time-travels 50 years into the future, meeting on the exact same day and spot.
  4. Create atmosphere: dim the lights, ask one person per group to place their phone on the floor with the flashlight on, scrunch a Post-it note and place it on top to simulate a small campfire.
  5. Elders share their story: What happened 50 years ago that made this future possible? What moments are they proud of? What was the biggest change they witnessed?
  6. Younglings ask questions and engage with curiosity: "How did you do this?" "How did you see the change unfold?"
  7. After 10 minutes, turn the lights back on and return to plenary.
  8. Debrief with 2–3 groups: what stood out to the Younglings? What surprised them? What did Elders notice when reflecting on the change initiative?

## Key Principles
- The exercise is explicitly **solution-focused**: by imagining a successful future, participants naturally articulate what they want and why it matters, rather than dwelling on problems.
- The **asymmetry of roles** (Elder vs. Youngling) creates naturally different perspectives in the debrief, making the conversation richer and more revealing.
- **Physical atmosphere matters**: dimming lights and creating a mock campfire primes participants emotionally and helps them step into the imaginative frame more fully.
- Carsten credits this exercise to **Barry and Christian from The Liberators** and his colleague **Jespa**, acknowledging it exists in multiple versions — facilitators should feel free to adapt it.
- The future-framing allows participants to talk about change in a **proud, proud, narrative tone** rather than a critical or analytical one.

## Facilitation Tips
- Assign Elder roles to people who were genuinely involved and can speak to what actually happened — they become the storytellers.
- Use the campfire prop (phone + crumpled Post-it) to set the mood; it is low-effort but high-impact for shifting the group's mental state.
- Coach Younglings to ask open, curious questions rather than challenge or critique — their role is to draw out the story.
- In the plenary debrief, deliberately invite both an Elder and a Youngling voice from different groups to surface the contrast in perspective.
- According to Carsten, 10 minutes per campfire is a good timeframe; keep it contained so energy stays high.

## Best Used When
- Closing a full-day workshop or multi-day offsite where a change agenda was discussed or launched.
- Wrapping up a significant sprint, project phase, or organizational change initiative.
- When the group needs an energizing, imaginative alternative to a standard retrospective or lessons-learned session.
- When you want to reinforce positive momentum and help a team articulate their aspirations in concrete, story-based terms.

## Related Concepts
- **Liberating Structures** — the exercise draws on LS principles of small group autonomy, role diversity, and structured invitation.
- **Solution-Focused Coaching** — the future-positive framing is rooted in solution-focused practice.
- **The Liberators** (Barry Overeem & Christian Hofstadter) — credited as a source of this exercise format.
- **Time Travel Retrospective** — a broader family of techniques using temporal displacement to shift perspective.
- **Campfire storytelling format** — using narrative and roleplay to make reflections more vivid and memorable.
