# Retrospectives — Topic Overview

> Knowledge curated from YouTube channels. Each entry references the source video.

## Formats & Frameworks

### Carsten Lützen's 4-Step Retrospective
A structured 60-minute format that balances positivity with actionable improvement.
1. **Achievement Posters** (~5 min) — Silent individual reflection on sprint achievements in categories: Product, Knowledge, Social, Other.
2. **Follow-Up** (~5 min) — Review previous retro focus points. Driver gives update. Decide: close, carry on, or deprioritize.
3. **Retro Wheel** (~30 min) — Modified starfish with 6 categories: Start, Stop, Continue, More, Less, Appreciative Feedback. Uses 1-2-4-All. Dot-voting to find energy.
4. **Focus Points** (~15 min) — Top-voted items get a driver + "signs of positive improvement" (imagine it's solved — how would you know?). No premature solutions.

→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A) — Carsten Lützen

## Techniques & Building Blocks

### Achievement Posters
Visual celebration of sprint achievements in categories (Product, Knowledge, Social, Other). Builds a motivating history over time. Empty "Social" = check on team wellbeing.
→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=18) — Carsten Lützen

### Signs of Positive Improvement
Instead of jumping to solutions, imagine the problem is already solved: "How would you know? What would be different?" The driver then uses these signs to develop better solutions post-retro.
→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=214) — Carsten Lützen

### Appreciative Feedback (in Retros)
Structured slot for "non-sarcastic, non-ironic appreciative feedback to a person in this meeting — and it cannot be me [the facilitator]." This framing works especially well with engineers.
→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=125) — Carsten Lützen

### Driver Role for Action Items
The "driver" is NOT the person who does all the work. The driver ensures something happens and gives updates. This distinction is critical for sustainable follow-through.
→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=90) — Carsten Lützen

## Facilitation Tips

- **Always start with silent individual reflection** before group discussion (every step in the 4-step format uses this)
- **Track trends across retros** — items often move Start → Continue → Stop. Valuable pattern data for the facilitator.
- **If "Social" achievement category is consistently empty**, check on team wellbeing
- **Frame appreciative feedback explicitly** to close avoidance behaviors
