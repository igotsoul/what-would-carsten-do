# Facilitation — Topic Overview

> Knowledge curated from YouTube channels. Each entry references the source video.

## Techniques

### 1-2-4-All in Practice
Carsten uses 1-2-4-All (or a simplified version: individual → plenary) as his default facilitation pattern in retrospectives. Key: always start with ~1 minute of individual, silent reflection before any group discussion.
→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=125) — Carsten Lützen

### Avoiding Premature Solution Mode
Instead of brainstorming solutions in the room, use "signs of positive improvement": imagine the problem is solved and describe observable signs. Let the driver develop actual solutions afterward with more time and thought.
→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=214) — Carsten Lützen

### Introducing Appreciative Feedback to Engineers
Frame as: "non-sarcastic, non-ironic appreciative feedback to a person in this meeting — and it cannot be me." This explicit framing neutralizes common avoidance behaviors.
→ Source: [My 4 Step Retrospective](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ls-K9AuOI2A&t=125) — Carsten Lützen

## Facilitation Principles

- **Silent-first:** Every group activity benefits from starting with individual silent reflection
- **Positive framing first:** Begin sessions with what went well before diving into problems
- **Track patterns over time:** As facilitator, observe how topics evolve across sessions
- **Driver ≠ Doer:** Assign accountability for follow-through without overloading individuals
